# Goods on Country: Partner Guide

> Superseded by [wiki/articles/brand-comms/](../../wiki/articles/brand-comms/). This document is kept as a partner-facing summary. For voice rules, email templates, and pipeline mapping, see the canonical guide.

Goods on Country is a First Nations social enterprise. We design and make essential goods (Stretch Beds, Pakkimjalki Kari washing machines) with remote Indigenous Australian communities, then transfer manufacturing to community ownership. Our goal is to become unnecessary.

This guide is for organisations partnering with us. Choose the partnership shape that fits.

## What we make

| Product | Status | Use |
|---------|--------|-----|
| Stretch Bed | Available now | Buy or sponsor for communities |
| Pakkimjalki Kari (washing machine) | Prototype | Register interest |
| Basket Bed | Open source | Download plans |

Stretch Bed specs: 26kg, 200kg load, 188×92×25cm, assembles in 5 minutes with no tools, 5-year warranty, 10+ year design life. Recycled HDPE plastic legs, galvanised steel poles (DNA Steel, Alice Springs), heavy-duty Australian canvas (Centre Canvas, Alice Springs). 20kg of plastic diverted per bed.

## Where we work

400+ beds delivered across Tennant Creek (139), Palm Island (141), Alice Springs (60), Utopia Homelands (24), Maningrida (18), and Townsville as a regional logistics hub. 107 beds on order.

Every deployment happens through a community partner organisation. We never deploy without invitation.

| Tier | Contribution | Outcome |

| Founding Partner | $50,000+ | 80+ beds plus annual story feature |

- $600 per bed retail.

- 4-6 week lead time for orders under 100 units.
- Freight quoted to destination on confirmation.
- Spec sheet, sample, and video walk-through available on request.

Contact: hi@act.place with destination, volume, and timing.

### Community partnership

If you represent a community considering whether Goods would be useful, the conversation starts as a yarn. Decisions belong to community. We never lead with a pitch.

What we can offer:

- Co-design sessions with Elders, paid at fair rates ($3,800/day comparable to university research).
- Sample products to handle and test in community.
- Cultural protocols followed throughout.
- Asset tracking via QR code so community-owned beds and washing machines have a record that stays in community.
- Storytelling through Empathy Ledger with full consent. Storytellers retain ownership of their stories and can update or remove anytime.
- A pathway to local production if the fit is right. The plant is designed to move to community ownership.

### Manufacturing partnership

We are building On-Country manufacturing capability. Current partners include Defy Design (Sydney, recycling and training), Envirobank (plastic supply), and Oonchiumpa Consultancy (cultural leadership). Future production sites are planned with PICC at Palm Island and the Bloomfield family in Alice Springs.

If your organisation has manufacturing capability, plastic supply, or training capacity that could serve On-Country production, contact us at hi@act.place.

## Brand voice in partner communications

When representing or referencing Goods on Country, follow these rules:

- Goods is a social enterprise, not a charity. Never frame products as gifts.
- Centre named community voices over our own. Never paraphrase a quote into something a person did not say.
- Be specific. Real numbers, real materials, real places. Never "remote Australia" generic.
- Always capitalise On-Country, Country, Elder (when title), First Nations.
- Always credit Elder Dianne Stokes for naming Pakkimjalki Kari in Warumungu language.

Banned in Goods communications: "donate" / "donation" / "charity" framing, "beneficiaries", "empower", "unlock", "leverage", "synergy", em dashes, generic Indigenous Australia imagery as decoration.

## FAQ

**Are these donations?**
No. Sponsorship and procurement, not donations. Tax-deductible receipts are available where applicable, but the framing is enterprise: communities are co-designers and customers, not beneficiaries.

**How do you choose communities?**
We don't. Communities approach us through health services, Aboriginal corporations, or trusted introducers. We work where invited. Currently active in Tennant Creek, Palm Island, Alice Springs, Utopia, Maningrida, and others. New communities are added when relationships warrant.

**Can I specify a community?**
Yes. During checkout you can select a specific community or "where the need is greatest right now". Specific community selection is honoured wherever feasible.

**Will I receive photos?**
Yes, where the community storyteller has given consent. We never publish identifying photos without explicit permission.

**Why does the bed cost more than a budget bed at IKEA?**
Because we make it On-Country, repairable, washable, and last 10 years. A $50 bed in Sydney is $400 by the time freight hits Maningrida. We are $600 delivered, made for the conditions, designed to last.

**Who actually makes the beds?**
Currently, the production plant operates at our facility partnership in Sydney with Defy Design, with components built into the containerised plant that will move On-Country. Palm Island (Ebony and Jahvan Oui) and Alice Springs (Bloomfield family via Oonchiumpa) are confirmed future production sites. Community ownership transfer is the model, not the byline.

**What's the long-term goal?**
Manufacturing transferred to community ownership. We become unnecessary. Communities co-design, name, manufacture, and sell. Revenue stays On-Country.

**What if my community doesn't want this?**
Then we don't deploy. We've walked away from communities who weren't ready. That's the right call every time.

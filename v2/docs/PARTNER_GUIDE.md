# Goods on Country - Partner Guide

Welcome to the Goods on Country partnership program. This guide provides everything you need to know about partnering with us to deliver essential items to remote Australian Indigenous communities.

---

## About Goods on Country

Goods on Country is a social enterprise that delivers beds, washing machines, and other essential household items to remote Australian Indigenous communities. Our mission is simple: ensure every person has access to a comfortable night's sleep and basic household necessities.

### Our Impact

- **400+ beds delivered** to communities across Australia
- **15+ communities served** in remote regions
- **Local artisan employment** through handcrafted bed production
- **Cultural connection** through traditional crafting techniques

### How We Work

1. **Community Partnership** - We work directly with communities to understand their needs
2. **Local Production** - Beds are handcrafted by local artisans using traditional techniques
3. **Dignified Delivery** - Items are delivered with respect and cultural sensitivity
4. **Ongoing Support** - We maintain relationships with communities post-delivery

## Brand Guidelines

### Using Our Logo

**Do:**
- Use the official logo files provided
- Maintain clear space around the logo
- Use on appropriate backgrounds

**Don't:**
- Alter colors or proportions
- Add effects or shadows
- Use in contexts that don't align with our values

*Logo files available upon request from partners@goodsoncountry.org.au*

### Brand Voice

When representing Goods on Country:

- **Respectful**: Always speak about communities with dignity
- **Authentic**: Share real stories with real impact
- **Humble**: Focus on community outcomes, not organizational achievements
- **Culturally Sensitive**: Follow cultural protocols and get appropriate consent

### Photography Guidelines

When using Goods on Country imagery:

- Always credit the photographer/storyteller
- Obtain consent for any identifiable individuals
- Respect cultural protocols (some images may have restrictions)
- Contact us if unsure about appropriate usage

## FAQ

### About Goods

**Q: Where do the beds go?**
A: Beds are delivered to remote Indigenous communities across Australia, including regions in the Northern Territory, Queensland, and Western Australia.

**Q: Who makes the beds?**
A: Beds are handcrafted by local artisans using traditional weaving techniques. This provides employment and preserves cultural practices.

**Q: How do you choose communities?**
A: We work with community organizations, health services, and local contacts to identify communities with the greatest need.

### About Sponsorship

**Q: Is my donation tax-deductible?**
A: Please contact us for current tax-deductibility status, as this may vary by jurisdiction and donation type.

**Q: Can I specify which community receives my sponsored bed?**
A: Yes, during checkout you can select a specific community or choose "where needed most."

**Q: Will I receive photos of the delivery?**
A: Yes, with community consent, we share delivery photos and impact stories with sponsors.

### About Partnership

**Q: How long does partnership setup take?**
A: Typically 2-4 weeks from initial conversation to signed agreement.

**Q: What's the minimum partnership commitment?**
A: We're flexible and can work with partners of all sizes. Contact us to discuss options.

**Q: Can we co-brand marketing materials?**
A: Yes, subject to our brand guidelines. We'll work with you on appropriate co-branding.

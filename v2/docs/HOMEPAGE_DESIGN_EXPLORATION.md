# Goods Homepage Design Exploration

**10 Distinct Concepts for Review**

---

## Design Principles to Enact

From the Knowledge Compendium:

### Core Philosophy
1. **Respecting Country and Community** — Circular design connecting environmental care with community benefit
2. **Community-Driven Construction** — Built for local success, co-designed with communities
3. **Place-Based Adaptation** — Made for Country, adapted to specific conditions

### Brand Voice (Curious Tractor)
- Grounded yet Visionary
- Humble yet Confident
- Warm yet Challenging
- Poetic yet Clear

### What to Avoid
- Savior framing ("helping communities in need")
- Extractive language ("harvesting data")
- Corporate jargon ("leveraging synergies")
- Overclaiming ("world's first/best")

## Comparison Matrix

| Concept | Primary Focus | Tone | Best For |
|---------|--------------|------|----------|
| 1. Country First | Place/landscape | Reverent | Brand awareness |
| 2. Circular Story | Environment | Innovative | Eco-conscious buyers |
| 3. Community Voices | Testimonials | Intimate | Trust building |
| 4. Bold Impact | Data/numbers | Confident | Quick conversion |
| 5. Gentle Earth | Craft/care | Nurturing | Premium feel |
| 6. Mission Forward | Purpose | Action-driven | Social enterprise |
| 7. Woven Stories | Culture | Connected | Cultural alignment |
| 8. Clean Commerce | Product | Efficient | Direct sales |
| 9. Journey Map | Narrative | Educational | Engagement |
| 10. Sponsor Hero | Giving | Generous | Donations |

---

## Recommendation

**Hybrid approach suggested:** Combine elements from:
- **Concept 3 (Community Voices)** for authenticity
- **Concept 6 (Mission Forward)** for clarity of purpose
- **Concept 8 (Clean Commerce)** for e-commerce UX

**Primary palette recommendation:**
- Warm earthy tones (Concepts 1, 5, 7)
- Avoid dark themes for a social enterprise (Concept 4 may feel too corporate)

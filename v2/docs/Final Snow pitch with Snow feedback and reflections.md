A Curious Tractor requests **$120,000** from the Snow Foundation to scale production of on-country Goods and accelerate deployment of beds across remote Australian communities. This investment will fund **100 beds for immediate deployment** ($60,000) and **establish critical production infrastructure** ($60,000) to enable community-based manufacturing and reduce reliance on imported, disposable furniture that ends up in landfill.

This proposal responds to **200-350 bed requests** from communities and health organisations, and positions ACT to move toward financial sustainability through a social enterprise model while maintaining deep co-design partnerships with First Nations communities.

---

### Project Vision: The Big Hairy Audacious Goal

**Our ambition is bold:** Make Goods beds as ubiquitous in remote Australian communities as a troop carrier or an Akubra hat.

We envision a future where:

**1. Product ubiquity** . Visit any remote community in Australia and you'll see a Goods bed, either in use or available for purchase. It becomes the product of first choice, naturally displacing inferior alternatives.

**2. Environmental impact** . By creating a highly desirable product that people _want_, we slow the flood of cheap, disposable furniture into communities. When one Alice Springs provider sells **$3 million of washing machines annually**into communities, most ending up in dumps, we see the scale of the problem. Our solution is to introduce a "virus" of better products that people choose first, naturally reducing demand for disposable alternatives.

**Evidence of demand:**

- **Diane Stokes** received one bed and within two weeks requested 20 more, offering to fund funding herself
- **Norm Frank** called requesting three beds in maroon after his daughter tried them
- **Utopian homelands** want beds for every child (~$150,000 worth)
- **Homeland Schools Company** requesting 65 beds for kids across Maningrida communities
- **Four health organisations** have reached out following last year's forum, including Healthy Homes coordinators and OT workers

### Funding Request Breakdown

#### **Total Request: $120,000**

**1. Immediate Bed Deployment: $60,000**

- 100 beds @ $600 per bed
- Current production cost: $550-650 per bed
- Addresses urgent requests from health organisations and communities
- Expands field testing sample size from 15-20 beds to enable confident scaling decisions

**2. On-Country Production Plant Infrastructure: $60,000**

- Completes containerised production facility (current investment: ~$100,000 from TFN and ACT)
- Enables community-based manufacturing creating local jobs
- Facility capabilities:

- Shred plastic waste
- Create pellets and press sheets
- Computer-controlled router to cut bed components
- Capacity: ~30 beds per week when deployed for 2 months
- Each bed uses ~25kg of plastic waste (fits in 25L tub)

**Production model:** One container with shredder fills 200 tubs with processed plastic; production container arrives to build 200 beds from that plastic.

**Future capability:** Same facility will produce washing machines and fridges using different molds and cut files.

### Production Facility: Asset Value and Monetisation

The production facility is **not a sunk cost, it's a impact asset.**

**Evidence of commercial interest:**

- Palm Island Community Company saw video and said "we'll buy it"
- Communities might pay ~$5,000/week to host facility, creating local jobs while producing products

**Social enterprise pathway:**

- Queensland has large social impact fund with limited recipients
- Snow Foundation has offered access to social impact loans
- Model: Use philanthropy to build production plant, then loan or rent to communities creating revenue for reinvestment

**Current challenge:** Product sales generate no margin, funds come in, products go out. This investment helps shift toward sustainable model.

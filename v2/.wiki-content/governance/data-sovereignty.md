# Data Sovereignty

> Goods' data-sovereignty position should be written as a practice, not a slogan. The core commitment is that people own their stories, know how they are used, and can withdraw or change consent.

## Principle

Community stories are not marketing stock.

The working principle is that people should be able to own their stories and retract them at any time. That includes photos, videos, quotes, transcripts and the way a story is used in reports or pitch material.

## Current practice

- Share photos and videos back with community members.
- Check in with people whose stories are used, including leaders such as Dianne Stokes.
- Record interviews and conversations where appropriate.
- Use transcripts for themes, design feedback and learning.
- Keep story use tied to consent.
- Use Empathy Ledger as the technical direction for consent and withdrawal.

## OCAP and Empathy Ledger

OCAP stands for Ownership, Control, Access and Possession. It is a useful reference point for First Nations data sovereignty, but it should not be used as decoration.

Empathy Ledger supports consent, withdrawal and story governance. Goods still needs to make sure the lived process matches the technical promise.

## Reporting order

The useful question is: who are we reporting to first?

The answer should be:

1. community members and partners
2. funders and investors
3. internal dashboards for founders and ACT

Dashboards should support that order, not replace it.

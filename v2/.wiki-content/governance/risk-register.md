# Risk Register (working)

> This is a working risk picture, not a board-approved register. It needs to stay practical, especially on environmental risk, key-person risk, debt risk and community governance.

## How to read this

Use this page to guide decisions. Do not treat it as a finished compliance document.

The next version should add owners, review dates, triggers and actions.

## Current risks

| Area | Risk | Likelihood | Consequence | Current response |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Product safety | A bed, washing machine or plant process harms someone | Low to medium | High | Testing, QA, warranty, supervision and practical service loops |
| Environmental | Goods accidentally adds to the waste problem it is trying to solve | Medium | High | Design for repair, reuse and end-of-life; add environmental risk controls |
| Community trust | Poor follow-through damages a partner relationship | Medium | High | Regular communication, return visits, local partner authority |
| Story and data | A story is used in a way the person does not want | Medium | Very high | Consent practice, story return, withdrawal rights, careful approvals |
| Key people | We remain the bottleneck | High | High | Move production, operations and sales into clearer roles |
| Production | Materials, machinery or labour delay delivery | Medium | Medium to high | Supplier planning, inventory, simpler SOPs, local operator training |
| Cashflow | Costs land before grant or customer payments | High | Medium to high | Working capital planning, shorter payment cycles, debt only when matched to revenue |
| Debt | Repayable capital is taken before the model can service it | Medium | High | Patient terms, stress tests, debt tied to credible demand |
| Governance | Legal or board structure lags investor needs | Medium | High | QBE, SIH, PIN and legal support to pick minimum viable governance |
| Community ownership | Local ownership model is too Western, complex or under-resourced | Medium | High | Learn from community-controlled structures and test place by place |

## Environmental risk needs more work

Environmental risk should be visible in the risk register because Goods is trying to solve a waste problem, not move it somewhere else.

Questions to add:

- What happens if bed parts break and are not repaired?
- Who collects end-of-life products?
- What if local plastic collection does not supply enough usable material?
- What if transport emissions offset some circular-economy value?
- What if a plant creates unusable offcuts or processing waste?

## Scenario thinking

**If QBE match is missed:** slow the plant rollout, keep the product moving through smaller funded batches, and avoid taking debt to protect momentum.

**If capital lands faster than expected:** resist over-hiring centrally. Put the money into inventory, plant readiness, local roles and the finance controls needed to manage growth.

**If resident purchasing is slower than hoped:** keep institutional and philanthropic channels open while reducing price and proving desirability.

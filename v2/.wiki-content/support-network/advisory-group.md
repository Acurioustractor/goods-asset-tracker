# Goods Advisory Board

> Goods has a named advisory board and a wider support network. The advisory board is useful and real, but it is not a legal board. This page separates advisory board members from community partners, technical partners and program support.

## Role

- The advisory board helps test judgement across community, product, design, philanthropy, manufacturing, health, social enterprise and capital-readiness questions.
- It meets monthly, with ad hoc support between meetings.
- The main gap is more commercial and social-enterprise scale experience.
- The advisory board does not hold fiduciary authority for Goods.

## Known gap

Goods needs more people around the table who have taken a product organisation through sales, operations, procurement, capital and governance at scale.

The specific gap is:

- product scale and quality systems
- procurement and institutional sales
- inventory and working-capital management
- concessional debt and investment governance
- independent finance oversight
- community-controlled operating structure design
- board discipline without losing relational accountability

## What not to overclaim

- The advisory board is not a legal board.
- It does not hold fiduciary authority for Goods.
- It does not yet have a published charter.
- It does not replace a conflict register, delegation matrix, risk committee or formal finance review process.
- Some names are part of the support network rather than the advisory board itself, so external material should keep roles precise.

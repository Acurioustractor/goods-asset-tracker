## Reviewable Substance

This source is useful when it helps Goods understand buyers and alternatives. It is risky when it becomes broad market commentary without strong sources.

| Market area | Useful question |
|---|---|
| Federal procurement | Which departments, panels or programs might buy or influence essential goods? |
| Health procurement | Which ACCHOs or health programs can frame beds/washers as health hardware? |
| Housing procurement | Which housing and homelands pathways aggregate real household need? |
| Indigenous suppliers | Who could partner, compete, distribute, manufacture or advise? |
| Competitors | What alternatives do buyers already use, and why are they failing? |
| Philanthropy | Which funders are mission-aligned and patient enough? |

## Rules Before External Use

- Check every external claim.
- Remove source-poor market-size statements.
- Prefer direct Goods evidence over generic market claims.
- Do not imply competitors are doing nothing; explain the specific gap Goods addresses.
- Keep procurement strategy tied to community ownership and repairability.

# 07. The Goods Live Session Deck

One simple deck used for every live session. Funder pitch, partner intro, conference talk, internal handover, community presentation. Same spine, same images, different speaker notes per audience.

Ten slides. Five minutes if you race, twenty if you yarn.

Image references are paths in [v2/public/images/](../../v2/public/images/). Specs come from [v2/src/lib/data/products.ts](../../v2/src/lib/data/products.ts). Quotes come from [02-storyteller-voices.md](02-storyteller-voices.md).

When the full visual deck exists in Figma, Pencil, or Google Slides, link it here. Until then, this markdown is the spine.

## Slide 2: The problem

**Title:** This is preventable.

**Image:** [community-testing-bed-golden-hour.jpg](../../v2/public/images/media-pack/community-testing-bed-golden-hour.jpg)

**Body (three stats, sourced):**

- 59% of remote homes lack a working washing machine. *(FRRR, 2022)*
- 1 in 3 children in remote communities have scabies at any time. *(PLOS Neglected Tropical Diseases)*
- 55% of very remote First Nations homes are overcrowded. *(AIHW, 2021 Census)*

**Voice:**
> "Hardly anyone around the community has beds. When family comes to visit, people sleep on the floor."
> Ivy, Palm Island

**Speaker notes:**
- Don't read the stats verbatim. Land Ivy's quote first. Then say "the numbers behind that are these."
- Health funders care about the scabies stat (it leads to RHD). Procurement audiences care about the overcrowding stat. Pick which to emphasise.

## Slide 6: How it's made

**Title:** Six steps. Designed to move on-country.

**Image:** Three-up: [process/01-source.jpg](../../v2/public/images/process/01-source.jpg) + [process/04-build.jpg](../../v2/public/images/process/04-build.jpg) + [process/06-deliver.jpg](../../v2/public/images/process/06-deliver.jpg)

**Body:**

1. Source: collect plastic from communities.
2. Process: shred, melt, press into sheets.
3. Cut: CNC router shapes the bed legs.
4. Build: hydraulic press forms the legs.
5. Assemble: poles thread through canvas, legs click on.
6. Deliver: family assembles their bed in five minutes.

**Speaker notes:**
- The plant is two shipping containers. ~$100K invested already (TFN and ACT). Capacity is around 30 beds per week when deployed for two months.
- "We can move the plant to a community" is the model. Communities can host or own it.
- This is the answer to "how does this scale?": not by us scaling, by communities owning.

## Slide 9: The model

**Title:** Enterprise transferred to community ownership.

**Image:** [stretch-bed-kids-building.jpg](../../v2/public/images/product/stretch-bed-kids-building.jpg)

**Body:**

- We sell the Stretch Bed. Revenue funds the work.
- The plant is designed to move to community ownership.
- Communities co-design, name, manufacture, sell.
- We become unnecessary.

**Pathways:**

1. Sponsor beds (philanthropy)
2. Buy beds (procurement)
3. Host or own a plant (enterprise transfer)
4. Catalytic capital (blended finance, see QBE program)

**Speaker notes:**
- Different audiences need different pathway emphasis. Funder: pathway 1 and 4. Health service: pathway 2. Community: pathway 3.
- The community ownership model is what differentiates us from any other social-enterprise furniture brand. Press it.

## Common questions and short answers

**"How does this scale?"**
> Not by us scaling. By communities owning the plant. Every plant we transfer is one we don't operate.

**"Why do you cost more than IKEA?"**
> Because we make it on-country, repairable, washable, and last 10 years. IKEA is $50 in Sydney and $400 in Maningrida by the time freight hits. We are $600 delivered, made for the conditions.

**"What about the freight tax?"**
> The plant model removes it. When we make on-country, no freight. Until then, we use regional logistics hubs (Townsville for far north QLD, Alice Springs for NT) to share freight loads.

**"Are these donations?"**
> No. Sponsorship and procurement, not donations. We're an enterprise. Communities are co-designers and customers, not beneficiaries.

**"Who's done the impact measurement?"**
> Empathy Ledger captures storyteller voices with consent. ALMA framework underpins our theory of change. Quantitative deployment numbers are tracked in our asset register. See [v2/src/lib/data/impact-model.ts](../../v2/src/lib/data/impact-model.ts).

**"What if a community doesn't want this?"**
> Then we don't deploy. Decisions belong to community. We've walked away from communities who weren't ready and that was the right call.

**"What's the catch?"**
> The catch is that this only works if you don't try to scale faster than community readiness. We've built the work to honour that pace. Funders who can't honour it aren't the right partners.

## Handing off the deck

When you send the deck (PDF) to a funder pre-meeting:

```
Subject: Goods on Country / pre-read for [date] meeting

Hi [first name],

Pre-read for our chat on [date]. Twelve slides. Five minutes.

Let me know if you'd like the longer-form data behind any of the numbers before we meet.

[First name]
```

Don't pad it. Don't apologise for the brevity.

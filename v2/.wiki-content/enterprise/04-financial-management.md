# 4. Financial Management and Performance

> Goods has real funding history, real product demand and real operating costs, but it is not yet financially self-sustaining. The financial story should be honest: Goods is still batch-funded, grant-paced and ACT-supported. Before repayable capital is sensible, the team needs a Goods-specific finance pack that reconciles received funding, receivables, unit economics, cashflow timing, working capital and debt capacity.

## The Human Version

Goods is past concept stage, but it is not yet a clean product business with predictable inventory, regular sales conversion and standalone reporting.

The practical financial problem is timing:

- materials, labour and freight need money before delivery,
- funders and buyers often pay on their own cycle,
- remote delivery costs are high and lumpy,
- the team is still moving from funded batches toward repeatable sales,
- ACT currently absorbs some complexity through shared services and cashflow.

That does not mean the model is weak. It means the next financial system has to match the real operating rhythm rather than pretending Goods is already a mature ecommerce company.

## Cost Drivers

The mentor pack should show the cost stack in plain language.

- **Materials:** recycled HDPE, steel poles, heavy-duty canvas, hardware, packaging and any outsourced components.
- **Production:** labour, machine time, quality control, plant setup, rework and wastage.
- **Freight:** long-distance transport, last-mile delivery, storage and failed-delivery risk.
- **Community delivery:** time on Country, feedback collection, partner support and follow-up.
- **R&D:** Stretch Bed refinements, washing-machine prototype work, future fridge thinking and testing.
- **Support:** repairs, replacement parts, asset tracking, QR follow-up and service response.
- **Shared services:** ACT finance, admin, website, communications, technology and founder time.

## Cashflow Risk

The risk is not only "we need more money." The risk is that the right money arrives too late.

Examples to check:

- Snow timing and whether any expected payment moved from January into later 2026.
- QBE Stage 1 invoice/payment status.
- Any overdue debt or buyer invoice mentioned in founder working notes.
- Material purchase timing for the next production run.
- Freight and delivery costs that must be paid before buyer/funder cash arrives.

This should be turned into a simple cash calendar:

| Month | Cash coming in | Cash going out | Decision needed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Next 30 days | To confirm | Materials, production, delivery and operating costs | Can the next batch proceed without founder cash strain? |
| 30-90 days | To confirm | Staff, freight, product build and reporting | Is working capital required? |
| 90-180 days | To confirm | Plant, inventory and team capacity | Which capital source fits the timing? |

## What Financial Sustainability Means Here

Financial sustainability should not be framed as "no grants." That would be the wrong test for this stage.

The better test is:

- grants fund R&D, learning, community engagement and early proof,
- sales and procurement fund repeatable products,
- patient capital funds working capital and inventory only where repayment timing is credible,
- community production models become locally owned rather than centrally extractive,
- ACT shared services are visible rather than hidden.

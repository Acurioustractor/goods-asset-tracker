# Program Structure

> Catalysing Impact has two stages: capability building first, then a matched-funding process. Goods should use Stage 1 to build the evidence, governance, finance and capital discipline needed to make Stage 2 credible.

## Stage 1: Capability Building

Stage 1 includes the program support that should directly improve this working pack.

| Support | What it is | What Goods should use it for |
|---|---|---|
| Participation grant | A $10,000 program participation grant. | Cover the real time and admin burden of engaging properly. |
| Diagnostic | Tailored workshop and review of investment readiness. | Stress-test the ten topic areas in [[workshop-evidence-map]]. |
| Tailored advisory | PIN expert support on priority gaps. | Focus on finance model, governance, legal structure, impact measurement, procurement or capital pack gaps. |
| Mentoring | One-to-one mentoring with a PIN member over the program. | Keep the capital path and operating model honest. |
| Skilled volunteering | QBE professionals supporting a specific challenge. | Give them a scoped problem, not the whole organisation. |
| Hackathon | One-day QBE employee problem-solving session. | Use only once the problem is tight enough to benefit from a sprint. |
| Virtual check-ins | Cohort check-ins during the program. | Capture lessons and update the working pack. |

## Stage 2: Matched Funding

Stage 2 is the potential grant process.

| Element | Working detail |
|---|---|
| Grant pool | $1 million total across the cohort. |
| Enterprise cap | Up to $400,000 per enterprise. |
| Match requirement | External investment or funding must at least match the QBE grant ask. |
| Assessment | Steering committee discretion and due diligence. |
| Timing | Application around September 2026, assessment around October 2026, outcomes around November 2026. |
| Evidence | Funding commitments, pitch material, business plan, financial model, impact report, demographics, corporate structure, financials and governance documents. |

## Due Diligence Frame

The steering committee will not only read a story. They will test whether Goods can use capital responsibly.

The live diligence questions are:

- Is the legal vehicle clear enough?
- Are financials clean enough to underwrite?
- Are commitments real enough to count as match?
- Does the impact model distinguish live evidence from future metrics?
- Are product claims backed by specs, photos, use and feedback?
- Does governance match the risk of debt and scale?
- Does the capital stack support the community-ownership direction?

## Human Review Questions

- Which Stage 1 supports should be used for which exact gap?
- What is the smallest credible Stage 2 ask if the full stack is not ready?
- Which funding commitments can become letters of intent before August?
- Which documents need accountant, legal or board-style review before submission?
- Which parts of the working pack should become a pitch deck, and which should stay as diligence backup?

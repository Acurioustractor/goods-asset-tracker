# Human Verification Sprint

> This is the working checklist for us to turn the connected wiki from a smart draft into trusted operating material. Each source is reviewed for truth, currency, consent, shareability and usefulness before it drives QBE pages, investor material, procurement conversations or GHL follow-up.

## How To Use This

Work through one source cluster at a time:

1. Open the source page in the wiki.
2. Open the original source location named on that page.
3. Check the claim, number, quote, photo or document against the source.
4. Decide the review status.
5. Record the decision in Notion.
6. Write any changed truth back into the wiki.

Do not try to solve every source in one sitting. The first pass is about separating safe truth from useful but unverified material.

Notion mirror: [Human Verification Sprint - founder Review](https://app.notion.com/p/354ebcf981cf815b8c0edac5bd2ab0dc).

## Review Status

| Status | Meaning | What happens next |
|---|---|---|
| `approved-internal` | Accurate enough for internal planning and mentor discussion. | Can be used in QBE working pages with caveats. |
| `shareable` | Safe and accurate enough for external funder, mentor or partner use. | Can appear in artifact packs and external links. |
| `sensitive` | Useful but requires consent, privacy or relationship care. | Summarise carefully or keep internal. |
| `review-needed` | Evidence exists but has not been checked by founder. | Keep as draft and avoid strong claims. |
| `retired` | Outdated, wrong or no longer aligned. | Remove from topic pages or mark historical. |

## Output Of Each Review

Every reviewed source should leave behind:

- a status decision,
- a short note on what changed,
- any corrected numbers or dates,
- any consent or sensitivity rule,
- the pages that need updating,
- the artifact pack pieces it can support,
- any GHL or Grantscope action that is allowed or blocked.

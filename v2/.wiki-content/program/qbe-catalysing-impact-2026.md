# QBE Catalysing Impact 2026

> This is the QBE / Social Impact Hub / PIN program context for the Goods working pack. It should help us and mentors see the capital-readiness path without having to open admin dashboards, raw emails or internal memory files.

## What Goods Is In

Goods on Country, through A Curious Tractor, is participating in the 2026 Catalysing Impact program.

The useful framing is:

- QBE Foundation and Social Impact Hub are supporting a cohort of purpose-driven enterprises.
- PIN and skilled volunteers provide review, advisory, mentoring and practical support.
- Stage 1 is about capability building and investment readiness.
- Stage 2 is about competing for matched funding, with up to $400,000 available to an enterprise if external capital is secured and accepted by the steering committee.
- Goods is using the program to test whether impact investment, concessional debt and blended capital are actually right for this work.

## Why It Matters For Goods

This program is not just an application deadline. It is a forcing function for the parts Goods already knows need to mature:

- clearer financial model,
- stronger investment materials,
- legal structure decision,
- governance and risk discipline,
- better impact measurement,
- standard procurement and buyer evidence,
- source-controlled proof that can still be reviewed by humans,
- a capital pathway that does not distort the community-ownership vision.

The QBE pack should therefore be honest. It should show the strength of the work and name the gaps that support can help with.

## Program Lessons To Carry Into The Pack

The induction material and founder notes point to a few practical lessons:

- Investors ask whether the problem matters, whether this organisation is the right one to solve it, and whether the plan is achievable.
- Goods should be targeted about capital and drop mismatched funders quickly.
- Debt brings future cashflow forward, so it should only be taken against credible revenue timing.
- Grant money is scarce and should be used to crowd in the right capital, not cover every operating gap.
- Letters of intent and conditional commitments matter, but they need to be real enough for due diligence.

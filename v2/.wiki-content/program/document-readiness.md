# Document Readiness

> This page turns the evidence bundle into reviewable material. It does not pretend every source is send-ready. It names what each document proves, how it fits the Goods philosophy, and what needs human review before it is used with QBE, SIH, PIN, investors or community partners.

## How To Read This

Use this page with [[workshop-evidence-map]]. The point is not to show that Goods has a polished document for every question. The point is to show that the work has been thought through, that the strongest evidence is already visible, and that the remaining gaps are named honestly.

Status language:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Send-ready | Can be shared with light framing and no major rewrite |
| Review first | Useful evidence exists, but the language, numbers or claims need human review |
| Internal only | Useful for thinking, but not suitable to send as a standalone external document |
| Missing | Needs to be created or exported before the workshop |

## Goods Strategy PD

**Accessible source:** [[../sources/goods-strategy-pd]]
**Best use:** vision, ambition, strategic direction, community ownership pathway.

This is the January 2026 strategy/product document. It states Goods as a circular economy social enterprise co-designing beds, mattresses and washing machines with remote First Nations communities, and converting waste into manufacturing inputs. It names the mission as transferring ownership of production and profits to First Nations communities through local resource use.

What it shows:

- a clear mission and vision,
- an ambition for Goods beds to become a product of first choice in remote communities,
- early impact metrics and community demand evidence,
- a community ownership and profit-sharing ambition,
- the strategic link between product design, waste, health hardware and local enterprise.

Readiness judgement:

- Strong as an internal strategy source.
- Not fully send-ready without caveats because some January 2026 figures and product descriptions may be older than the current Stretch Bed truth.
- Needs review against the latest product specs, legal structure and funding position.

## March 2026 Compendium

**Accessible source:** [[../sources/march-2026-compendium]]
**Best use:** operating snapshot, team, brand voice, community data, deployment summary, funding and partner context.

This is the strongest single source for the broad Goods story. It contains the brand identity, team backgrounds, origin story, milestones, deployment summary, impact data, funding context and voice guidelines.

What it shows:

- the philosophy: "Our job is to become unnecessary",
- our team story,
- why the work connects beds, washing machines, scabies, RHD and health hardware,
- communities and deployment figures,
- demand signals, partners, funders and advisory context,
- voice guidance that avoids charity language.

Readiness judgement:

- Strong internal compendium.
- Review first before sending externally because counts differ across the wiki and source systems.
- Best used as source material for a shorter mentor-facing operating snapshot, not as the only document.

## Community Voices From The Ground

**Accessible source:** [[../sources/community-voices-from-the-ground]]
**Best use:** social objective, impact story, design validation, human evidence.

This document captures voices from 15 storytellers across 6 communities. It is the strongest evidence that the problem is real in homes, families and community routines, not just in market analysis.

What it shows:

- people describing the lack of beds and mattresses,
- freight and access barriers,
- safety, dignity and shame dynamics,
- why portability, washability and repairability matter,
- place-based evidence from Palm Island, Tennant Creek, Kalgoorlie, Mount Isa, Darwin and East Arnhem pathways.

Readiness judgement:

- Strong human evidence.
- Review first because story consent, quote use and community control of story must be checked before external sharing.
- Fits the Goods philosophy deeply when used with care.

## Production Facility Guide

**Accessible source:** [[../sources/production-facility-guide]]
**Best use:** process maturity, production transfer, operational proof.

This guide documents a containerised production facility for making Stretch Bed components from plastic waste. It includes site layout, equipment, power constraints, process flow, quality checks and safety details.

What it shows:

- the production system is not just a pitch idea,
- knowledge has been written down for transfer,
- equipment, constraints and workflow are visible,
- community ownership is treated as an operational design problem, not only a slogan.

Readiness judgement:

- Strong proof of operational thinking.
- Review first because it may contain assumptions that need reconciliation with current bill of materials and Notion production notes.
- Useful to show mentors that process maturity is emerging, even if not yet multi-site mature.

# Wiki Evidence Rebuild Audit

> This is the working standard for turning the Goods wiki from an index of source trails into a proof-rich operating pack. A good page should stand alone when we are not in the room: plain language, real photos, direct links, community voice, current numbers, claim status, and the human-review questions that still need judgement.

## The Standard

Every major wiki page should answer five questions without forcing the reader to open a local source file:

1. What is the claim?
2. What real evidence backs it?
3. What photo, video, document, Notion page or public route helps people see it?
4. What is verified, what is inferred, and what is still being tested?
5. What do we need to review before it becomes external material?

Reader-facing pages should not rely on hidden source mechanics as the answer. Those are useful for checking, but the real substance needs to be visible in the page itself.

The pattern is:

- lead with the human version,
- show product and community proof in the page,
- embed local images where they help,
- include direct website, Notion, Drive or Descript links where available,
- separate current evidence from future or possible metrics,
- name the gaps honestly,
- include a short human-review list at the end.

## Consent And Privacy Rules

- Treat named community quotes, portraits and videos as consent-sensitive until checked.
- Treat asset rows with names, contacts, household details or precise location as private.
- Treat washing-machine telemetry as private unless aggregated.
- Treat Xero, invoices, receivables and funder correspondence as internal unless Ben confirms sharing.
- Use claim labels: verified, computed, reported, requested, exploring, still being tested.

# Theory of Change (one paragraph)

> If we build products that actually survive remote conditions AND we run the production loop On-Country AND we transfer ownership to community over time AND we capture impact evidence with consent, THEN families get durable furniture at fair prices, waste stays out of dumps, jobs and wealth stay local, and communities control the narrative of what the work means. The alternative (charity-framed donations of cheap goods) is the status quo we are displacing.

## Inputs → Activities → Outputs → Outcomes → Impact

```
INPUTS          ACTIVITIES           OUTPUTS               OUTCOMES                 IMPACT
─────           ──────────           ───────               ────────                 ──────
Recycled HDPE   Product design       Stretch Bed units     Beds in homes            Child health
Steel           Container plant      Washing machines      Hygiene infrastructure   School attendance
Canvas          Assembly             Revenue               Jobs in community        Economic agency
Grant capital   Deployment           Jobs created          Plastic diverted         Waste reduced
Debt capital    Storytelling (EL)    Stories (consented)   Community evidence       Narrative sovereignty
Advisory        Ownership transfer   Plant ownership       Community ownership      Systemic shift
```

## Three shifts we are trying to produce

1. **Material:** durable goods replace disposable imports.
2. **Economic:** profits stay in community, plants become community-owned.
3. **Narrative:** communities control what the work means via Empathy Ledger.

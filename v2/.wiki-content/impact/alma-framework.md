# ALMA Framework

> ALMA = Adaptive Learning for Meaningful Accountability. ACT's impact framework. Instead of forcing every insight into a single metric, ALMA asks six signal questions of every data point. Designed to respect Indigenous data sovereignty rather than override it.

## The six signals

| Signal | Question |
|---|---|
| **Authority** | Who holds decision-making power over this data? |
| **Evidence** | What type of evidence is this? (story, survey, observation, measurement) |
| **Harm Risk** | What's the risk of sharing it? |
| **Capability** | How ready is the system to act on it? |
| **Option Value** | Does this open future possibilities? |
| **Community Value Return** | Does value flow back to the community that generated the data? |

## Why this instead of SROI

- SROI forces every impact into a dollar value, which is extractive on its own terms when the "value" being counted is cultural knowledge.
- Single-number frameworks invert the power relationship: the funder decides what counts, not the community.
- ALMA lets multiple signals coexist and flag when a given piece of evidence is ready to act on versus still in dialogue.

## How Goods uses it

Every impact data point we collect (story, survey, bed-journey record, washing-machine telemetry event) can be tagged against the six signals. In practice:
- **Stories** (Empathy Ledger): Authority held by storyteller, Evidence = story, Harm Risk managed by consent, Community Value Return guaranteed by platform architecture.
- **Bed deployments:** Authority = community partner, Evidence = observation, Harm Risk low, Capability high (system ready to scale).
- **Fleet telemetry:** Authority = community hosting, Evidence = measurement, requires consent framing not just aggregate.

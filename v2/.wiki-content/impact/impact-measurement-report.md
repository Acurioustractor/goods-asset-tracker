# Impact Measurement Report

> QBE Stage 2 will need an impact report, but Goods does not yet have a single polished measurement document. The next version should be candid: what we know from deliveries and stories, what the product data shows, and what we still cannot claim.

## Recommended structure

1. The problem: sleep, hygiene, repair, waste and money leaving community.
2. What Goods does: products, production loop and ownership pathway.
3. Current evidence: deployments, communities, feedback, stories, partner check-ins and limited telemetry.
4. What people told us: consented quotes and story summaries, with community control over use.
5. What we are testing next: QR check-ins, bed survival, wash frequency, repairs and referrals.
6. What we cannot claim yet: longitudinal health outcomes, clinical sleep data or full economic return.
7. What capital unlocks: inventory, local plants, jobs, repair loops and stronger measurement.

## Tone rule

Do not make the report sound more mature than the work. The credibility comes from clarity, not polish.

Use "we know", "we heard", "we are testing" and "we do not know yet" as separate categories.

## Current state

- Not yet compiled as a single document.
- Underlying material exists in transcripts, stories, product records, Xero, partner notes and app data.
- Priority before Stage 2 is to compile a report that investors can trust without stripping out the human evidence.

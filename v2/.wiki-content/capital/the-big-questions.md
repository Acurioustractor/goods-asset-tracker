# The Big Questions to Decide

> Five capital decisions every founder makes before picking instruments. From the CASE Smart Impact Capital framework via the QBE induction.

## The five questions

| Question | What it decides | Goods' answer |
|---|---|---|
| **Making debt repayments:** are you able to make regular cash payments to investors (e.g. quarterly)? | Can you take debt? | **Yes.** 50-55% gross margin at scale; institutional B2B cashflow predictable. |
| **Type of ownership:** are you planning for an exit (IPO, acquisition, buyout)? | Is equity on the table? | **No in the conventional sense.** Community-ownership transfer (PICC plant purchase) is the designated "exit". |
| **Valuation:** are you willing to let an investor set a valuation? | Price discovery for equity | **Deferred.** Not needed for 2026 (debt + grant + recoverable). Required if we open equity later. |
| **Growth rate:** are you targeting 3-10x or more in 5 years? | Does the VC math work? | **Yes, lower end (3-5x).** NT Housing $4B program + institutional demand supports it. Not hyper-growth VC territory. |
| **Customisation level:** are you willing to negotiate customised terms with each investor? | Do you have one-size-fits-all product, or bespoke? | **Yes.** Every partner is bespoke given community-ownership clauses. |

## What this locks in for Goods

- **Debt: yes** → SEFA, IBA, possibly PRI Debt from Mindaroo.
- **Equity: deferred** → no 2026 round; revisit in 2027 once CLG/Ventures structure decisions land (see [[../governance/legal-structure]]).
- **Grant + recoverable: yes** → QBE, Snow, PFI, Mindaroo.
- **Guarantees: yes** → Snow letter of support as catalytic signal.
- **Crowdfunding: plausible but not 2026** → Kindship-style DPO for community investors could work later.

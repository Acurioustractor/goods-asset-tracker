## What the diagnostic actually is (and where we drifted from it)

The SIH diagnostic scores **10 areas** — *not* the 12 we've been tracking. Areas **11 (Cost Model v6)** and **12 (Investor Alignment Tool)** are our *own* internal lenses, not part of the SIH framework. The diagnostic gives each area a **current→future maturity score**, flags **5 Priority Opportunity Areas**, and lands **6 ranked recommendations** — **two of which are external projects** (one SIH advisory, one QBE skilled-volunteering). That last point is the thing we've half-lost and most need to realign on.

**Headline pattern.** SIH scored us **8/10 on Vision** and **7/10 on Process & Tech** — both above the "managed" line, neither a priority gap. Every priority gap sits in the **paperwork layer**. That matches our own one-liner exactly: **strong proof, weak paperwork.** The diagnostic agrees with us about what's *good*; the open question is whether we've moved the *gaps* in the four weeks since 1 May.

## What we still need to align on

1. **Report against SIH's 10, not our 12.** Keep 11/12 as internal tools, but the scorecard we discuss with SIH/QBE is the 10.

2. **The two external projects are the biggest open items — and both sit in Area 3:**
   - **SIH Priority Advisory** (production-cost tool): did our v6 model **pre-empt, satisfy, or duplicate** it? SIH explicitly gated their tool on "a GOC-only financial model" existing first — so **rec #1 is doubly load-bearing** (it unblocks both #1 and #2).
   - **QBE Skilled-Volunteering** (market demand research): appears **not started**; it's the named QBE deliverable. Needs scoping — which ≤4 segments (primary = beds, secondary = washing machines), who, by when.

3. **The real deadline is rec #1, ~1 July, blocked on the founder FTE rate + accountant** — the same single decision our internal stack already ranks #1. We're *aligned internally*; we just haven't made the call.

4. **Numbers basis drift.** The diagnostic anchors on **~$537,595** cumulative revenue (1 May); we now run **$649,710.79** paid (workpaper). Reconcile the basis before anything goes back, or it reads as inflation — exactly the "aspirational vs active" flag SIH raised.

5. **SIH's process rule (a recommendation, not a nicety):** *nothing* goes back to SIH/QBE unless a founder has audited it and can defend it in live Q&A — they flagged our pre-read as AI-ish/aspirational. **Founder sign-off is a release gate on the whole pack**, not a final polish.

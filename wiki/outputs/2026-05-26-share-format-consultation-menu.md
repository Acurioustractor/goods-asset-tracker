# "How would you like to share this?" — consultation menu

> **Date:** 2026-05-26
> **Purpose:** Answers Benjamin's point 1: ask the three first audiences (community, Centrecorp, Oonchiumpa) HOW they want to share the Utopia story, in the format they actually use. Fold this into the existing "consent pending" conversation: what to share AND how to share, one talk, respects their time.
> **Use:** A prompt sheet to bring into each conversation. Not a form to hand over. Ask, listen, note what they pick.

---

## The ask, in one line
"We have built the story of the trip. Before we share it anywhere, two things: are you happy with what is in it, and how would *you* like to be able to use it?"

## The consent half (what to share)
Confirm per person and per clip/photo:
- Happy to be named, or kept unnamed?
- Happy on the public site, or community/partner only?
- Any photo, quote, or video to pull?
- Who else needs to say yes (family, organisation, Elders)?

Current "consent pending" items to clear: the welcome speaker at Utopia, Johnny, the two Ampilatwatja Elders, Katrina Bloomfield's quote, the Oonchiumpa young people's footage.

## The format half (how to share)
"A link to a website is one option. It might also be none of these, or something we have not thought of. What would actually be useful to you?"

Lay these out as options, not a pitch:

- **A photo set they can text around** (phone-ready, no app, no login)
- **A short video** (60 to 90 seconds, sendable on WhatsApp/SMS)
- **A printable one-pager** (PDF, A4, for a wall or a desk)
- **A slide deck** ready to drop into a board or community meeting
- **An audio piece** (a few minutes, for people who do not read screens)
- **Printed copies** that go back out on the next trip
- **A book**, like the one Nic's dad made (community loved that)
- **A private link** that lives somewhere they can reach without remembering a URL
- **Something else entirely** (the most important option: leave room for it)

## Tailor the prompt per audience (what they are likely trying to do)

**Oonchiumpa** (Karen, the team): grant submissions, recruiting young people, explaining the model to government, board reporting, socials.
→ Likely useful: the 90-second Mykel/Fred clip for grant apps, a clean photo set for socials, the "program behind the door" framing as a grabbable block. Ask: "What would help you recruit the next mob? What does a funder need to see?"

**Centrecorp Foundation** (the board / trustees): justify and grow the investment internally, possibly bring other funders alongside.
→ Likely useful: a trustee one-pager, an impact summary, a video for meetings, a co-brandable asset. Ask: "What do your trustees need to see to back the next round? Would a version with both logos help you bring others in?"

**Community** (Kristy, the Arlparra team, the Soapy Bore mob, families): photos back to families, something they can hold, something on Country not behind a URL.
→ Likely useful: printed copies on the next trip, a community-facing version they can reach easily, a book. Ask: "What would the families actually want? What should come back out with us next time?"

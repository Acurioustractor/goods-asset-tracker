# QBE Mentor Interview 1: CEO Review and Pack Plan

## TL;DR

We are not under-prepared. We are over-distributed. The wiki is genuinely board-grade in three areas (vision, business model, process and tech) and genuinely thin in three areas (financial management, governance, people and organisation). The mentor pack we deliver should reflect both honestly. The pack's job is not to impress. The pack's job is to compress a mentor's first 20 minutes of pre-read into a single ask: help us close $400K of matched capital by 31 August 2026 and seat the structures that unlock SEFA and IBA debt.

Three things are doing the heavy lifting in this CEO review:

1. **Mentor matching is the gate, not the meeting.** We owe two surveys (Induction Evaluation, due 3 April; Mentoring Preferences, due 10 April). Both appear overdue as of today. Until those are in, no mentor is assigned and no first session is scheduled. This is the single highest-leverage action in the entire prep cycle.

2. **The 51% Indigenous ownership structure is existential to the GTM.** Four of the eight procurement channels (NT Housing, Aboriginal Hostels, NACCHO/ACCHOs, State AHO, mining-region foundations to a partial extent) gate on Supply Nation registration and IPP eligibility. IPP tightens 1 July 2026. We have ~10 weeks to land a structure that today exists as a sketch. If a mentor can only do one thing for us, this is it.

3. **The financial model is the thing investors will pull on first.** SEFA and IBA cannot underwrite to a directional 5-year cashflow. The 3-statement integrated model is named in `enterprise/04-financial-management.md` as a priority gap. Without it, the QBE match-funding milestone at 31 August is at risk because the LOIs we are chasing all need a model to anchor diligence.

Everything else, including the 8-channel GTM, the blended capital stack, and the washing machine's go-to-market, sits downstream of those three.

## 4. Volunteer and PIN engagement strategy

Skilled volunteers are not a generic resource. Each ask must be specific enough that PIN, Mint Ellison, or the advisory group can match a person inside two weeks. Below is the literal "ask register" we should walk into the diagnostic with.

### Critical, 60 to 90 days

1. **CFO-grade financial modeller, 4-6 weeks engagement.** Profile: ex Big 4 or impact-fund CFO, has built models that survived SEFA, IBA, or equivalent debt diligence. Output: 3-statement integrated model, 5-year horizon, base/downside/upside, debt-covenant module, unit-economics tab per channel. This is the highest-leverage volunteer ask in the cohort.

2. **Indigenous corporate structures lawyer, 6-8 weeks engagement.** Profile: senior lawyer with co-operative, ORIC, or Indigenous-owned corporate structure track record. Output: term sheet and constituting documents for 51% Indigenous-owned operating entity (Goods Co-op Ltd or equivalent), Supply Nation registration pathway, IPP-eligibility pack. Mint Ellison's Keith Rovers is named in the wiki as already in the orbit. We escalate this from "warm" to "first pro-bono priority".

3. **Independent director recruitment, 60 days, 2-3 directors seated.** Profile: needs at least one with social-enterprise governance experience, one with finance / audit committee experience, one with Indigenous lived experience or deep First-Nations governance practice. Pathway: convert 1-2 advisory group members (April Long, Sally Grimsley-Ballard, or Daniel Pittman are plausible candidates) plus one fresh recruit via PIN.

### High, inside 6 months

4. **Pakkimjalki Kari washing machine GTM lead.** Conditional on the kill/defer/commit decision in §6 below. Profile: whitegoods commercial procurement or remote retail (Outback Stores or ALPA alumni). Output: 12-month commercial trajectory, retail channel deal, signed pilot procurement.

5. **Procurement channel intelligence volunteers, ongoing.** Profile: retired or alumni officers from DHLGCD, NIAA, AHL, AHO. Output: warm intro paths to the right named buyer in each of the eight channels, what specifically each procurement panel needs to list us.

6. **Impact evaluation researcher, 6-12 month engagement.** Profile: PhD or postdoc in remote health, ideally Lowitja Institute or NACCHO research network. Output: independent longitudinal study design (sleep, scabies, RHD, school attendance), ethics-approved.

### Medium, opportunistic

7. **Mining-region foundation introductions.** Profile: someone with active relationships at BHP Foundation, Rio Tinto Foundation, Glencore Coal Communities. Output: 1-2 warm intros, "Mining Sector Pack" pre-feedback.

### Where we should NOT use volunteers

- Strategic vision and storytelling (Ben's keep).
- Brand voice (Ben + Fred).
- Community relationships (Nick + Fred).
- Product design (proven internally over 140+ beds).
- Technical product and Empathy Ledger (Ben).

The discipline here is critical. Outsourced narrative is brittle. Outsourced relationships rot. We protect those, and free up Ben's calendar to plug in skilled volunteers on the financial and governance gaps.

## 6. Top 5 questions to bring to mentor session 1

These are stitched into the pack. The mentor is told upfront that we want their judgement on these.

1. **51% Indigenous ownership.** Given the 1 July 2026 IPP eligibility tightening, what is the fastest credible path from sketch to binding term sheet with Oonchiumpa Bloomfield Family Trust and Aboriginal Investment NT? Who in your network would you put us in front of inside 14 days?

2. **Financial model.** We need a 3-statement integrated model in 60 days that survives SEFA and IBA underwriting. Should we engage a PIN volunteer for 4-6 weeks, hire an interim CFO, or both? What does the model need to do that we are likely to underspecify?

3. **Match-funding sequencing.** With $400K to match-fund by 31 August across a warm but unsigned pipeline (Snow R4, SEFA, Mindaroo, PFI), which deal do you push us to close first, and why? What is the killer LOI we want signed inside 30 days?

4. **Pakkimjalki Kari champion.** The washing machine has community pull (Dianne Stokes received one and asked for 20 more inside two weeks) and 11 fielded units with telemetry, but no GTM champion. We are not asking whether to kill it. We are asking whether you can help us find a champion: ideally someone with whitegoods commercial procurement or remote retail background (Outback Stores or ALPA alumni). What is your network in that space? What evidence would help us screen the right person inside 12 weeks?

5. **Independent directors.** SEFA covenants will require an independent director majority. We have advisors but no seated independents. Who is your recommended first independent director profile, and how do we attract them in 60 days without paying market fees?

## 9. The CEO read

We are in an unusually well-prepared spot for an impact-grant program at this stage of life. The qualitative pack (vision, business model, impact, narrative) is genuinely better than most of what walks into Stage 2. The quantitative pack (financial model, governance, signed LOIs) is exactly where most early-stage social enterprises fail.

The mentor should be asked, explicitly, to be a force-multiplier on the quantitative gaps and let us keep doing the qualitative work ourselves. That is the cleanest division of labour.

The single biggest risk between now and 31 August is not the program. It is that the founder tries to plug the financial and governance gaps personally, gets stuck, and the entire match-funding window slips. The pack must signal "we know we cannot solve these alone, here is exactly what would help, and here is who you would match us with."

If the mentor walks out of session 1 with that crisp picture, the pack has done its job.

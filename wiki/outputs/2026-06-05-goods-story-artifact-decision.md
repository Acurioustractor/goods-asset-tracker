# The Goods story artifact: what form it takes

Decision report for Ben, 2026-06-05. Produced from a deep-research run (111 agents, 28 sources fetched, 126 claims extracted, 25 claims put through 3-vote adversarial verification: 24 confirmed, 1 refuted). Question: book, documentary, combination, or living ledger?

## The verdict

**The living ledger is the artifact. Build it as an accumulating, consent-governed public feed now. The book is a crystallization you print FROM the ledger at month 10 to 12. The documentary is deferred, and probably never yours to make alone.**

This was the highest-confidence finding of the run, and every audience test, budget test, and precedent test pointed the same way. The strongest single reason: you already own the rarest part. Empathy Ledger, with consent gates, storyteller control, cultural tags, and place data, is the infrastructure HONY never had and StoryCorps took two decades to build. The artifact form question is really a surfacing question.

### Why each alternative loses as the primary form

- **Book first**: a book is a snapshot. It freezes a story that is still moving (community ownership transfer has not happened yet). Self-publishing economics in Australia put a quality photo-book run at AU$15 to 30K of the budget for an object with no compounding audience. Verified pattern: HONY's books worked BECAUSE the feed came first; the books were crystallizations of an already-loved corpus, each one a bestseller pre-sold to millions of followers (confirmed 3-0).
- **Documentary first**: In My Blood It Runs took roughly eight years end to end (confirmed 3-0) with a production company, screen-agency finance, and a co-designed cultural governance process. Screen Australia mandates ICIP protocols as a funding condition (confirmed 2-1), which you would clear, but the timeline and the AU$300K+ budgets are simply a different vehicle. A documentary about Goods is something a production company makes WITH the communities in years 3 to 5, ideally after your feed has proven the audience exists.
- **"Combination" as a plan**: doing all three at once on $50K and one founder is how nothing ships. Sequencing IS the combination: ledger now, book from the ledger later, documentary when someone else funds it.

### The verified precedent chain

1. **HONY (confirmed 3-0 across 5 claims)**: started 2010 as a photography project; the accumulating feed came first; the feed later raised ~US$20M for the people in the stories; books crystallized from the feed, not before it. What to copy: one face, one voice, one place per post; the storyteller's words, not the organisation's; relentless cadence over polish. What to avoid: the founder IS the brand (Brandon Stanton's eye, voice, and judgment are unreplaceable, a real fragility), and consent practice that was street-level informal. You have a consent system; HONY had a handshake.
2. **StoryCorps (confirmed 3-0)**: the proof that a consent-governed living archive is a durable centering artifact. The archive accumulates; books, animations, and broadcasts are all downstream cuts. The archive is the institution. That is the Empathy Ledger end-state.
3. **In My Blood It Runs (confirmed 3-0 / 2-1)**: the Australian First Nations proof that non-extractive, co-designed storytelling WORKS and moves audiences and policy, and that it takes years and a coalition. Its impact campaign is the model for how a story artifact feeds policy and funder outcomes. Copy the cultural governance (storytellers as co-designers with veto, ongoing consent); avoid the timeline as your critical path.
4. **Ngapartji Ngapartji / Big hART (confirmed 3-0)**: deliberately combined many storytelling forms around one community over years, governed by Indigenous consent and cultural protocols, and left an explicit durable living archive behind. The closest Australian shape to what Goods is doing: the project is the work on country, the archive is the artifact, the shows/films/books are cuts.
5. **Mukurtu + Ara Irititja (confirmed 3-0, with one claim killed)**: Mukurtu CMS was born from a 2007 collaboration with the Warumungu community in Tennant Creek (confirmed 3-0). Your washing machine was named in Warumungu by Elder Dianne Stokes. The global standard for consent-governed Indigenous archives and your project share a community. That is a movement-narrative gift: Goods + Empathy Ledger sits in a recognised lineage of community-controlled archives, with a contemporary, product-attached twist. **Caution (the killed claim)**: verifiers refuted the claim that Ara Irititja has been straightforwardly replicated by 20+ groups as a copyable template (1-2). Do not build the ACT pitch on "archives replicate easily." Build it on "we built the consent layer once, other ACT projects plug into it," which is your actual architecture. Also verified: TK Labels are not legally binding (3-0); they are protocol signals, so your enforcement lives in the consent gates, not the labels.

### What the funder-perception angle found

The identifiable-victim research (UC Press, Collabra; confirmed) supports what you intuited: a single named person with a face moves giving decisions more than statistics. But for catalytic capital specifically (your QBE/Minderoo lane), the artifact is evidence of TRACTION, not a substitute for the numbers. The feed shows funders three things a report cannot: demand is real (people in communities, named, using the product), the relationship is real (ongoing consented presence, not a fly-in shoot), and the model is alive (the same archive that builds public audience IS the impact evidence). First Australians Capital / Block coverage shows Australian catalytic funders explicitly buying Indigenous-led, relationship-evidenced models. The ledger's funder cut is your "evidence with a human face" layer on top of the canonical numbers, never instead of them.

## The 12-month plan inside AU$50K

Cadence rule (the whole plan in one line): **weekly story post, monthly field note, quarterly funder cut, annual printed volume.**

**Q1, Jun to Aug 2026 (unlock + cadence): ~$8K**
- Flip the consent-cleared voices live: Mykel + the 6 Utopia voices already cleared. The 165-photo tagged library + EL quotes = a 3-month content runway that exists today.
- Start the weekly post. The unit is the HONY unit: one photo, the person's own words, their name, their place. Publish on goodsoncountry.com first (the ledger surface: /stories + field notes already built), syndicate to Instagram/social.
- Write the one-page story protocol (True Tracks-shaped: free prior informed consent, ongoing consent with re-checks, storyteller payment rates, Elder review for anything cultural, no sacred content, unpublish on request). This document is itself a movement artifact.
- Spend: storyteller payments + Elder honorariums for the first cleared set.

**Q2, Sep to Nov (field rhythm + QBE window): ~$14K**
- Every field trip ships a field note within 2 weeks (the Utopia pattern is the template). Trips are already happening; the marginal cost is capture discipline plus edit time.
- Contract a part-time story editor/producer (the single most leveraged hire at ~$1K/month-equivalent of days): cuts trip material into the weekly units so cadence survives Ben's calendar.
- September QBE application: the funder cut goes in as traction evidence alongside the financials.
- Kit + capture: a small camera/audio kit upgrade so any team member or community member can capture to standard.

**Q3, Dec to Feb (community hands on the ledger): ~$13K**
- Pilot community editorial control: 2 to 3 paid community storyteller/reviewer roles (this is the ownership-transfer story enacted in the artifact itself, and the strongest anti-extraction move available).
- Backfill the ~21 EL profiles; publish-flip the cleared backlog; quotes-with-place live on captions (architecture doc build steps 3 and 4).
- First "movement" cut: a how-it-works piece on the consent infrastructure for ACT, referencing the Mukurtu/Warumungu lineage.

**Q4, Mar to May 2027 (crystallize): ~$12K**
- Print **Volume 1**: a 100 to 140 page field-notes annual from the year's consented feed. Short run (~500), designed on the existing brand system, launched in community before anywhere else. Storytellers get copies and a revenue/recognition share per the protocol.
- Decision gate with evidence: if the feed has compounded (archive size, list growth, funder citations), Volume 1 becomes the publisher conversation and the documentary becomes a coalition conversation. You will be choosing among suitors instead of pitching cold.

**Contingency: ~$3K.** Total: ~$50K. Nothing in this plan depends on the documentary or a publisher saying yes.

## Top 3 risks

1. **Extraction / cultural harm (highest stakes).** Mitigations already designed, now made non-negotiable: FPIC with ONGOING consent re-checks (verified as the ICIP standard, 3-0), storyteller-controlled unpublish, payment for stories, Elder review, and the quarantine pattern from the architecture doc §9 (agents that read unconsented material cannot publish; only the gated publisher touches the public surface). The protocol doc makes this legible to funders and communities alike. Remember the killed claim: do not over-promise replicability of community archives; promise control.
2. **Founder dependency (the HONY trap).** Brandon Stanton is unsubstitutable and HONY ages with him. Mitigations: the byline is the storyteller, not Ben; the editor hire takes cadence off Ben's plate by Q2; community storyteller roles in Q3 make capture survivable without Ben on every trip; and the ledger itself (EL, not a camera roll) is the institutional memory.
3. **Attention economics (feeds die quietly).** HONY took roughly three years to compound; most feeds stop at month 4. Mitigations: a cadence sized to reality (weekly, not daily); a 3-month runway banked before launch (you have it: 165 photos + cleared quotes); one capture re-cut many ways via the artefact engine so each trip feeds a month; measure compounding by archive growth, list growth, and funder citations, not likes. The annual volume gives every year a finish line even if social reach plateaus.

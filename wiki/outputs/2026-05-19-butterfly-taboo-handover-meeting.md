# Butterfly Movement Ltd / TABOO Foundation handover meeting

## Context and background

- The Butterfly Movement Ltd charity is transitioning stewardship from the TABOO Foundation board to a Goods on Country / A Curious Tractor board
- TABOO Foundation has five directors; Sonia (accountant/lawyer) and Bryony participated in this meeting
- The Goods on Country team in attendance: Nic, Ben, and Kristy (prospective director)
- The full TABOO Foundation board has been informed of the transition progress
- Same legal entity continues throughout: not a separate organisation, all operations remain under the same reporting structure

## Transition mechanism

- Directors can resign and be replaced through casual vacancy appointments under **clause 16.5 of the Butterfly Movement constitution**
- New directors can be nominated by the board without waiting for the AGM
- Formal confirmation of new directors will occur at the AGM alongside financial statement approval
- One transitional director from the TABOO side may remain temporarily to support the handover and ensure clean transition
- Constitution stipulates a minimum of three directors

## Financial cleanup strategy

- Goal: hand over a clean balance sheet without TABOO Foundation's historical liabilities
- All TABOO Foundation debt will be written off by the company
- Estimated $10,000 to $15,000 in final costs to wrap up TABOO Foundation activities
- Financial cleanup can be achieved through accounting adjustments rather than physical transactions
- Clean end-of-year balance sheet is the target, with audited statements to follow

## Donation and auspicing considerations

- Opportunity to attract donations before 30 June 2026 for current financial year tax purposes
- Discussed potential for Butterfly Movement to receive and auspice donations on behalf of Goods on Country during transition
- TABOO Foundation has a standard operating procedure (SOP) for auspicing requests that requires board approval
- Auspicing must align with TABOO Foundation's mission, vision and strategic priorities, which differs from the charity objects, so auspicing scope is narrower than the constitution scope
- Donation receipts can be retrospectively issued once a DGR application comes through
- Note: the charity already holds DGR status, so issuing receipts may be available without a new DGR application

## Administrative tasks

- Update ACNC with new responsible people
- Update bank account signatories
- Update directors with ASIC
- Bank account: existing account is in Butterfly Foundation name, associated with the ACN
- Constitution requires authorised signatories to reflect current directors
- Strong board secretary critical for back-end ACNC functions and system access (last transition's biggest pain point was administrative backlog)

## Key considerations and learnings

- Same legal entity continuing means continuous reporting; no need to re-incorporate
- Previous transition experience showed administrative updates took significant time and persistence
- Having a dedicated person with time and expertise for the administrative back end is crucial
- Goal: clean handover without creating confusion about which directors carry responsibility for which periods

## What this unblocks for the SIH diagnostic narrative

The diagnostic (V4) identified governance and legal structure as priority opportunity areas. Each of those is materially advanced by this transition:

- **Aboriginal-led board** with Kristy Bloomfield (confirmed) as a founding director, and a clear pathway for further Aboriginal directors from the Goods advisory group and from manufacturing groups themselves
- **DGR-enabled charity** with constitutional alignment to Goods on Country's purpose, removing the SIH-flagged gap of "no clean DGR pathway for philanthropic capital"
- **Three-engine operating model** (Goods on Country Pty Ltd as product engine, Butterfly Movement Ltd as charity engine, community-led production entities as ownership engine) which directly answers SIH Section 5 (operating model) and Section 9 (legal structure)
- **Same legal entity continuing** removes the SIH-flagged risk of "relative newness of incorporated entity" — Butterfly Movement Ltd has continuous reporting history
- **Member-led future board** with manufacturing-group representatives answers SIH Section 7 (board skills mix) and Section 8 (capacity transfer from founders to community partners)
- **Pre-30-June auspicing window** unlocks immediate donor activation, which directly answers SIH Section 10 (capital raising readiness)

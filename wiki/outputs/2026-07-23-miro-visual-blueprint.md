# Goods on Country Miro visual blueprint

**Purpose:** Define the visual architecture, asset shortlist and evidence gaps for the living Goods community opportunity atlas and its derived funder slide deck.

## The visual rule

Each major story frame should contain four things:

1. **A human or place anchor:** who, where and what was asked for.
2. **A system explanation:** how the request connects to Goods modules and wider systems.
3. **Evidence:** an image, video, map, number, document or measured result.
4. **A fundable next step:** what capital makes possible and what must be validated first.

Do not fill frames with galleries. Use one hero, one supporting visual and one proof artefact wherever possible. Keep the larger library in a separate evidence frame.

## Canvas architecture

The canvas should read left to right at three zoom levels.

### Zoom level 1: The whole opportunity

A visitor should understand the idea without opening anything:

- Start where the community is.
- Every community is different.
- Goods holds a shared backbone and modular capability menu.
- Communities choose the useful next step.
- Funders can back an outcome, a module, a pathway or the shared backbone.

### Zoom level 2: The community portfolio

The centre of the canvas should be a map connected to community pathway cards:

- Utopia
- Oonchiumpa / Alice Springs
- Maningrida
- Palm Island
- Tennant Creek
- Other live or emerging requests, shown only when verified

Every card uses the same structure:

- Place
- What community asked for
- Why
- What is already strong
- Modules selected
- Current stage
- Evidence available
- Readiness gaps
- Complete cost status
- Next fundable step

### Zoom level 3: Evidence and economics

The lower and right-hand zones hold:

- Module details
- Pricing canvas
- Unit economics and break-even
- Equipment and process proof
- Story and consent register
- Existing case studies
- Source documents

## What not to use

- Jaquilane material while the clearance conflict remains
- Draft or reconstructed quotes
- Old Weave Bed references
- Basket Bed imagery as if it is the current product
- Equipment-only figures presented as complete project budgets
- Modelled economics without their label
- Named community requests that have not been confirmed

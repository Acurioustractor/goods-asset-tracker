## 1. Clarity of Vision and Ambition (V4 score: 8/9, near-final state)

Goods on Country exists to build the missing layer of household infrastructure in remote First Nations communities: durable, repairable, washable, locally-makeable essential goods that move from outside delivery to On-Country production. The Stretch Bed is the proof point. The vision is a network of Aboriginal-owned and operated production entities making, repairing and improving the goods their own communities need, with Goods on Country providing the design system, training, evidence and back-office capability behind them.

- Long-form vision and three-horizon roadmap: [`thoughts/shared/qbe-program/diagnostic/01-vision-and-ambition.md`](../../thoughts/shared/qbe-program/diagnostic/01-vision-and-ambition.md)
- Public Stretch Bed product page: [goodsoncountry.com/shop/stretch-bed-single](https://www.goodsoncountry.com/shop/stretch-bed-single)
- Canonical product specs: [`v2/src/lib/data/products.ts`](../../v2/src/lib/data/products.ts)
- Compendium with full vision context: [`v2/docs/COMPENDIUM_MARCH_2026.md`](../../v2/docs/COMPENDIUM_MARCH_2026.md)

**Butterfly overlay:** the transition operationalises the long-term ownership pathway by separating the product engine (Goods on Country Pty Ltd) from the public-benefit engine (Butterfly Movement Ltd) from the community-ownership engine (manufacturing groups represented on the Butterfly board as members with voting rights). See [`wiki/outputs/2026-05-19-butterfly-movement-transition-proposal.md`](2026-05-19-butterfly-movement-transition-proposal.md) Section 5.

## 2. Social Objective and Impact Measurement (V4 score: 5/8, priority opportunity)

Goods addresses a precise and measurable problem: poor-quality mainstream products fail in remote conditions, replacement cycles drive household financial drain and environmental waste, and the lack of durable washable bedding is implicated in scabies and rheumatic heart disease. The /impact page now tags every public metric (6 verified, 5 modelled, 6 estimated, 1 design, 2 partial) so funders can distinguish today's evidence from future-state targets. The ALMA framework (Authority, Evidence, Harm Risk, Capability, Option Value, Community Value Return) governs how impact is measured, with the Empathy Ledger providing consent-led storyteller infrastructure across 33 Goods-connected storytellers.

## 3. Business Model Clarity and Sustainability (V4 score: 4/7, priority opportunity)

Goods sells durable essential goods to remote communities through three validated channels: institutional B2B at $560 per bed (community-controlled organisations, councils, philanthropy-funded delivery), retail at $600 (direct ecommerce, live since 18 May), and production services through the On-Country plant. Unit economics are documented across four volume tiers from $550 today (~15/month) to $479 at 1,500/year, $351 at 5,000/year, and $270 at 12,000/year. The seven-segment revenue model targets $1.13M in Year 1 from a mix of donor-funded institutional (44%), direct institutional commercial (27%), corporate RAP (9%), community/maker (5%), government (4%), direct retail (9%) and adjacent (2%).

## 5. Strategic Planning and Risk Management (V4 score: 5/7, priority opportunity)

Goods operates on an LCAA methodology (Listen, Co-design, Adapt, Account) that is genuinely adaptive: a practice loop rather than a Gantt. A working risk register covers strategic, operational, financial, reputational/cultural and compliance/legal categories, with key-person risk on Ben and Nick named explicitly at High/High and a mitigation strategy of "transfer capability to community partners and formalise governance, not hire our way out". Three scenarios (Base / Upside / Downside) are now modelled across the 36-month cashflow with explicit operational defer/slow/accelerate lists tied to a four-state buffer policy (NEGATIVE, BELOW_FLOOR less than 3 months runway, BELOW_TARGET 3 to 6 months, OK greater than or equal to 6 months).

## 9. Legal Structure (V4 score: 5/7)

A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd was recently incorporated as the trading vehicle for Goods. The proposed future-state structure is a hub-and-spoke model: Goods on Country Pty Ltd as the trading and operating company, The Butterfly Movement Ltd as the aligned DGR charity, and community-controlled production entities (Oonchiumpa initially; further entities as production capability rolls out On Country). The Butterfly Movement Ltd has continuous reporting history under its existing ACN; the transition uses casual vacancy appointments under clause 16.5 of the existing constitution, so the legal entity continues unchanged while stewardship changes. Mission-protection mechanisms (B Corp certification or equivalent, social-purpose clauses, cultural-protection provisions) are on the long-term map.

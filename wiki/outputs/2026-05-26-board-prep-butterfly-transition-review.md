# Board-prep review: Butterfly Movement / Goods transition (1 June 2026)

> **For:** Ben (to prepare for, and table at, the 1 June board meeting)
> **Reviewed:** the 4 Board Resolutions + Butterfly Movement Transition Plan in `01.06.2026/`
> **Date:** 2026-05-26
> **Bottom line:** the pack handles the *compliance handover* well. It is light on the *governance design* that actually delivers "Aboriginal-led", silent on *members* (the real control lever), and completely silent on *IP ownership*, which is central to the whole "hand over to community" mission. None of this is legal advice: the standouts below should go to the charity lawyer (Mint Ellison) before 26 June.

## What the pack covers well
- Same-entity stewardship transition (not a new charity): lower risk, faster.
- Director swap sequenced against the constitution, minimum-3 maintained, casual-vacancy pathway.
- Sonia retained for continuity + a hard audit deadline (1 Oct) with a named owner.
- Clean balance-sheet / historical-liability review (TABOO legacy) before handover.
- Warm, respectful exit for outgoing directors.

## The gaps, by what you flagged

### 1. First Nations leadership + MEMBERS (the real lever, and it's missing)
- "Aboriginal-led governance" appears as a *principle* and a "build toward", but the pack never **defines** it or builds the mechanism. Define it: majority of Aboriginal directors? Aboriginal chair? Reserved community seats? Cultural-authority / Elders body with defined say? Right now it is an intention, not a structure.
- **The bigger miss: members, not just directors.** Butterfly is a company limited by guarantee. **Members** appoint and remove directors and amend the constitution. If membership stays with the outgoing TABOO people, or quietly defaults to the founders, then any "Aboriginal-led" director line-up can be reversed later by whoever holds the membership. Durable Aboriginal control is secured at the **member** level. The pack only asks *"whether member resolutions are required"* (procedural) and never asks *who the members are or should be*. **This is the single most important thing to resolve.**
- The proposed board is thin and contingent: Kristy Bloomfield (Aboriginal, confirmed), **Audrey Deemal (TBC)**, Sonia (non-Indigenous, transition only). If Audrey does not confirm, the handover board is 1 Aboriginal director + 1 transition director. "Aboriginal-led" then fails on day one. Need a fallback name and a defined point at which Sonia exits and an Aboriginal majority is locked.
- Nothing on cultural governance: Elders/community advisory, how community authority binds the board, free-prior-informed-consent on major decisions.

### 2. Board make-up
- **No chair, treasurer, or secretary** named (the plan lists these as open questions, unresolved). Decide at 1 June.
- **Three directors is the floor, not a board.** No skills matrix (finance, legal/charity, fundraising, lived experience, manufacturing/enterprise). Stewarding philanthropic funds + a scaling enterprise needs more depth.
- **Related-party conflict is structural and under-addressed.** Ben, Nic, Eloise and Alexandra Savas sit across Goods on Country Pty Ltd (the trading co) *and* drive this charity transition. A DGR charity funnelling philanthropy toward a founder-controlled trading company is exactly what the ACNC scrutinises. The plan flags "related-party and conflict-of-interest safeguards" but does not resolve them. Need: a written, arms-length relationship agreement between Butterfly (charity) and Goods Pty Ltd, a conflicts register, and founders kept off charity votes where conflicted. Note Kristy (Oonchiumpa, a paid Goods partner) carries a related-party conflict too: include, but manage it.
- **Constitution fit not yet confirmed.** Workstream D says "map Goods activities against the charity's objects." Until that is done, it is unconfirmed the constitution even permits Goods' work, which could require a member-approved constitutional amendment (loops back to point 1).

### 3. Other considerations affecting Goods
- **The board is only seeing one of three entities.** The real structure is Goods on Country Pty Ltd (trading) + Butterfly Movement Ltd (charity, this pack) + community-led production entities. The board should see the whole map: who owns what, how money flows, who employs people, who holds the plant and the contracts.
- **Funder flows change with this.** Snow ($395K commitment) and Centrecorp ($388K invoiced) fund "Goods on Country" against project code ACT-GD. If Butterfly becomes the philanthropic home, do grants now route through the charity for DGR? That affects live agreements, acquittals (Snow FY26 acquittal due 31 Jul) and cashflow. Confirm before redirecting any funder.
- **Known entity-cleanliness issues** (separate work): revenue sitting in ACT-ST not ACT-VC, and Goods expenses paid through Nic's personal "NJ Marchesi T/as ACT Everyday" account creating a director-loan/reimbursement question. These touch what the charity can cleanly receive. Mint Ellison was already flagged to advise.
- **Production plant ownership.** The Centrecorp/Snow-funded plant (~85% built): owned by the Pty Ltd, the charity, or a community entity? This is both an asset question and a mission question.

### 4. IP ownership — NOT talked through anywhere in the pack
- **None of the five documents mention intellectual property at all.** No bed designs, production process, plant design, trademarks, or naming rights. For an organisation whose mission is "design for our own obsolescence / hand the keys to community", **this is the most important omission.**
- Open IP questions the board should at least name:
  - Who owns the **Stretch Bed / Basket Bed / washing machine designs**? They were designed *in community with the Bloomfield family*. The Snow report states cultural knowledge and design input are "owned by community" and commits to Indigenous Data Sovereignty, but there is **no legal structure** behind that intent. Intent without an assignment/licence is not ownership.
  - The **Samuel Hafer design consultancy** ($19,500): was IP assigned to Goods? Get the assignment in writing.
  - **Production plant design** (shredder / extruder / CNC bed-component tooling): who owns it?
  - **Trademarks / naming:** "Goods on Country", and "Pakkimjalki Kari" (named by Elder Dianne Stokes in Warumungu, cultural naming rights). Registered? To whom?
  - The **Basket Bed is being open-sourced** (per CLAUDE.md). Open-sourcing gives the design away. Is that a deliberate, consented decision, and does the community whose knowledge is in it agree?
  - **Which entity holds the IP** (Pty Ltd, charity, or community entity) decides whether "community ownership" is real or rhetorical. If the Pty Ltd owns the bed IP, true community ownership requires a licence or assignment to community/Aboriginal-controlled entities. This is a governance + mission decision, not just a legal one.

## Questions to table at 1 June
1. Who are the **members** of Butterfly, and should membership be moved to Aboriginal/community control to make "Aboriginal-led" durable?
2. What is our **definition** of Aboriginal-led (majority directors? chair? reserved seats? Elders body?), and what is the **fallback** if Audrey Deemal does not confirm?
3. Who is **chair / treasurer / secretary**, and what skills are we still missing on the board?
4. What is the **arms-length agreement + conflicts framework** between the charity and Goods Pty Ltd, given the founders sit across both?
5. Does the **constitution's objects** permit Goods' work, or do we need a member-approved amendment?
6. Do **funder flows** (Snow, Centrecorp) re-route through the charity, and what does that do to live acquittals and cashflow?
7. **Who owns the IP** (designs, plant, trademarks, the Pakkimjalki Kari name), and how do we structure it so community ownership is real? Put IP on the agenda.

## Recommended additions before/at the meeting
- A one-page **three-entity structure map** (Pty Ltd / charity / community entities: ownership, money flow, employment, assets, IP) so the board governs the whole, not a fragment.
- An **IP register + ownership decision** as a standing agenda item (with the Bloomfield family / community at the table for the design-IP question).
- A short **Mint Ellison brief** covering: membership control for Aboriginal-led durability, charity-to-trading-co related-party structure, constitutional objects fit, and IP ownership/assignment. These four are the gating legal questions before 26 June.

# Goods on Country — Entity Wording Block
## QBE Diagnostic Area 09 — Legal Structure

> **What this is.** A single, reusable description of how Goods on Country is legally structured today, written to be accurate during a live transition rather than to sound finished. The structure is mid-migration this financial year, so the wording deliberately separates what is settled from what is not.
>
> **How to use it.** Section A is the canonical paragraph and four tailored variants. Once Keith Rovers (MinterEllison) approves the wording, the team can paste the relevant variant verbatim into public pages, investor materials, grant "legal entity" fields and contract preambles. Do not paraphrase the approved version — paraphrasing is how the sole-trader, company and charity roles get blurred again.
>
> **Status discipline.** This is a draft for legal review, not legal advice and not approved copy. Every registration number and date used appears in Section E flagged "to be confirmed by founder/legal". Until that confirmation, treat them as working values, not gospel.

## A. The approved wording block

### A.0 Canonical paragraph (the master version — reuse this verbatim)

> Goods on Country is a social enterprise delivering quality, repairable household goods to remote Indigenous communities. It trades today through a sole trader (Nicholas Marchesi) and is migrating all of its trading activity this financial year into A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd, which will trade as Goods on Country. Tax-deductible philanthropic giving is handled separately through The Butterfly Movement Ltd, a registered charity with deductible-gift-recipient (DGR) status that becomes the charitable home for this work from the 2026–27 financial year; that pathway is not yet live. Goods on Country's longer-term aim is for production to be owned and run by Aboriginal-controlled or locally owned entities, and the legal form for that is still being designed.

*Why these four sentences and not fewer: the structure does three different jobs (trading, charitable/DGR funding, future community-owned production) through three different legal positions. Collapsing them into one sentence is exactly the blur the public-copy rules forbid. Each variant below keeps the same three roles in the same order so the versions stay consistent with each other.*

### A.1 Public website / one line

Two options depending on space. Both are accurate and consistent with A.0. Neither claims DGR for Goods, and neither names a charitable pathway as live.

**One line (footer / about strip):**

> Goods on Country is a social enterprise currently operated by Nicholas Marchesi (sole trader) and is moving its trading to A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd, trading as Goods on Country, during the 2026–27 financial year.

**Short paragraph (about page):**

> Goods on Country designs and delivers repairable household goods with remote communities. We trade today through a sole trader and are moving all of our trading this financial year into A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd, which will trade as Goods on Country. Charitable and tax-deductible giving is handled separately, through a registered charity, and our longer-term goal is for production to be owned and run on Country. Our community-ownership plans are a direction we are working toward, not a completed transfer.

*Public-page note for the team: do not add an ABN or ACN to public pages until Section E is confirmed and legal has approved the contracting-party wording. Do not write "Goods on Country is a registered charity", "DGR", "tax-deductible", or "we transfer ownership to communities" anywhere on the public site.*

### A.2 Investor materials (catalytic / philanthropic investor brief)

> Goods on Country's legal structure is in transition this financial year, and we describe it precisely rather than as finished. The business trades today through a sole trader (Nicholas Marchesi) and is migrating all trading activity into A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd, which will trade as Goods on Country and is the confirmed go-forward operating company. Tax-deductible philanthropic funding flows only through The Butterfly Movement Ltd, a registered charity with Item 1 DGR status that becomes the charitable home for this work from the 2026–27 financial year and is not yet operational for Goods. The structure beneath this — whether Goods becomes a subsidiary, a company limited by guarantee or a hybrid, the legal form of the community-production entities, and the mission-protection mechanism — is still being designed with our lawyers (MinterEllison). We are not asking an investor to accept that the structure is settled; we are showing what is confirmed, what is mid-migration, and what is being decided, so capital can be matched to the right entity at the right time.

*Investor note: keep the "we describe it precisely rather than as finished" framing. For this audience the honesty about the transition is a credibility asset, the same way the evidence labels are in the strategy memo. Do not state that a 51%+ First Nations-owned operating entity exists yet — it is a precondition being worked toward (see Section C), not a current fact.*

### A.3 Grant application — "legal entity / applicant" field

Most grant forms have a single short field for the legal applicant. Use the version that matches what the form is asking. **The applicant entity during the migration is an open legal question (Section C, Q3) — confirm with the founder/lawyer which entity is the correct applicant before submitting any specific grant.**

**If the form asks for the entity that contracts and receives funds today:**

> Nicholas Marchesi, sole trader, trading as Goods on Country (ABN to be confirmed in Section E). Trading activity is migrating during the 2026–27 financial year to A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd (ACN 697 347 676), trading as Goods on Country.

**If the form asks for the go-forward / preferred contracting entity:**

> A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd (ACN 697 347 676), trading as Goods on Country. (Goods is mid-migration this financial year from a sole-trader trading base into this company; please confirm with us which entity should be named as applicant if your timing requires the current trading entity.)

**If the grant is philanthropic and DGR-dependent:**

> Tax-deductible / DGR-eligible giving for this work is received only by The Butterfly Movement Ltd (a registered charity with DGR status), from the 2026–27 financial year. Goods on Country, A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd and the sole trader do not themselves hold DGR status. Please confirm timing with us before relying on a DGR receipt.

*Grant note: never tick "DGR: yes" or "registered charity: yes" against Goods on Country, A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd or the sole trader. DGR is only ever via The Butterfly Movement Ltd, and only from FY2026-27.*

### A.4 Contract / counterparty preamble

Use the recital that matches who is actually signing. **Until the migration completes and seller-of-record / contracting-party questions are resolved (Section C, Q3), confirm with the founder which entity is the correct signatory before executing any contract.**

**Current trading entity as counterparty (today):**

> This agreement is entered into by Nicholas Marchesi (ABN to be confirmed), trading as "Goods on Country" ("Goods"). Goods is migrating its trading operations during the 2026–27 financial year to A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd (ACN 697 347 676), and the parties acknowledge that Goods may, on notice, novate or assign this agreement to A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd as part of that migration.

**Go-forward company as counterparty (once migration of the relevant activity has occurred):**

> This agreement is entered into by A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd (ACN 697 347 676), trading as "Goods on Country" ("Goods").

*Contract note: warranty, product-liability and seller-of-record obligations should sit with whichever entity is named as counterparty in the recital — these need to be unambiguous when money changes hands, and they are an open item for legal (Section C). The Butterfly Movement Ltd is not a party to commercial supply contracts; it is the charitable/DGR vehicle only.*

## C. Open questions for Keith Rovers (MinterEllison)

Each item below is unsettled and is phrased as a decision to confirm. The "why it matters" column is the consequence of getting it wrong.

**C.1 — Go-forward entity form beneath A Curious Tractor.**
Should Goods on Country sit as (a) a trading name of A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd, (b) a separate subsidiary company, (c) a company limited by guarantee, or (d) a hybrid?
*Why it matters:* this determines whether liability, accounts and a future fiduciary board can be cleanly separated from the wider A Curious Tractor activity, which in turn affects investor and lender readiness (the SEFA working-capital line is gated on an independent-majority fiduciary board).

**C.2 — Legal form of the community-production entity.**
What is the right vehicle for on-Country production owned by Aboriginal-controlled or locally owned entities (e.g. ORIC corporation, co-operative, Pty Ltd with community shareholders, licence/franchise to a local entity)?
*Why it matters:* this is the structure that makes "community ownership" real rather than rhetorical. It also intersects with the **Supply Nation 51% precondition** below — the chosen vehicle has to be capable of being 51%+ First Nations-owned.

**C.3 — Contracting party / applicant / seller of record during the migration.**
While trading moves from the sole trader to A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd this FY, which entity is (a) the grant applicant per grant, (b) the seller of record for Stripe and institutional buyer contracts, and (c) the party that gives product warranties and carries product liability — and on what date does each switch?
*Why it matters:* match eligibility and clean contracting. A grant or contract signed by the wrong entity, or an ambiguous seller of record, creates problems for funders' due diligence and for any later novation.

**C.4 — Supply Nation 51% First Nations-ownership precondition.**
What entity structure achieves 51%+ First Nations ownership of the operating entity, and on what timeline?
*Why it matters:* a 51%+ First Nations-owned operating entity is a precondition for Supply Nation certification and roughly four of the eight procurement channels Goods is targeting. The Indigenous Procurement Policy threshold tightens around 1 July 2026, so the timing is live.

**C.5 — Mission lock.**
What mission-protection mechanism is credible and practical (purpose-lock constitution, asset lock, special/reserved rights for a community or Aboriginal-led body, B Corp or equivalent, a community-ownership deed)?
*Why it matters:* there is currently **no formal mission lock** — protection is described by the team as "cultural and relational" only, which the team itself says is "not enough to carry larger capital." A funder or lender providing six-figure catalytic or concessional capital will reasonably ask what stops the purpose drifting; today there is no legal answer.

**C.6 — Donor tax-deductibility timing and the related-party boundary.**
From what date can a donor to this work receive a tax-deductible (DGR) receipt through The Butterfly Movement Ltd, and what related-party agreement governs Butterfly funding or buying from the trading entity?
*Why it matters:* the DGR pathway is not live today, so any "tax-deductible" claim is premature until Butterfly's FY2026-27 handover and related-party safeguards are complete. Getting the boundary wrong creates both a compliance risk for the charity and an overclaim risk in public copy.

## D. Do-not-say checklist (public-copy risk rules — Area 09)

Honour all of these in any externally-facing use. Drawn from the live QBE tracker and the Area 09 review.

- [ ] **Do not** say Goods on Country (or A Curious Tractor, or the sole trader, or A Kind Tractor) is a registered charity, is DGR, or that gifts to it are tax-deductible. DGR is only via The Butterfly Movement Ltd, and only from FY2026-27.
- [ ] **Do not** describe the DGR/charitable pathway as live today. It becomes operational from FY2026-27 (~1 July 2026); donors cannot tax-receipt this work yet.
- [ ] **Do not** blur the three roles — keep "sole trader trades today", "A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd is the go-forward trading company", and "The Butterfly Movement Ltd is the charity/DGR home" as three distinct statements.
- [ ] **Do not** present A Kind Tractor Ltd as the Goods entity, the charity, or a DGR pathway. It is dormant and not used.
- [ ] **Do not** promise community-ownership transfer as complete. It is a direction being worked toward; the legal form is not yet chosen. Use "working toward", not "we transfer".
- [ ] **Do not** state that a 51%+ First Nations-owned operating entity already exists. It is a precondition being designed, not a current fact.
- [ ] **Do not** imply a mature Goods subsidiary, CLG or hybrid exists beneath A Curious Tractor. The sub-entity form is undecided.
- [ ] **Do not** publish an ABN or ACN on public pages, or in a grant/contract field, until Section E is confirmed by founder/legal and the contracting-party wording is approved.
- [ ] **Do not** reuse the legacy code/site phrases "We don't license. We transfer", "ACT is a registered charity", "DGR1 status" (as a Goods claim), or "40% community share" (as if committed). All are stale or overclaimed.

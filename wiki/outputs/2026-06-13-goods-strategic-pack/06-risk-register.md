# Scored risk register

> **What this is.** The working risk picture from the strategy synthesis, converted into a scored register with a defined likelihood × consequence scale, named owners, and a shareability decision on every line. It exists so the same risks get reviewed every month against the same scale, and so we know — before anything leaves the building — which lines are safe to put in front of QBE and which stay with the founders, the accountant, and legal.
>
> **It is not audited.** This is a founder-review draft. The scores are judgement calls anchored to the evidence in the master alignment map, not an independent assessment. Several of the highest-scoring risks concern things that are explicitly *unsettled* — the entity, the QBE match, capital decisions, and the community-production model. Those are marked Internal-only or worded for external use, per the pack's public-copy rule: nothing unresolved gets presented as settled.

---

## How to read this — the scoring key

Each risk is scored on two 1–5 scales. The score is **Likelihood × Consequence** (range 1–25), bucketed into four bands.

**Likelihood (L) — how probable in the next 12 months**

| L | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 1 | Rare — would be a surprise |
| 2 | Unlikely — possible but not expected |
| 3 | Possible — even odds over the period |
| 4 | Likely — expected unless something changes |
| 5 | Almost certain / already happening |

**Consequence (C) — severity if it occurs**

| C | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 1 | Minor — absorbed in normal operations |
| 2 | Moderate — a setback, recoverable in weeks |
| 3 | Serious — sets the plan back a quarter or damages a relationship |
| 4 | Major — threatens the raise, a community relationship, or safety |
| 5 | Severe — physical harm, loss of community trust at scale, or organisation-ending |

**Score bands**

| Band | Score (L×C) | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | 20–25 | Act now; carry to every founder and governance review |
| **High** | 12–19 | Active mitigation with a named owner and a next action |
| **Medium** | 6–11 | Managed; review monthly |
| **Low** | 1–5 | Monitor; review quarterly |

**Shareability** — `QBE-shareable` (safe to put in front of QBE / catalytic funders as worded) or `Internal only` (founder / accountant / legal — concerns an unsettled entity, the QBE match, a capital decision, or the community-ownership model, and must not be presented externally as settled).

**Owner** — single accountable person, even where work is shared. `Added by analysis` marks risks reasoned in beyond the synthesis list.

## Top 5 risks — narrative, mitigation, next action

### 1. Key-person / founder bottleneck (R1, score 20 — Critical)
Two founders hold everything: every buyer, funder, production and community relationship routes back through Ben or Nic. This is not a future risk — it is the current operating state, and it caps how much capital and demand the organisation can absorb. **Mitigation:** the two named priority hires (General Manager and Business Development lead), with interim cover from QBE skilled-volunteering and the 6-session PIN mentoring. **Next action:** build the delegation map — write down what has actually become teachable versus what is still founder-only — and sequence the GM hire to land alongside the capital, so there is someone to absorb it. *Shareable with QBE as a recognised constraint with a credible hiring plan.*

### 2. Story / data consent (R2, score 20 — Critical)
Goods works at the join between household hardware and people's health and dignity, which means it holds raw transcripts, images, names and CRM records. If any of that is used or shared without the person's control, the consequence is severe and not recoverable — it breaks the relationship and the model's foundation. Only two storytellers (Ivy Johnson, Dianne Stokes) are cleared for external use; the rest are consent-pending, and plaintext passwords still sit in Notion. **Mitigation:** consent-led review before any external use, de-identified examples only, and the consent gate run before anything is published. **Next action:** rotate the plaintext passwords out of Notion and lock the consent-cleared list before the pack goes anywhere. *The consent practice is shareable; the specific gaps and the pending list are not.*

### 3. Cashflow (R3, score 16 — High)
About 89% of money in is grant-funded by design, and grant and customer receipts are lumpy — costs routinely land before the cash does, and there is no buffer policy yet. This is the risk most likely to force a bad decision (taking bridging debt, or building inventory the cash cannot support). **Mitigation:** a working-capital model with payment-milestone rules and a defined cash buffer. **Next action:** set the buffer policy and the milestone rules before any inventory build or plant spend, so timing discipline exists before the capital arrives. *Internal-only: cash timing and the missing buffer policy stay with founders and the accountant; the working-capital discipline can be described to QBE.*

### 4. Capital-raise / QBE-match miss (R4, score 16 — High, added by analysis)
The headline ask depends on closing roughly AU$400K of signed, match-eligible capital by 31 August 2026 — and the QBE match is an *output* of money raised first, not an input. Signed match-eligible LOIs today: zero. The synthesis is right that this is "a conversation problem, not a discovery problem," but the deadline is real and the match cap is unconfirmed in writing. **Mitigation:** convert one procurement or offtake LOI — that single signature fixes both the match gate and the weak commercial-paperwork gap — and keep the live commitment register current. **Next action:** drive the highest-probability LOI to signature; if it slips, trigger the "capital late" scenario (slow the plant, keep funded batches moving, avoid bridging debt). *Internal-only: the 0-LOI position, the match mechanics and the $200K/$400K uncertainty must never be presented externally as settled.*

### 5. Legal-structure / entity decision delay (R5, score 16 — High, added by analysis)
The entity question is the keystone gap with zero artifacts: the form beneath ACT and the 51%+ First Nations-owned operating entity are both undecided, and that decision gates Supply Nation certification (the IPP threshold tightens 1 July 2026) and IBA/FAC eligibility — four of eight market pipes depend on it. **Mitigation:** approve one entity-wording block and a decided transition path, with MinterEllison's input. **Next action:** make the Indigenous-ownership-structure decision against the 1-July threshold rather than letting it drift past it. *Internal-only: the entity form is unsettled and must be described as direction, never as decided.*

> **A note on the next tier.** R6 (debt before serviceability), R7 (governance lagging investor needs) and R8 (community-ownership value/risk asymmetry) all score High (15–16) and are tightly coupled to the top five — debt and governance are the same gate, and the community-ownership asymmetry is the mission risk that the whole structure decision has to protect against. Treat the top five and this tier as one connected cluster, not eight separate items.

## The "do no harm" test (the risk frame behind the scores)

Nicholas named the governing test: **do no harm.** Every risk above maps to one of these practical commitments:

- Do not put unsafe products into houses. *(R13)*
- Do not put unsafe equipment into community production. *(R13, R11)*
- Do not use stories in ways people cannot control. *(R2)*
- Do not claim circular economy while creating a new waste problem. *(R9, R14)*
- Do not take debt that forces bad product or bad community decisions. *(R6, R3)*
- Do not let funder timelines override community readiness. *(R10, R11, R4)*
- Do not centralise value in ACT while talking about community ownership. *(R8)*

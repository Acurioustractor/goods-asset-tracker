# 12-month role map and founder-dependency plan

> Goods is founder-led, and honest about it. Two people hold the whole thing today. This page does three things: shows where capacity actually sits right now, names the bottleneck that creates, and lays out the phased hires that remove it — tied to the capital that funds them. Nothing here describes a hire that already exists. Every future role is labelled.

## Where capacity sits today

Goods is not "two people with an idea" — but the load-bearing capacity is two people. The rest is real support that we do not own and cannot task like staff. Keeping that distinction visible is the point of Area 08: founder labour is the engine, and it is currently subsidised rather than properly costed.

| Layer | Who | What they hold | Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Founders (FTE)** | **Ben Knight** | Relationships, sense-making, story and consent, governance, the product-feedback layer (does the bed actually work in a house). Background: Queensland Corrective Services, AIME, youth justice. | Owned capacity — the engine |
| **Founders (FTE)** | **Nicholas Marchesi** | Builder/operator: remote-community delivery, prototype-to-product, repairability, durability, unit economics. Background: Orange Sky, 10+ yrs remote-community work. | Owned capacity — the engine |
| **ACT shared services** | A Curious Tractor | Strategy, comms, finance/admin, tooling, project support — lets Goods move without a standalone team too early. | Shared, not dedicated — hides true cost |
| **Partners** | Oonchiumpa (Alice homelands), Palm Island Community Company conversations, other place-based orgs | Local trust, delivery insight, feedback, future production and repair pathways. | Partner capacity — not owned, not taskable |
| **Advisors** | 11-member advisory group (incl. Kristy Bloomfield, Corey Tutt, Judith Meiklejohn); cultural/industry advisors Fred Campbell, Daniel Pittman; MinterEllison (Keith Rovers) on structure | Challenge, advice, openings, credibility. | Advisory — not fiduciary, not operating capacity |

Two honest notes, because they are the live tracker's Area 08 rules:

- **Founder labour is real and currently subsidised.** Ben and Nic are the only two full-time-equivalent people consistently holding Goods, and they are not yet on fixed Goods salaries in a mature structure. ACT shared services and founder flexibility absorb the gap. Costing that founder time at a fair-market rate is an open P0 (master map §9.3) — until it is done, the economics understate the true cost of running Goods.
- **Partners and advisors are not headcount.** Oonchiumpa, the advisory group, and the cultural advisors extend reach and judgement. They do not run sales, operations, production, or finance, and this plan does not count them as if they did.

## The founder-dependency problem

This is the standing **#1 risk** on the register, and it is flagged as **"already happening,"** not as a future possibility (master map §8). Every buyer conversation, every funder, every production fault, every community relationship and every governance question currently routes back through the same two people.

The closeness is what makes Goods good. It is also the ceiling. Goods cannot become a repeatable product system across remote communities if every relationship, decision, sale, story, and repair depends on Ben or Nic being in the room.

The single-point-of-failure map — six domains, two people:

| Domain | Who holds it today | What breaks if that person is unavailable |
|---|---|---|
| Sales / buyer pathways | Ben + Nic | Warm interest stalls; no one drives LOIs to signature (the match gate stays at $0 signed) |
| Operations / procurement | Nic (+ ACT admin) | Orders, freight, inventory, supplier management lose their only owner |
| Production | Nic | On-Country plant troubleshooting has no backup; scale cannot start |
| Finance / modelling | Ben + Nic (+ ACT) | Cashflow, working capital, unit economics have no dedicated discipline — and debt needs that discipline |
| Community relationships / consent | Ben | Consent and follow-up capacity is the constraint on serving more places safely |
| Governance | Ben + Nic | Advisory ≠ board; decisions concentrate in the founders |

The encouraging signal: production knowledge is already starting to leave founder hands. Nic has moved from teaching himself shredding, pressing, CNC and assembly to lower-skilled operators running parts of the process. That is the direction for everything: turn founder intuition into teachable systems. This plan is how that happens across the other five domains, production included.

## The 12-month role map

Phased, and disciplined: each hire removes a named bottleneck and is triggered by a real-world event (signed capital, order volume), not by a calendar. The first two are GM and Business Development — the two from the deck and diagnostic, funded from the ~AU$200K hires line in the raise. **All roles below are FUTURE — not yet hired.**

### Phase 1 — first two hires (0–6 months)

| Role | Why it removes a bottleneck | Trigger to hire | Funding source | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **General Manager** | Takes day-to-day operating load off both founders so they can hold strategy, community and capital — the single role that unwinds the most concentration across all six domains. | First tranche of signed/match-eligible capital closes (~AU$400K target by 31 Aug) | ~AU$200K hires line (the raise) | **FUTURE — not yet hired** |
| **Business Development / sales lead** | Owns the buyer pipeline end-to-end: target list, pipeline stages, follow-up rhythm in HighLevel/GHL. Directly attacks the $0-signed-LOI gate — turns warm interest into managed pathways without losing the community-ownership vision. | First tranche of capital closes, or buyer pipeline crosses a volume the founders can no longer manage by hand | ~AU$200K hires line (the raise) | **FUTURE — not yet hired** |

### Phase 2 — capacity build-out (6–12 months)

Sequenced after the first two, each gated on its own trigger. GM is deliberately first, not last — the load already justifies it; the rest follow demand.

| Role | Why it removes a bottleneck | Trigger to hire | Funding source | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Operations / procurement lead** | Connects orders, inventory, freight, production, repair and supplier management into repeatable routines with SOPs and a weekly operating rhythm — frees Nic from being the only operations owner. | Sustained order volume past founder-manageable; or first procurement-channel contract signed | Raise + first contract revenue | **FUTURE — not yet hired** |
| **Production lead / trainer** | Lets local operators run the Stretch Bed process safely and consistently (production guide, quality checks, training record, named local process owner) so On-Country production no longer depends on Nic troubleshooting every step. | In-source 50-bed proof run passes and a plant moves toward steady production | Plant capex + raise | **FUTURE — not yet hired** |
| **Finance / CFO support** | Builds the cashflow discipline, working-capital visibility and unit-economics reporting that repayable capital requires — and produces the Goods-only finance pack. Likely fractional/external first. | SEFA concessional-debt line advances (debt needs the discipline before it lands) | Raise (working-capital line) | **FUTURE — not yet hired** |
| **On-Country coordinator roles** | Keep consent, feedback, follow-up and expectation management close to community rather than centralised in Brisbane/ACT — the constraint on serving more places well. Partner-held where possible. | Each new production facility / each step-up in communities served | Funded per facility; grants | **FUTURE — not yet hired** |

**What we are deliberately not building:** a large central organisation just to look serious. Goods grows through local enterprise capability, production and repair roles, procurement discipline, partner capacity, shared ACT infrastructure, and lean governance — not headcount in Brisbane. The point is to make the product system work and shift more power, production and value On-Country.

## Interim mitigation (now → first hire)

Until Phase 1 lands, the bottleneck is managed two ways: borrowed capacity, and reducing how much depends on a founder being present.

**Borrowed capacity (running now):**
- **QBE skilled volunteering** — backfilling specific gaps while the raise closes.
- **PIN mentoring** — a 6-session program, running now, ending ~August. Time-boxed; not a substitute for hired roles.
- **Advisory group (11 named)** — judgement, openings and challenge. Advisory, not operating capacity.
- **Partner capacity (Oonchiumpa and others)** — local delivery and feedback in place. Partner-held, not owned.

**Founder-dependency reductions (lower the dependency itself, not just add hands):**
- **Documented runbooks** — turn founder intuition into teachable SOPs (production guide, buyer pipeline stages, consent process), so context stops living only in founder heads.
- **The QR / asset-register system** — every unit tracked, so delivery, repair and reporting do not depend on founder recall.
- **Shared back-office** — ACT finance/admin/tooling already removes overhead the founders would otherwise carry, with the caveat that this cost must be made visible.

> Note the timing risk: PIN mentoring ends ~August, the same window the first capital tranche is targeted to close (31 Aug). The interim scaffolding is genuinely interim — Phase 1 hiring needs to begin as that support tapers, or the bottleneck reasserts.

## What this unlocks

Removing the founder bottleneck is not overhead on top of the raise. It is part of what the catalytic capital is for.

- The **~AU$200K hires line** in the AU$900K–$1M ask funds the first two roles (GM + BD). They are named here, not as placeholders, so the team slide can carry real roles with real triggers.
- The #1 risk on the register is the founder bottleneck. **De-risking is the explicit purpose of this round** — it sits at the end of "proven product + proven delivery" and the start of "proving the repeatable commercial engine." A venture that runs entirely through two unpaid-by-Goods founders is exactly the concentration catalytic capital exists to relieve. Funding the BD lead is, directly, funding the conversion of warm interest into the first signed dollar that triggers the QBE match.
- It also clears two of the five raise-blockers (master map §9): the **people/governance** readiness gap, and part of the **financial-discipline** gap once finance support lands. Costing founder time at fair-market rate stays a prerequisite — this plan assumes that work is done in parallel, so the economics never quietly drop founder labour.

> The honest version for a funder: "Two people hold everything, and that is the biggest risk we carry. We are not asking you to fund a finished org — we are asking you to fund the first two roles that take the load off the founders, in the order that removes the most concentration first, triggered by signed money rather than ambition. That de-risking is the round."

Dear [confirm contact / salutation],

We are writing ahead of your June board with one request: that Centrecorp's next round of beds for Central Australia be a decision the board can make this month, and that it count as committed support we can build further capital behind.

First, what your backing has already done. Three weeks ago we delivered 87 Stretch Beds across Utopia Homelands over two days, working with the Oonchiumpa Consultancy and the Utopia Council. Thirty-six went straight into households; the rest went to community institutions to place on their own terms. That run kept 1.74 tonnes of plastic out of the community dump. One bed went to Ray Nelson, out on the Plenty Highway, and he told us he is no longer waking up with back pain. That is the whole point of the thing, said by the man sleeping on it. Every bed carries a QR tag, so you can follow each one in the register rather than take our word for it. This is the delivery your support set up, and the evidence base we promised to bring back to you.

We will be straight about where the wider business sits, because Centrecorp has earned that. Goods is about 89% grant-funded today, by design. We have proven communities want the bed and that funders will back deployments. What we have not yet proven is that we can make the beds cheaply enough to stand on their own. The next step is a 50-bed run made end to end, costed at about AU$60,000 to $80,000, that turns our central cost claim from modelled to measured.

So, the ask. We would like Centrecorp to commit to the next round of Stretch Beds for Aboriginal families and young people in Central Australia, [confirm next-round amount / bed count; the consolidated board package proposes ~130 beds]. As before, this is restricted to Central Australian benefit: beds delivered with local partners, allocated transparently, with photos, counts and feedback reported back to you. No part of it funds product development or work outside Central Australia.

Two things make the timing matter. Your board meets this month, and a clear decision then lets us plan delivery properly. A committed contribution from Centrecorp would also count as match-eligible external capital under QBE's Catalysing Impact program, which matches the capital we raise and so stretches the reach of your dollar, provided a commitment is in place before 31 August 2026. Today we hold none. The real ask, then, is a board decision in the next few weeks.

We are gathering everything the board asked for into one package, built on the work you have already funded. Could we find twenty minutes before the board to walk you through it and answer anything? We would rather you saw the honest version first.

With real respect,

Ben Knight & Nicholas Marchesi
Goods on Country

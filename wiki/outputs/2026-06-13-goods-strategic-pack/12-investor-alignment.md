# Investor alignment — Goods on Country

> **What this is.** The CASE Smart Impact Capital Investor Alignment Tool, filled in with Goods' real pipeline. It does two jobs: states Goods' own filter (the six knockout criteria plus ten weighted fit criteria — Tab 1), then scores every funder in the pipeline against that filter (Tab 3). The output is a defensible shortlist of who is worth the founders' time for the first ~AU$400K of the blended raise.
>
> **The discipline.** Beck Parkinson's rule runs through this: *"a quick 'no' is much more helpful than a long drawn-out process."* The tool exists to drop mismatches fast, not to manufacture precision. Every rating below traces to a wiki investor profile. Where a profile does not state a position, the cell reads **Don't Know** — that is an honest gap, not a guess. The numbers (n/3, n/4, n/10) are a triage aid, not a probability of funding.

## Section A — Our Needs (Goods' filter)

This is Tab 1 of the CASE tool: the criteria on which Goods judges every potential investor. The six knockout criteria are pass/fail — a single fail removes an investor from the pipeline. The ten fit criteria are negotiable, each carried at a High, Medium, or Low priority.

### The six knockout criteria — Goods' position

| # | Knockout criterion | Goods' hard requirement |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Investment Geography** | Invests in remote Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities / remote Australia (NT, QLD, WA, SA). National funders qualify; funders mandated to other geographies do not. |
| 2 | **Legal Structure** | Currently trades as Nicholas Marchesi sole trader (ABN 21 591 780 066), migrating all Goods activity into **A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd** (ACN 697 347 676, t/a Goods on Country) this FY; DGR pathway via **The Butterfly Movement Ltd**, operational FY2026-27. The subsidiary / CLG / hybrid form is the unsettled keystone. Investor must be able to fund a for-profit Pty Ltd and/or a not-for-profit/DGR vehicle, or a hybrid; **equity into the eventual structure is complex and not the 2026 need.** |
| 3 | **Investment Size** | The first ~AU$400K of signed capital, sitting inside a **AU$900K–1M blended raise**. Tranche-dependent: grant tickets ~AU$50–200K, concessional debt ~AU$300K, IBA blend up to AU$5M as a later anchor. Funders whose typical cheque is far above or far below the relevant tranche fail. |
| 4 | **Return Expectations** | Concessional / social-first. Non-equity. Repayable preferred by the QBE match rule, but grants welcome. 5–10 year horizon; the eventual "exit" is **community ownership of production**, not an investor liquidity event. |
| 5 | **Capital Type** | Grants + concessional / recoverable / patient debt. **No conventional equity in 2026.** Equity is revisited only inside a future model where communities themselves become the equity holders. |
| 6 | **Stage of Growth** | Early commercial — post-prototype, proven delivery (496 beds across 9 communities, tracked), pre-revenue commercial engine. This is a **de-risking round, not a growth round** (~89% grant-funded by design). Not seed; not growth-stage scale capital. |

> **How a knockout fail shows up in the tool.** A funder fails a knockout when they are *Not Aligned* (or unknown) on that criterion. In the CASE logic the "Knockout pass?" result is "Yes" only when the venture is Somewhat- or Very-Aligned on **all six**. One *Not Aligned* knockout flips the result to "No".

### The ten fit criteria — Goods' priority and why

Priority is Goods' call on how much each fit criterion matters right now. The CASE tool counts the High-priority fits separately (the n/4 column) and all fits together (the n/10 column).

| Fit criterion | Priority | Why this priority for Goods |
|---|---|---|
| **Social Mission** (understands and supports our mission) | **High** | The mission — washable, repairable goods that prevent disease, built *with* communities, moving toward community ownership — is the whole proposition. A funder who does not get it will misread every later decision. |
| **Company Vision** (understands and supports our vision) | **High** | The vision is to *become unnecessary* — local people making and owning the goods. Funders chasing a scaling-equity story or a perpetual-charity story both misfit; we need backers who hold the "design ourselves out of the job" endgame. |
| **Governance** (their governance expectations match ours) | **Medium** | Goods has an advisory board, not yet a fiduciary one. A debt funder requiring an independent-majority board (SEFA) is a real gate; a grant funder comfortable with an advisory group is not. Medium because it is solvable, and the board build is already on the critical path. |
| **Restrictions** (use-of-funds restrictions work for us) | **Medium** | Some of the warmest money is geography-restricted (Centrecorp Trust-Deed-bound to Central Australia; NT-restricted Snow rounds). Restrictions are workable — Goods deploys in those places anyway — but they shape what each dollar can fund (e.g. not the in-source R&D run). |
| **Impact Evidence** (we can provide the evidence expected) | **Medium** | Goods has strong delivery evidence (tracked register, consented stories) but the MEL framework and longitudinal outcomes are draft. A funder demanding mature impact metrics today is a stretch; most catalytic funders accept the de-risking framing. |
| **Connections** (able and willing to make useful introductions) | **High** | The raise is "a conversation problem, not a discovery problem." A funder who opens doors to procurement, government, finance, and governance expertise is worth more than the cheque alone. The first signature's signalling value (Snow → the rest of the stack) lives here. |
| **Geography experience** (experience in our locations) | **Medium** | Remote-Australia knowledge means a funder will not panic at slow timelines and freight realities. Useful, not decisive — a national funder willing to learn properly still qualifies. |
| **Industry experience** (experience in our market segment) | **Low** | The market — remote household goods, recycled-plastic manufacture, Indigenous procurement — is narrow; very few funders have direct sector experience, so we do not weight it heavily. WINYA-style precedent matters more than a funder's own portfolio. |
| **Business-challenge experience** (experience with the specific issues we face) | **Low** | Helpful if a funder has backed early-commercial social enterprises through the prove-the-engine stage, but not a filter we can afford to apply strictly given the pipeline. |
| **Impact on future investments** (effect on our future raises) | **Medium** | A marquee catalytic name (Snow, PFI, IBA) de-risks the *next* raise and the community-ownership transition. Negative-signal risk is low here; upside is real, hence Medium. |

**Priority summary:** High = Social Mission, Company Vision, Connections (3 High-priority fits → the n/3 column). Medium = Governance, Restrictions, Impact Evidence, Geography experience, Impact on future investments. Low = Industry experience, Business-challenge experience.

> Note on the High-priority count. The CASE worked example treats four criteria as High; Goods carries **three** (Social Mission, Company Vision, Connections), so the High-priority fit column reads "n/3" for Goods. The populated workbook computes this automatically from the priority row.

## Section C — Ranked shortlist for the first ~AU$400K

Best-fit targets for the first ~AU$400K of signed, match-eligible capital, in priority order. The ranking weights: knockout pass, High-priority fit, relationship warmth, and — decisively — proximity to a **signed commitment before 31 August 2026**, because the QBE match is an output of money raised first and signed eligible LOIs today are **zero**. This is "a conversation problem, not a discovery problem", so warm relationships that can convert fast rank above colder perfect-fits.

1. **Snow Foundation — the first signature.** Perfect fit (10/10, all knockouts pass), the deepest relationship (~AU$493K since 2023, advisory-group member), and the Jigsaw co-investment precedent means Snow's signature changes the room. The ask (note 08, R4/R5 ~AU$130–200K, NT-restricted fine) is framed precisely to make Snow the first match-eligible commitment that unlocks the rest of the stack. **Highest priority because it is the unlock, not just a good fit.**

2. **Centrecorp Foundation — the nearest board decision.** Strong fit (8/10, knockouts pass), an active buyer-to-capital relationship, and — critically — a **board meeting this month** (~26 June). The next-round beds commitment (~130 beds, ~AU$106K, note 09) can become signed, match-eligible external capital on a near-term, known date. Trust-Deed-bound to Central Australia, so the framing stays forward-beds-only with the QBE match named as a multiplier. **Ranked second purely on timing — the soonest realistic signature.**

3. **SEFA — the senior concessional base.** Top fit (10/10, knockouts pass) and the natural senior debt at the base of the blend (~AU$300K), with a syndicate-agent option (the Jigsaw model). Ranked third for the first AU$400K because it is slower and gated: SEFA's three covenants (completed Goods-only financial model, independent-majority fiduciary board, serviceable revenue covenant) are not yet met. The note (10) opens the conversation honestly now so the facility is ready when the gates clear. **A near-term conversation, a medium-term signature.**

4. **PFI (Paul Ramsay Foundation) — the catalytic recoverable layer.** Top fit (10/10), EOI already in, AU$640K recoverable-grant target. Ranked fourth because the ball is in their court (awaiting feedback) and the timeline is theirs, not ours. Confirm the figure still stands and that they will open doors (they are rated only *Somewhat* on Connections — the one soft spot). **High value, founder-can't-accelerate.**

5. **IBA — the later senior anchor.** Top fit (10/10), eligibility confirmed, up to AU$5M. Deliberately *not* in the first-AU$400K tranche: it is the large later anchor, gated on the business plan and — harder — the entity/Indigenous-ownership decision. Progress the business plan in parallel; do not let it set the pace of the match. **The big anchor for the full raise, not the opening move.**

**Bench (keep warm, do not lead with):** First Australians Capital (10/10 — strong, but research-stage; pursue via an IBA/SIH intro as an IBA complement); FRRR (10/10) / VFFF (9/10) — received funders, coordinate a second ask with the QBE match process; TFN (10/10 — best used as a connector to new funders, not a follow-on cheque). **Confirm-then-consider:** Minderoo, Tim Fairfax, AMP — each has a Don't-Know knockout to resolve before serious outreach. **Out:** QBE Ventures (knockout fail, 2027); Dusseldorp (not a Goods funder).

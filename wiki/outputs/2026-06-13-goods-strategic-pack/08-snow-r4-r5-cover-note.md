# Cover note: Snow Foundation, Round 4/5

> DRAFT for founder review. Placeholders are in `[square brackets]`. The actual send is Ben's to make.

---

Dear [confirm contact / salutation],

We are writing to ask Snow to do something specific, and to be the first to do it.

First, thank you. You backed Goods since 2023, through every awkward middle stage. Most of what we can now show a room full of funders, we can show because you were patient first. Sally on our advisory group has meant we hear the hard questions early.

Here is the freshest of it. Three weeks ago we delivered 87 beds to Utopia Homelands over two days, with the Oonchiumpa Consultancy and the Utopia Council. That run put a washable bed under 36 households and kept 1.74 tonnes of plastic out of the community dump. One bed went to Ray Nelson, out on the Plenty Highway. He told us he is no longer waking up with back pain. That is the whole point of the thing, said by the man sleeping on it. We now have 496 beds across 9 communities, every one tagged and trackable in a register you can scan.

We will be straight about where the business sits, because you have earned that. Goods is about 89% grant-funded today, by design. We have proven communities want the bed and that funders will pay for deployments. We have not proven we can make it cheaply enough to stand on its own. The lever is making the recycled-plastic legs ourselves instead of buying them in at an 8.6 times markup. We have modelled what that does to the cost. We have not yet run it. That is the gap we are asking you to help close.

So, the ask. We are opening a Round 4/5 conversation for a new commitment of around **AU$130,000 to $200,000**, separate from your existing support. NT-restricted is completely fine. That money does two things. It co-funds the AU$60,000 to $80,000 in-source production run, fifty beds made end to end on country, that turns our central cost claim from modelled to measured. And it anchors a blended raise behind it.

The second part is where timing bites. QBE's Catalysing Impact program will match the external capital we raise, but the match only releases once a signed, eligible commitment is on the table, and it has to be signed before 31 August 2026. Today we hold zero. Our warmest funders are watching each other, waiting for the first to move. You co-invested in Jigsaw alongside the SEFA syndicate, so you know how the first signature changes the room. We are asking Snow to be that first signature. So the real ask is a decision in the next few weeks, not a grant by August.

Could we find twenty minutes in the next fortnight to talk it through? We will bring the one-pager and the full deck, and answer anything. No pressure on the number in that call. We would rather you saw the honest version first.

With real gratitude,

Ben Knight & Nicholas Marchesi
Goods on Country

# Strategic plan on a page

> This is the founder-authored half of Area 05. The scored risk register — likelihood × consequence, owners, shareability — sits in `06-risk-register.md` and is not repeated here. Read the two together.

## Where we are

We have proven the product and proven we can deliver it. 496 beds are tracked in the asset register across 9 communities [VERIFIED, 29 May 2026], the Stretch Bed design holds up in real remote conditions, and we have standing demand we cannot yet fill. What we have not proven is that this can pay for itself. Commercial revenue to date is AU$90 across three shop orders [WORKPAPER] — proof that the pipe works, not proof of a market. Roughly 89% of money in has been grant-funded, by design: this is a de-risking round, not a growth round. The central economic claim — that in-sourcing the recycled-HDPE legs drops our marginal cost from AU$684.79 to AU$425.74 [both WORKPAPER/MODELLED] — is proven as a process (40 beds made in-house at the farm, Maningrida INV-0303) but not yet measured at volume, so the cost figure stays modelled. And we have AU$0 of signed, match-eligible capital today. So the honest position is: strong on product and delivery, untested on unit economics, and standing at the start line on capital. Everything below is built to close those three gaps in order.

## The 3-year strategy

The strategy is three phases. Each one earns the right to the next; we do not start a phase before the prior phase's proof is in hand.

**Phase 1 — De-risk (next ~12 months).** Turn the four open questions into answered ones. Run the 50-bed in-source production run (~AU$60–80K) that converts AU$425.74 from modelled to measured. Close the first ~AU$400K of signed, match-eligible capital so the QBE match has something to match against. Get an accountant carve-out so there is one Goods-only revenue figure an accountant will stand behind. Decide the legal entity and the First Nations-ownership structure. Nothing in Phase 2 is committed until these land.

**Phase 2 — Prove the repeatable engine.** Move institutional buyers from grant-funded pilots to procurement orders — the same bed, bought because it works and the paperwork clears, not because it was donated. Stand up the first community-employed operator, so production is no longer two founders. This is where "we delivered beds" becomes "we run a business that delivers beds repeatedly," which is the thing capital is actually backing.

**Phase 3 — Scale toward community-owned production.** Scope the first Aboriginal-controlled operating entity by end FY2026-27, and begin distributing on-country plants so production sits where the demand and the materials are. The endgame is to make ourselves unnecessary — local people making, owning, and repairing the goods. Phase 3 is a direction we build toward, not a date we have set; the entity form and the ownership mechanism (the profit-share principle is settled, the mechanism is not) are resolved in Phase 1 before any of this is built.

## Scenario plan

These are the decisive forks, each tied to the raise. For each: the trigger we watch for, and the move we make. They are pre-agreed so the decision is not made under pressure.

| Fork | Trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 50-bed run — **PASS** | In-source run confirms AU$425.74 (or close) | Scale the raise on the proven number; proceed toward the first distributed plant; tell funders the unit economics are measured, not modelled. |
| 50-bed run — **MISS** | Run lands materially above AU$425.74 | Re-base the entire ask honestly at AU$684.79; redo breakeven; do not present the in-source cost as proven; reset funder expectations before, not after. |
| QBE match — **locks** | First ~AU$400K signed and verified by SIH at the Sept 2026 application | Draw the match (up to AU$400K, ≥1:1, repayable preferred); deploy to plant capex + working-capital buffer + GM/BD hires per the use-of-funds plan. |
| QBE match — **does not lock** | Match not legally binding by the Sept 2026 application | Pursue the conditional-grant path SIH allows; keep converting the warmest LOI to signature; do not assume the match in any forward plan until it is verified. |
| Capital comes in **slow** | Signed commitments arrive behind the August timeline | **Defer the next plant build.** Keep smaller funded batches moving, protect community trust, and do not take bridging debt just to hold momentum (debt only against matched demand). |
| Capital comes in **strong** | Stack closes at or above target | Proceed with the plant build — but resist central over-hiring; direct money to inventory, production readiness, local roles, finance controls, and repair/service loops. |
| Cash is tight | Runway pressure with capital not yet landed | **Compress geographic scope.** Concentrate on the deepest, highest-readiness relationships (Palm Island, Tennant Creek, Alice Homelands) rather than spreading thin; pause new-community expansion until cash and the proven cost support it. |

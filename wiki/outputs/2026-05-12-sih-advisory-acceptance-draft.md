> **Subject:** Accepting the Priority Advisory Project: production cost sensitivity at scale

> Hi Sarah,

> Thank you, and the team, for the diagnostic. We have read it carefully and discussed it together. It is sharper, more useful and more actionable than we hoped. It has already changed our priorities and improved what is on our website (see below).

> We would like to formally accept the **Priority Advisory Project** flagged in Recommendation #2: detailed production cost estimates with sensitivity analysis at different scales.

> A short framing of what we hope to get out of it, so the advisor has the context to be effective:

> **Why this project matters for us right now**
>
> The production cost model on our /impact page is built from a stage-by-stage breakdown anchored on current production-run data. It is honest at small N (we have now labelled it MODELLED following your feedback) but cannot defend the per-unit economics we will need to articulate to SEFA, IBA and QBE Stage 2 investors. The capital stack we are designing assumes per-unit cost decreases as production volume rises, and we have no rigorous sensitivity analysis behind that assumption.

> **What we hope the advisor will produce**
>
> 1. A validated bill-of-materials and per-stage labour model for the Stretch Bed at three production volumes:
>    - Current (~15 beds/month, campaign-based)
>    - Year 1 target (~125 beds/month, 1,500/year)
>    - Year 3 target (~417 beds/month, 5,000/year)
> 2. Sensitivity to ±20% labour cost, ±20% materials cost, and to two operating models (on-Country containerised production vs centralised production + warehousing).
> 3. A view on which stages have the biggest sensitivity (we think CNC time, but want it tested).
> 4. A recommendation on where to invest engineering/tooling effort to reduce per-unit cost most efficiently.

> **What we will provide**
>
> - Stage-by-stage cost data from current production runs
> - Plant capex schedule and depreciation assumptions
> - Container-deployment timing for plants 2 and 3
> - Founder availability for working sessions and clarifications
> - Access to Defy Design (our industrial design partner) for technical validation

> **What we are doing in parallel**
>
> - Building the broader GOC-only financial model (your Recommendation #1, HIGH 0-2mo). Sensitivity outputs from this advisory project plug directly into Week 5-6 of that build. Internal scope at `wiki/outputs/2026-05-12-financial-model-scope.md` and Week 1-2 operational checklist at `wiki/outputs/2026-05-12-financial-model-week1-kickoff.md`.
> - We have already shipped the /impact page rewrite addressing the aspirational-as-active language you flagged. Every metric now carries a provenance tag (verified, modelled, partial, estimated, design target). Live at goodsoncountry.com/impact.
> - We have adopted a formal AI-human-in-loop policy for all external materials, also in response to your feedback.

> **Timing and logistics**
>
> Could you let us know:
>
> 1. **Project advisor**: who you have in mind, their background, and how to reach them.
> 2. **Project format**: number of sessions, duration, ways of working (in-person, video, async).
> 3. **Timing**: ideal completion window. From our side, having a v0.1 output by end of June 2026 would let us feed it into our investment memo by 30 June, which is our internal target.
> 4. **Inputs you would like from us in advance**: any document pre-reads, data templates, or briefings you would like before the advisor starts.

> Thank you again. We will treat this advisor's input as the production-cost validation layer underneath everything else we are about to build.

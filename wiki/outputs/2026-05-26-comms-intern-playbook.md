# Goods on Country — Comms volunteer & intern playbook

> **Date:** 2026-05-26
> **Why this exists:** Benjamin's point. The small pieces of comms work become mini-projects owned by volunteers / marketing interns. Ben is the visionary; interns execute under this guide; Ben verifies before anything goes out. A guide like this lets a constant stream of trusted-and-verified interns boost output and follow-through without diluting the voice.
> **How to use it:** Read top to bottom once. Then pick a mini-project from the catalogue, follow its brief, and run the verify checklist before anything is published or sent.

---

## 1. The rule that beats all the others: meet people in THEIR medium

Benjamin again: where this work goes wrong is the creator imposes their medium on the audience. You build a beautiful membership site, pile great content on it, then spam people to "come join", and nobody logs in. The same content delivered as a Facebook group or a one-way WhatsApp broadcast would have worked. Same message, wrong delivery.

**So: getting the message and the medium to the market, then learning how to convert, is most of the game.** Before making anything, ask: where does this audience ALREADY spend attention? Deliver there. Do not make them come to us.

### Audience → medium map (start here every time)

| Audience | Where they actually are | Deliver as | Do NOT |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Community / families (on Country)** | In person, phones, WhatsApp, printed things they can hold | Photos texted back, WhatsApp, printed copies on the next trip, a book | Expect them to open a website URL |
| **Oonchiumpa** | Their own grant apps, their socials, their meetings | Grabbable clips + photo sets + framing they drop into THEIR channels | Make them link out to our site |
| **Centrecorp board** | Email, board meetings, PDFs | Trustee one-pager (PDF), a short board video, a co-brandable asset | Send them a scroll-heavy web page |
| **Grassroots / public supporters** | Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp | Social posts, a WhatsApp broadcast, a Facebook group, short video | Assume an email list is enough |
| **Funders / institutional buyers** | Email, LinkedIn, the web | `/get-involved`, `/partner`, email, a deck | Rely on social alone |

**What this changes about what we just built:** the website + email drip is the right medium for the **funder / institutional** audience (they live there). For the **grassroots / community-facing** audience, the email "nurture" drip is probably NOT the strongest medium. The same nurture beats (`/the-work` content, the N1 to N5 emails) should be re-cut for **WhatsApp broadcast and/or a Facebook group** where that audience already is. The content is written once; the delivery is chosen per audience. That re-cut is itself a mini-project (below).

## 2. Brand voice (the hard rules, non-negotiable)

These are zero-tolerance. An intern's work gets bounced at verify if any are broken.

- **No em dashes.** Ever. Use colons, full stops, commas, parentheses.
- **"On Country" / "On-Country" is always capitalised.** It is a proper noun.
- **Never "co-design".** We design IN community, WITH community, FOR community. Goods supports the build and the realising. The community runs and owns the plant.
- **Warm, grounded, community-first.** Lead with impact, not charity. Centre Indigenous voices and agency. Use real community language where it is ours to use.
- **Consent first.** Anything marked "consent pending" does not get published or sent externally until consent is confirmed with the person and Oonchiumpa. Names, faces, quotes, video: confirm before use.
- **No invented numbers.** Every stat traces to `v2/src/lib/data/products.ts`, the compendium, or a verified source. If you cannot source it, do not write it.

The canonical facts (memorise the ones you use): 400+ Stretch Beds since 2023; 107 on the May 2026 Utopia trip; $560 institutional / $600 retail; recycled HDPE plastic legs + galvanised steel poles + Australian canvas; 26kg, holds 200kg, ~5 min to assemble; production plant ~85% built, moving to community ownership.

## 3. What we are trying to do (so you make the right call when the brief is silent)

- **Two outward audiences, two jobs.** Supporters/funders need to be CONVERTED (clear asks, lead capture). Public/learners need to be NURTURED (context, belonging, no hard sell). Do not mix the two in one piece.
- **First audiences (community, Oonchiumpa, Centrecorp) come first and own their story.** Our job is to repackage the trip into TOOLS that serve THEIR objectives, in THEIR medium. Multiply the work, do not hoard it on our site.
- **The long story is the source, not the delivery.** The field-notes page is the rich archive. For everyone else, the story is fed in small pieces through the right channel, one beat at a time. Less per touch.

## 4. How we work: visionary + interns + verify

- **Ben is the visionary** for each mini-project. He sets the intent and the standard.
- **The intern owns the execution** of one mini-project end to end. Ownership means you chase it to done, not just do a task.
- **Nothing goes out without Ben's verify.** Trust and verify. You draft, Ben checks against the brand rules + consent + facts, then it ships. Build the verify step into your timeline, do not bolt it on at the end.
- **When unsure, ask early, not late.** A two-line question before you build beats a finished thing that misses intent.

## 5. The mini-project catalogue

Each is small, ownable, and has a clear "done". Pick one. Use the brief template in section 6 to flesh it out, then run the verify checklist.

1. **Oonchiumpa grant clip** — a 90-second cut of Mykel / Fred that Oonchiumpa drops straight into a grant application. *Medium: a file they can upload. Done: clip exported, captioned, consent confirmed, handed to Oonchiumpa.*
2. **Centrecorp trustee one-pager refresh** — keep the Utopia outcomes one-pager current for the board. *Medium: PDF + email. Done: PDF updated, facts verified, no invented figures.*
3. **Centrecorp board video** — a 2 to 3 minute cut for a board meeting. *Medium: a file / private link. Done: cut, consent confirmed, sent.*
4. **Public WhatsApp broadcast** — re-cut the nurture beats (N1 to N5) as short one-way WhatsApp messages for the grassroots audience. *Medium: WhatsApp broadcast list. Done: 5 short messages drafted in voice, schedule agreed.*
5. **Instagram photo set** — a clean, consent-cleared set from the Utopia trip for socials. *Medium: Instagram. Done: 6 to 10 images selected, captions in voice, consent confirmed.*
6. **Facebook group seed** — stand up a "follow the build" Facebook group and seed it with the first few story beats. *Medium: Facebook group. Done: group live, first 3 posts up, link added to /the-work.*
7. **Community print pack** — printed copies / a small book of the trip for families, to go back out on the next trip. *Medium: print. Done: layout ready, consent confirmed, print quote obtained.*
8. **Field-notes follow-the-build broadcast** — convert the converter beats (C1 to C6) into a sequenced WhatsApp option for people who prefer it over email. *Medium: WhatsApp. Done: sequence drafted, opt-in path agreed.*
9. **Partner kit upkeep** — keep `/kit` current as new assets land. *Medium: the web page. Done: new assets linked, dead links fixed.*

Each new piece of comms work should be added here as a mini-project so the catalogue stays the backlog interns pull from.

## 6. The brief template (makes any mini-project intern-ready)

Copy this for each project. It mirrors how we scope work so nothing starts fuzzy:

```
Project: <name>
Visionary: Ben
Owner (intern): <name>
Goal: <one sentence — what success looks like>
Audience: <who> · Medium: <where they already are>
Inputs: <files, links, who to talk to>
Brand notes: <voice rules that matter most here + any consent items>
Definition of done: <concrete, checkable>
Verify gate: Ben signs off against brand + consent + facts BEFORE it goes out
Fallback: <what to do if blocked — e.g. "if consent not confirmed, hold and flag", never guess>
```

## 7. Verify checklist (run before anything publishes or sends)

- [ ] Zero em dashes.
- [ ] "On Country" capitalised everywhere.
- [ ] No "co-design" or charity-framing language.
- [ ] Every stat is sourced. No invented numbers.
- [ ] Consent confirmed for every name, face, and quote used. Nothing "consent pending" is going out.
- [ ] Delivered in the audience's medium, not ours.
- [ ] Ben has seen it and signed off.

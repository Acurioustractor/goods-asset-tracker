# QBE diagnostic artifact builder

## Diagnostic summary

The SIH / QBE diagnostic says Goods has a strong mission, strong community trust, strong product proof, and strong founder articulation. The gap is not the work itself. The gap is the artifact layer: the written, source-backed material that lets QBE, SIH, PIN, funders, lenders, buyers and advisors understand the real operating system without needing the full founder conversation.

The diagnostic's strongest message:

- The verbal explanation was richer than the written collateral.
- Some materials blurred live metrics with future or aspirational metrics.
- Impact investment readiness now depends on clean finance, cost proof, governance, legal structure, risk, market sizing and founder-authored investor materials.
- Stage 2 will need evidence of matched funding, supporting materials, impact proof, corporate/governance documents and accountant-endorsed financials.

Use this page to build the artifact pack area by area. Every claim should be labelled:

- `verified`: source-backed now.
- `modelled`: based on an explicit model or assumption.
- `target`: intended future state with a plan.
- `future`: not true yet.
- `internal only`: useful for thinking, not external.

## Artifact stack

For each diagnostic area, build two layers:

1. **Evidence artifact** - proves what exists now.
2. **Decision artifact** - names the choice, gap, risk or next build.

The goal is not to make Goods look finished. The goal is to show disciplined progress and make the gaps legible.

## Area-by-area builder

### 1. Vision & Ambition

Diagnostic read: vision is a strength. The missing piece is mapping the relationship between Goods, ACT, the future entity model and community-controlled production.

What exists:

- Strong public product proof on the Stretch Bed and process pages.
- Canonical product truth in `v2/src/lib/data/products.ts`.
- Goods HQ and the QBE Readiness Hub now show the operating context.
- Long-term community production pathway is present but still needs a clean diagram.

Build:

- **Evidence artifact:** "Goods in one page" - founder-authored, plain language, product proof first.
- **Decision artifact:** operating model diagram showing ACT, Goods, Butterfly, Oonchiumpa/production entities, IP, capital and benefit flow.

Do not overclaim:

- Do not say the future ownership model is already settled.
- Do not lead with future fridges. Lead with the Stretch Bed.

### 2. Social Objective & Impact

Diagnostic read: problem definition is strong, measurement needs a clearer logic chain and a shortlist of priority metrics.

What exists:

- `/impact` with metrics tagged live/modelled/estimated.
- Asset register and QR-linked bed records.
- Empathy Ledger and consent-led storytelling.
- Impact Claim Map in Notion.

Build:

- **Evidence artifact:** impact claim map with every claim labelled `verified`, `modelled`, `target`, `future` or `internal only`.
- **Decision artifact:** Theory of Change diagram: inputs -> activities -> outputs -> short-term outcomes -> long-term impact.
- **Decision artifact:** priority metric shortlist for three audiences: community, funders/investors, founders.

Do not overclaim:

- Do not claim measured health outcomes yet.
- Say products are designed to support sleep, hygiene, dignity and household wellbeing.

### 3. Business Model

Diagnostic read: demand channels are validated, but the transition to product sales, standing inventory and clear customer capacity-to-pay needs sharper packaging.

What exists:

- Stretch Bed ecommerce and Stripe buy-now.
- Institutional/donor-funded proof through Centrecorp.
- `/admin/deals`, GHL pipeline, partner/funder pages.
- Corrected cost model and Bed COGS page.

Build:

- **Evidence artifact:** buyer segment map with four lanes: community organisations, government/housing/homelands, NDIS/aged-care, disaster/temporary accommodation.
- **Evidence artifact:** Stretch Bed buyer sheet with product, price, freight, warranty, support, QR reporting and contracting party.
- **Decision artifact:** product-sales transition plan from batch-funded delivery to standing inventory.

Do not overclaim:

- Do not imply standing inventory exists yet.
- Do not treat all demand as signed orders.

### 4. Financial Management

Diagnostic read: this is a high-priority gap. Goods needs a GOC-only model, founder time costed, 3-year forecast and investor-readable financials.

What exists:

- Financial model v0.1, cashflow, scenarios, buffer policy and capital stack.
- Corrected COGS ladder: `$469.79` direct materials, `~$525.74` with assembly before route freight, `~$600` fully loaded at low volume.
- Xero reconciliation and admin finance surfaces.

Build:

- **Evidence artifact:** financial summary pack: revenue, COGS, cashflow, scenarios, capital stack, current caveats.
- **Decision artifact:** founder FTE and fair-market costing sheet.
- **Decision artifact:** GOC-only 3-statement model and accountant review checklist.

Do not overclaim:

- Do not use the old `$149` or `$400` COGS language externally.
- Do not present modelled scale economics as current economics.

### 5. Strategic Planning & Risk

Diagnostic read: strategy is adaptive and real, but investors need a short strategic narrative, explicit scenario planning and a formal risk register.

What exists:

- Buffer policy and three-scenario model.
- Risk philosophy: do no harm and avoid creating more landfill.
- QBE documentation deep dive and master register.

Build:

- **Evidence artifact:** 1-page strategy narrative: where Goods is now, next 12 months, September QBE gate, 31 August LOI gate.
- **Decision artifact:** formal risk register covering strategic, operational, financial, compliance, environmental, cultural/data sovereignty and product liability.
- **Decision artifact:** scenario response table: what changes if funding is delayed, partial or successful.

Do not overclaim:

- Do not frame adaptive strategy as lack of planning. Explain it as community-responsive planning plus explicit risk controls.

### 6. Process & Technology

Diagnostic read: process and technology are a relative strength, but founder dependency and SOP documentation need work.

What exists:

- Website, admin, asset register, QR flow, Stripe, GHL, Empathy Ledger, Notion/wiki, fleet tooling.

- Public `/wiki` and internal wiki have the knowledge base.

Build:

- **Evidence artifact:** operating systems map showing website, admin, Supabase, GHL, Stripe, EL, Notion/wiki, Xero/Dext and QR flow.
- **Decision artifact:** SOP index: which procedures exist, which are drafts, which are missing.
- **Decision artifact:** founder-dependency reduction plan.

Do not overclaim:

- Fleet telemetry is still a gap. Name it honestly if washing machines come up.

### 7. Governance, Data & Reporting

Diagnostic read: advisory group and data sovereignty practice are strengths. Formal governance, reporting cadence and independent oversight need strengthening.

What exists:

- Advisory group.
- ACT Pty Ltd incorporated.
- Butterfly DGR transition in motion.
- AI Human-in-Loop policy.
- Empathy Ledger/data sovereignty practice.
- `/admin/reports` and funder reporting surfaces.

Build:

- **Evidence artifact:** governance current-state memo: who advises, who decides now, what policies exist.
- **Decision artifact:** board/advisory skills matrix and gap plan.
- **Decision artifact:** reporting calendar: monthly internal, quarterly funder/investor, annual community/impact.
- **Decision artifact:** data sovereignty and consent one-pager.

Do not overclaim:

- Do not say independent governance is fully solved until board charter, decision rights and reporting cadence are formal.

- **Decision artifact:** IP/licence/benefit-share memo.

Do not overclaim:

### 10. Investors & Capital Raising

Diagnostic read: the investor narrative is compelling verbally. The biggest gap is founder-authored, concise and accurate investor documentation backed by model, projections and measurement framework.

What exists:

- Capital stack.
- QBE Catalyst Brief.
- Funder pages, reports and deals admin.
- QBE match logic and 31 August LOI gate.

Build:

- **Evidence artifact:** capital stack sheet with committed, likely, target and future lanes separated.
- **Decision artifact:** founder-authored investor pack.
- **Decision artifact:** LOI tracker with top 3 targets and next actions.
- **Decision artifact:** QBE matched-funding evidence checklist.

- No committed repayable finance today.
- QBE strengthens the case for finance; it does not mean the finance is already secured.

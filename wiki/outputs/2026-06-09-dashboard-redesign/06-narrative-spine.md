# Forward-Looking Impact Dashboard — Narrative Spine

## 1. The one-line thesis (page promise)

> **We are building productive assets that communities come to own, and the goods are already in service. Here is where we are heading, what it will take to get there, and how you can help fund the next handover.**

Short variants for the hero, by audience (same spine, swapped first line):
- **Funders:** "A community-owned production economy in remote Australia, and a matched-capital path to scale it."
- **Partners:** "What we are building together next, and the assets that stay on country when we are done."
- **Supporters:** "Beds and machines already in service, a plant that becomes community-owned, and the jobs that come with it."

The thesis deliberately leads with **direction and ownership transfer**, names the **goods in service** as live proof (not a thank-you), and treats the reader as someone who **backs the next stage**, never as a donor being thanked for the last one.

## 2. Ordered section spine (one long scroll, sticky scrollspy)

Flow: **the goal → the funding target + blended-finance path → what the community will own → goods in service now → community voice → growth trajectory → how to back it.** Each section answers one question and carries one confidence framing. The scrollspy labels are the short words on the left; the headings are the on-page H2s.

### Section 0 — Hero / Orientation
**Scrollspy label:** Where we're heading
**H2:** *On country, owned on country*
**Question it answers:** In one breath, what is this and where is it going?
**Content:** The thesis line, plus the five hero answers the research mandated, framed forward: what we are building, what goods have already moved, are they still in service, what is changing for people, how sure are we. A single "as at <date>" stamp and a one-line data-sovereignty statement ("Community holds the story; we hold the count").
**Confidence framing:** A legend introduced here and used everywhere below: **COUNTED** (administrative / audited), **MODELLED** (proxy / stated assumption), **NOT-YET-MEASURED** (named honestly, not hidden). Define the three grades once, in plain words, so every later badge is legible.
**Impact Frontiers anchor:** *What* + *Risk* (we name evidence risk up front).

### Section 1 — The goal
**Scrollspy label:** The goal
**H2:** *Where we are heading*
**Question it answers:** What future are we building toward, and why does it matter more than any single number?
**Content:** The destination in community terms: a recycled-plastic production economy that runs on country, makes beds and machines remote communities actually want, and **transfers to community ownership**. Output-vs-outcome made explicit here as a framing device for the whole page: *goods moved* (output) is not the point; *what changes for people, and who owns the means of making it* (outcome) is. One forward statement of the change we are pursuing, with the equity note that the asset is designed to leave our hands.
**Confidence framing:** This section is **direction, not measurement** — labelled as ambition/intent, explicitly NOT a metric. Honesty rail: we do not dress a goal up as an achievement.
**Frameworks:** Impact Frontiers *What* + *Contribution* (our role is to start it and hand it over); CARE principle of **Responsibility** (data and assets serve community self-determination).

### Section 2 — The funding target & the blended-finance path
**Scrollspy label:** The path
**H2:** *What it takes, and how the money is built*
**Question it answers:** What are we raising, in what shape, and what does each dollar unlock?
**Content:** State the **target as a target**, not a tally. The QBE Catalysing Impact path: up to ~A$400K of catalytic capital that **must be at least matched by signed external commitments** (matched, not secured; not awarded until awarded). Show the capital *stack* as a forward structure — repayable finance prioritised, grants where they fit, the match requirement visible — so the reader sees a deliberately blended instrument, not a wish list. A clear "what one container / one match unlocks" line that ties money to a unit of community-owned capacity. Include the honest status of each tranche (committed / in conversation / target).
**Confidence framing:** Every figure tagged: **secured vs matched-target vs aspiration**. The QBE amount is explicitly *matched-and-not-yet-awarded*. No figure here is allowed to read as money in the bank unless it is.
**Frameworks:** GIIN — **intentionality** and **financial-return-alongside-impact** (blended finance is named honestly); Impact Frontiers *Contribution* (catalytic capital is additionality, say so).

### Section 3 — What the community will own
**Scrollspy label:** Community-owned assets
**H2:** *The assets that stay*
**Question it answers:** What productive, lasting thing does the community come to own, and what is the handover path?
**Content:** The heart of the page. The on-country containerised plant (collect → shred → melt → press) framed as **a productive asset on a path to community ownership**, with the ownership-transfer mechanism described plainly. The First Nations jobs pathway (Oonchiumpa / Alice Springs, justice-involved youth) framed as **community as employer and owner**, not as recipients of jobs. Spell out what "owned" means concretely (control of the plant, the income, the maintenance, the decision rights). Centre Indigenous agency: this is community building its own means of production with us as a starter, not a service delivered to it.
**Confidence framing:** Separate **what exists today** (COUNTED — plant components, communities engaged) from **the ownership transfer** (NOT-YET-MEASURED / in design — name the milestones, don't claim them done). Equity note required: who holds title, who holds the income, who decides.
**Frameworks:** Impact Frontiers *Who* (community as **agent and owner**, not beneficiary) + *Contribution*; **Maiam nayri Wingara** Indigenous data-sovereignty principles and **CARE** (Collective benefit, Authority to control) — community governs the asset and its data.

### Section 4 — Goods in service now (the honest proof)
**Scrollspy label:** In service now
**H2:** *What is already on country, and still working*
**Question it answers:** Is the model real today, and how do we know?
**Content:** The proof layer, deliberately *after* the forward story so it serves the thesis rather than anchoring the page in the past. Beds delivered and **still in service**, washing machines live/deployed and pinging, plastic diverted, communities reached. Fewer numbers, more context: each headline metric gets a community comparison ("what this means for a household / a community") and an output-vs-outcome note (a bed delivered is an output; a bed still in service after N months is closer to the outcome). Use live asset-register and washer-ping data where it exists; show service-status, not just delivery count.
**Confidence framing:** This is the most-graded section. Each metric carries its badge: delivery counts **COUNTED**; plastic-diverted-per-bed **MODELLED** (state the 20kg/bed assumption); still-in-service rates **COUNTED where pinged, MODELLED where inferred, NOT-YET-MEASURED where we simply don't have eyes on it**. Name the dark machines / unmapped units honestly rather than rounding them into the win.
**Frameworks:** Impact Frontiers *How Much* (depth/scale/duration — duration = still-in-service) + *Risk* (evidence risk graded per metric); GIIN measurement-and-management.

### Section 6 — Growth trajectory / what's next
**Scrollspy label:** What's next
**H2:** *The next handovers*
**Question it answers:** Where does this go from here, on what timeline, and what has to be true?
**Content:** The forward arc made concrete: the Supabase roadmap (kanban / timeline) read as **upcoming community-owned capacity** — next communities, next plant, next handover, next jobs. ### Section 7 — How to back it
**Scrollspy label:** Back the next stage
**H2:** *Back the next handover*
**Question it answers:** What exactly can this reader do, and what will it move?
**Content:** The call to action, audience-aware (the one section whose copy most varies by template variant): for **funders**, the matched-capital ask and what one match unlocks; for **partners**, the next build and decision; for **supporters**, the concrete next unit they can move. Always phrased as **backing the next handover / the next community-owned asset**, never "give to help these people." Close with the data-sovereignty and contact note. End on direction, not gratitude.
**Confidence framing:** Asks tied to the **secured-vs-target** state from Section 2, so the reader knows whether they are filling a gap, providing the match, or extending the runway. No inflated ask, no implied urgency that isn't real.
**Frameworks:** Impact Frontiers *Contribution* (the reader's additionality is explicit); GIIN intentionality.

### Template-reuse notes (partners / supporters / funders)
- **One spine, three skins.** Sections 0–6 are shared; only the **first hero line** and **Section 7 (how to back it)** swap by audience. Keep the section order and confidence legend identical across all three so the standard travels.
- **Confidence legend is a shared component**, defined once (Section 0), reused as a badge on every metric — this is what makes the template trustworthy and portable.
- **Per-section trio is mandatory and reusable:** every measurement section carries (a) a community comparison, (b) an equity note, (c) a confidence note. Bake these as slots in the section component so a new partner page cannot ship without them.
- **Consent gating is structural, not editorial:** Section 5 and any photo/quote pulls render only what carries documented consent, so a new audience variant inherits non-extraction by default.

## 3. Language & framings to AVOID (hard list)

**Charity / gratitude tropes**
- "Thank you to [funder] for their generous gift" — drop backward gratitude entirely; this page is forward.
- "Thanks to your support we were able to…" — reframes the reader as past donor, not future backer.
- "Helping the less fortunate," "giving back," "making a difference," "changing lives," "lifting communities out of…" — saviour framing; delete.
- "Your donation provides X" charity-math when the instrument is blended/repayable finance — name the real instrument.

**Extractive / beneficiary language**
- "Beneficiaries," "recipients in need," "the people we help/serve," "vulnerable communities," "disadvantaged Indigenous people" — community is **agent, owner, employer, knowledge-holder**, never a passive object.
- "We delivered to them / we provided for them" — centre community action; we start and hand over.
- "Their story" used to mean *our content* — stories belong to the storyteller; attribute and consent-gate.
- Any identifiable detail without documented consent: full names, GPS, contact details, raw issue/case text, identifiable photos.

**Vanity / over-claimed numbers**
- Big round totals with no denominator or service-status ("1,000 beds delivered!") — show fewer numbers with context, and report **still-in-service**, not just shipped.
- Presenting MODELLED figures (plastic-per-bed, lives-touched) as COUNTED — every figure wears its grade.
- Presenting the QBE / matched capital as **secured** — it is **matched-target, not yet awarded**; say so every time.
- "Lives changed," "people impacted," "communities transformed" — unmeasurable inflation; replace with what is actually counted plus a named confidence grade.
- Cherry-picked wins that hide the dark units (unmapped machines, beds we've lost eyes on) — name the gaps.

**Style / brand rails**
- **No em dashes anywhere** (brand-hard + top AI tell). Use commas, full stops, or restructure.
- No "not just X but Y," no "showcases/underscores/unlocks/empowers" puffery, no forced rule-of-three, no decorative arrows in prose, no Wikipedia-AI tells.
- No false urgency, no "limited time," no manufactured scarcity around an ask.
- No jargon wall: frameworks (Impact Frontiers, GIIN, CARE) inform the structure but should mostly be *felt*, surfaced lightly (e.g. a confidence badge, an equity note), not lectured.

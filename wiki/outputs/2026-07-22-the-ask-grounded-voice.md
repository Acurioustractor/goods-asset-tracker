# The ask

> The ask in the ACT voice, funder-simple dial. The third leg after the story and the money. Operational detail (dates, per-funder steps, the stack) lives on the current Goods x QBE Start Here page; this is the narrative version. Claims honest: nothing is signed yet ($0 today); "raising", not "raised"; revenue is the accountant-signed carve-out only.

Three doors. The same bed behind each.

Goods ends the asking. Bed sales carry production, and the plant goes home to community. Getting there takes three kinds of money, and a funder walks through whichever door fits them.

**Invest the plant.** Money that builds the making: the presses, the containers, the run that proves the community line. It is repayable, because a plant that pays back should pay its investors back. (SEFA, QBE.)

**Fund the program.** Money that pays the people: the youth worker, the trainer, the training. It does not pay back, and it should not. The jobs are the dividend. (Snow, DEWR, place-based.)

**Back the proof.** The first, bravest money. We have already made our first 40 beds in-house at our own plant; this backs the measured run that locks the cost and rate, and the on-country proof, before the rest is certain. It makes the other two possible.

## The number in front of us

We are raising $400,000 in signed capital by 31 August. QBE matches it, dollar for dollar, up to $400,000. That is an $800,000 program. The application follows in late September, a pitch and interview after that, outcomes in early November. Nothing is signed yet. The first mover sets the pace.

## Why it is a real business, not an appeal

Goods already turns over $713,827 a year, accountant-signed. 540 beds, 11 communities, every bed tracked since 2024. The money does not start the story. It finishes a promise the communities already made us keep.

## What it is not

Not a subsidy that runs forever. Every dollar has a job and an end. Gifts end at break-even. Loans go home. Sales carry it. Then the plant changes owner.

Nobody funds Goods forever. That is the design.

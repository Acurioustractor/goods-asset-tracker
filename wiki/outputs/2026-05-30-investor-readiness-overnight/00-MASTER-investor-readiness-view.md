## a) The simple investor story

1. **What it is.** Goods on Country designs durable, washable, repairable goods — flagship is the **Stretch Bed** (recycled HDPE legs, galvanised steel poles, Australian canvas) — and makes them *on Country* from recycled plastic.
2. **The proof.** **496 bed units are deployed and tracked** across 10 remote communities, each with a scannable QR digital twin in a live asset register. This is real delivery, not a deck.
3. **The model.** Two capital streams feed one machine: **philanthropy** funds a fixed annual block (facility, founder production time, field travel); **product revenue** at **AU$750/bed** covers the per-bed cost. The end-state is **community-owned production** — the founders' job is to become unnecessary.
4. **The money so far.** **AU$649,710.79 received** (Xero workpaper, not audited) against AU$732,210.79 billed, anchored by Snow Foundation (AU$402,929.79 paid). Commercial-at-margin is *emerging*, not established.
5. **The ask.** A blended, **non-equity** stack: grants + recoverable/concessional capital, with **QBE's matching capital as the prize** — capped (≈AU$200K–400K, *to be confirmed*) and **contingent**: it matches eligible non-QBE capital we raise *first*.
6. **Why now.** The product, plant and delivery are proven. The next 12 months prove the *repeatable engine*: standing inventory, route pricing, and **in-sourcing plastic** (the swing that takes the marginal bed from ~AU$685 today toward ~AU$426).
7. **The honest gaps.** No signed LOIs yet, founder time is uncosted, and the trading entity's governance/DGR/legal migration is in flight — all known, all on the action stack below.

> **Never say:** pipeline is committed capital · the model is audited · there's one "cost per bed" · DGR is live · QBE money is secured.

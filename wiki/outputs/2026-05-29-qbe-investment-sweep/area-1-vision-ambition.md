# QBE Area 1 — Vision & Ambition

Diagnostic status: Strength (score 8, target 9, gap 1). Artifact build status: Built — needs review. Priority: P1. Owner: Ben.

## Summary

Vision is the strongest part of the diagnostic, not a gap. The founders clearly articulate the mission, the design philosophy (washable, repairable, durable, flat-packable), the near-term move to deploy the next On-Country production facility, and the long-term destination where Aboriginal-controlled or locally owned entities run production, recycling and maintenance on Country. The Stretch Bed is the live proof: recycled HDPE, galvanised steel and heavy-duty canvas, 26kg, 200kg capacity, ~5-minute toolless assembly, tracked through a QR-linked asset register. The real gap is not more vision — it is a founder-authored 1-3 page narrative and an operating-model diagram that make the relationship between ACT, Goods, Butterfly, funders, buyers and future community entities legible without a live founder explanation.

## Where we are vs the diagnostic

The diagnostic (V4, 13 May 2026) read vision as a strength and flagged two missing artifacts: a founder-authored 1-3 page strategy narrative and an operating-model diagram. Since V4, the full internal review has produced draft versions of both — a founder narrative draft and a Mermaid operating-model diagram — plus a verified data spine: 496 deployed bed units, 633 bed units across all statuses, 270 Stretch Beds, deployment across 10 communities (queried directly from v2 Supabase, 29 May 2026). What remains is the founder's own pass: signing the narrative, having the diagram legal/governance-reviewed, and choosing the canonical public bed number. The vision is sound; the artifact layer is in draft and needs the founder's hand and a copy clean-up before external use.

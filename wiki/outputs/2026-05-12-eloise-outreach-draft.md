> Subject: Quick Butterfly Movement / Goods on Country question

> Hi Eloise,

> I'm writing with a question I'd love your thinking on. You don't need to answer it today.

> Nic and I are working through how to set Goods on Country up for the next phase. The Social Impact Hub diagnostic landed last week and three things came clear at the same time:

> 1. We need a clean DGR vehicle to receive philanthropic capital for community-led production.
> 2. We need a small, capable, Aboriginal-led board.
> 3. We have a specific gap on commercial and social-enterprise scale-up experience.

> What we're exploring is a respectful transition of an existing dormant DGR charity, **The Butterfly Movement Ltd**, into the charitable home for Goods on Country. Goods on Country Pty Ltd would be the mission-led trading entity; community-led production entities would sit underneath; Butterfly would receive donations, fund access, and protect the charitable purpose.

> The directors we're proposing are: Kristy Bloomfield (cultural authority, Oonchiumpa chair), Nic and me as founders, and you.

> The reason we're asking you is specific: TABOO's structure is the closest precedent we have for what we're trying to build. A trading enterprise where the product engine and the charitable engine reinforce each other rather than one apologising for the other. The diagnostic explicitly named a gap that TABOO experience fills. You'd be the only director on the board who has actually scaled a social enterprise of this kind.

> No expectation, no pressure. Three things would help us most right now:

> 1. **Are you open to a 20-minute call in the next two weeks** to talk through whether this fits your time, your stage, and your sense of what's worth doing? Happy to send the full proposal in advance.
> 2. **If the proposal isn't right for you,** would you be open to recommending someone in your network with similar experience?
> 3. **Either way,** if you have 90 seconds, what's the single thing you'd want a board like this to get right from day one?

> Thanks Eloise. Whatever you decide, we appreciate you reading this far.

> Ben

We're working through ACNC, ASIC, DGR and governance due diligence before approaching the current Butterfly directors and members with a respectful transition proposal.

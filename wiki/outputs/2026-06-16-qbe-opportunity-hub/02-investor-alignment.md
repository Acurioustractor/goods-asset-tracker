## Why "warmest-first" is the right sort

The Stage 2 grant (up to AU$400K, $150K floor) is judged on **matched, legally-binding external commitments**, and **repayable finance is preferred over grants**. So the engine is not "find the most aligned funder in the abstract", it is "who can sign a match-eligible commitment first". Warmth here means **proximity to a signature before the September application**, built from three signals:

1. **Engagement stage** in the CRM (Identified → Qualified → Cultivating → Ask made → Committed → Stewarding/Reporting → Renewing). A funder already stewarding a grant is one conversation from a renewal ask; an "Identified" prospect is months of cultivation away.
2. **Capital type fit with the match rule.** A repayable / concessional commitment is worth more to the match than an equivalent grant, because the program prefers it.
3. **Knockout pass.** If a prospect fails any of the 8 knockouts (especially capital type or timing), it cannot count toward the match no matter how warm.

This is Goods' own definition, grounded in the diagnostic and the program terms. Use it as the yardstick every prospect is scored against.

### The 8 knockout criteria (a prospect must be at least "somewhat aligned" on all 8)

| # | Knockout | Goods' required answer |
|---|---|---|
| K1 | Investment Geography | Funds remote and regional Australia, First Nations communities (NT, WA, QLD, SA). |
| K2 | Legal Structure | Can deploy to the new Goods Pty Ltd (for-purpose company) and/or via the Butterfly Movement DGR charity. |
| K3 | Investment Size | Can commit toward the $150K floor and up to $400K (individually or as part of an aggregated round). |
| K4 | Timing | Can reach a signed, legally-binding commitment (LOI or beyond) by end August / September 2026. |
| K5 | Exit Expectations | Does not require an equity exit or liquidity event. Patient, no-exit, or simple repayment only. |
| K6 | Impact Alignment | Backs Indigenous-led, remote-community wellbeing, circular / recycled materials, local employment. |
| K7 | Capital Type & Structure | Grant **or** concessional / repayable debt. **Not** equity. (Founders assess equity inappropriate at this stage; revisit only in a future community-ownership model.) |
| K8 | Stage of Growth | Invests at early-commercial / pre-scale stage, not seed-only and not mature-only. |

### The 14 fit criteria (negotiable; priority labels are Goods' call)

**Mission & Vision (2)**
- F1 Social Mission — **High**
- F2 Company Vision (community-ownership pathway) — **Medium**

**What we expect to give (4)**
- F3 Control & influence we can accept (must protect the community-ownership pathway and Aboriginal-led governance) — **High**
- F4 Restrictions / reporting load we can carry — **Medium**
- F5 Impact evidence we can supply (consent-cleared stories, deployment data) — **Medium**
- F6 Connections we can offer them (remote-community access, ACT ecosystem) — **Low**

**What we want from the investor (8)**
- F7 Connections (other funders, buyers, co-investors for the match) — **High**
- F8 Remote-Australia / First Nations geography experience — **High**
- F9 Industry experience (manufacturing, social enterprise, circular economy) — **Medium**
- F10 Scale-up / business-challenge experience — **High**
- F11 Working style and behaviour (relational, not extractive) — **Medium**
- F12 Commercial flexibility (blended, patient, tolerant of early-stage) — **High**
- F13 Term complexity (we prefer simple terms; light legal lift) — **Medium**
- F14 Reputation and founder feedback (others have raised from them well) — **High**

Note on the QBE co-invest clause (cl 5.3): SIH or its nominee holds a 3-year, 45-day right to co-invest on the same terms in any future raise. Score this under F13 / term complexity for any prospect, and flag it before signing.

## Part C. What this tells us for Thursday and September

1. **The match can be built from warm ground.** Five Tier-1 relationships (Snow, SEFA, Centrecorp, Minderoo, VFFF) plus an active Tier-2 ask list already cover the $150K floor several times over **if** they convert to signed LOIs in time. The work is conversion, not discovery.
2. **SEFA is the single highest-value warm lead** because it is repayable and already in advanced discussion. Repayable commitments are what the program most wants to see matched. Prioritise moving SEFA to a term sheet.
3. **The repayable gap is real.** Almost everything warm is a grant. The strongest repayable plays are SEFA (already advanced) and First Australians Capital (open it now). QLD PFI is closed for this cycle (verified), so it is not a September match source.
4. **Keep grant-vs-match honest.** Centrecorp's bed order and the buyer pipeline are revenue, not match capital. The QBE and DEWR asks cannot count as match. Don't let the headline pipeline number flatter the match position.

## 1. The opportunity, in plain language

**Catalysing Impact 2026** is run by **Social Impact Hub (SIH)** in partnership with the **QBE Foundation**. A Curious Tractor / Goods on Country is one of **10 selected enterprises** sharing a **$1,000,000 capital pool**. There are four moving parts:

1. **The Stage 2 grant (the prize).** Up to **AU$400,000** per enterprise (with a **$150,000 floor**), from the shared $1M pool. It is **discretionary and competitive**, **must be at least matched by external commitments**, **prefers repayable finance over grants**, and is **not secured until awarded** by the Steering Committee. Stage 1 also pays a flat **$10,000 participation grant**. No participant fee.
2. **The QBE project = Market Demand Research.** This is the main SIH-supported workstream (and the likely home of the hackathon).
3. **The Tailored Advisory = Production Cost Estimates.** This is the **Excel production-costing model**, now led by **Matt** with **Mal** on QA/costing. This is the track Jay's email scopes.
4. **The hackathon.** A full day in **August**, about **15 QBE employees** from across the business working a design-thinking challenge we set.

Plus **mentoring**: 6 x 60-minute sessions over 6 months with a PIN member (informal, confidential, no deliverables; contact Jay).

**The logic of the whole thing:** the grant is judged on matched funding, so the real game is converting our warmest funder conversations into **signed, legally-binding external commitments** before the September application. SIH's "Letter to Funders" is their tool for that (EOI to LOI to Term Sheet to Funding Agreement), and SIH confirms commitments directly with funders at due diligence.

**Step 4, rank by proximity to a signature** before the September application, warmest first: **Snow R4/R5, Centrecorp, SEFA, VFFF.**

## 8. The hackathon challenge (to propose for August)

Jay's brief: accessible without deep sector knowledge, clear audience or problem, broad enough for creative thinking, specific enough for tangible ideas in one day, likely to lead to practical next steps. ~15 QBE staff, one day. The reference (Hotel Etico) worked because it picked **one stakeholder and one tightly framed problem** with a mission anyone grasps.

Three drafts (full versions in `03-hackathon-extract.md`):

- **A. The first night with a new bed.** A remote family's journey from unboxing to caring for the bed over years. Closest structural match to Hotel Etico.
- **B. Help a community tell its own impact story, in its own voice.** Best for a creative / comms-leaning room.
- **C. Make "community-owned" make sense to an outsider.** Strongest if the goal is sharpening our own pitch (the QBE staff are the real audience).

**Recommendation:** lead with **A**, offer **C** as the alternative.

## 1. The connected thread (so the deck is a story, not a quote wall)

**Primary spine — Mykel.** His arc *is* the catalytic thesis in one young person: **waste → product → skill → work → ownership.** Use him as the recurring thread — open on him, return to him, close on him.
- Hook: *"Never would've thought it would've come out like that."* (he built it)
- Proof/dignity: *"Comfortable as. Smooth, tight, hard, fancy."*
- The turn (jobs/local production): *"I was looking for something, job like this here, but couldn't find any."* → *"I'll be rocking up every day to make them."*
- The elder's blessing (hands it to the future): Fred — *"That could be a good employment for yourself too, grandson. Later on."*

**Secondary arc — Tennant Creek (depth in one place).** The Frank family + Linda Turner show a whole community moving co-design → need → self-determination → scale: Linda ("never been asked") → Dianne Stokes (Elder, co-designer) → Norman Frank ("our own ways… it'll be everywhere") → Patricia Frank ("we want to help other language groups"). Use sparingly as the "this goes deep and travels" proof.

**Rule:** one face per beat, the same few faces recurring, each earning their moment. Pair voice + face + the number on the same slide.

## 6. How to weave (principles)

1. **One thread, recurring.** Mykel opens, turns, and closes; Tennant Creek is the depth arc. Don't introduce 29 names — feature ~8 and let the rest live in an appendix/voices wall.
2. **Voice + face + number on the same beat.** A quote next to the data it humanises (Alfred ↔ $150 freight; Wayne ↔ RHD; Mykel ↔ the jobs/$194 cost-down).
3. **Agency, not charity.** Every voice frames community as the actor and owner — never the recipient of rescue. Lead with "asked for," "our own ways," "community-owned."
4. **Consent + data sovereignty (OCAP).** curatedQuotes is the public-cleared set; Utopia-trip voices (Mykel, Fred) cleared 2026-06-02. Anything outside these → confirm before public use. Attribute by name + community; never fabricate a location (some voices have none recorded — attribute by role/context instead).
5. **Don't sprinkle — earn it.** If a quote doesn't carry the beat's argument, cut it.

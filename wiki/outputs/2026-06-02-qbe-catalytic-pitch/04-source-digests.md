### What the program IS

### The thesis on catalytic / blended / impact capital
- **Catalytic capital** = "going where no one else will, for the purpose of releasing other capital." Chemistry metaphor: the catalyst enables the reaction but is consumed. It funds earlier (feasibility/seed), around the edges (learning/evaluation), or takes the higher-risk piece on concessional terms (first-loss, guarantees, longer/cheaper).
- **Blended finance** = the right mix of capital from stakeholders with different risk-return needs but common impact objectives. Concessional/philanthropic capital "de-risks" to pull in private/market-rate capital. The grant exists to solve the problem that commercial investors won't enter at the enterprise's risk/return profile alone.
- **Capital-stack reordering:** grants + guarantees sit junior (absorb first losses), protecting subordinated/standard debt and common equity. Unabsorbed grant value accrues as equity on the balance sheet.

4. **Who impact investors are** — 11 categories from HNWIs/angels through family offices, CDFIs/DFIs, foundations (PRI), government funds, corporates, institutional banks.

## What the tool is
Duke CASE's **Smart Impact Capital — Investor Alignment Tool** is an investor-**triage** spreadsheet, not an enterprise-readiness scorer. The venture documents its OWN filter criteria (Tab 1 "Our Needs"), logs investors it meets (Tab 2), then rates each investor against those criteria (Tab 3) to surface "top targets worth your time." Output feeds "Module 7" (relationship cultivation). Readiness, for Goods, = being able to answer all 16 criteria crisply and clear the knockout gate with the funders it targets.

## The 16 dimensions (4 groups)
**KNOCKOUT (6 — must match, no negotiation):** 1) Investment Geography (invests where we operate/HQ); 2) Legal Structure (for-profit / nonprofit / hybrid); 3) Investment Size (amount sought); 4) Return Expectations (financial/social, timeframe, exit); 5) Capital Type (debt / equity / grant / recoverable grant); 6) Stage of Growth.
**FIT — Mission & Vision:** 7) Social Mission; 8) Company Vision.
**FIT — Expected From Us:** 9) Governance (e.g. board seat); 10) Restrictions on use of funds; 11) Impact Evidence we can supply.
**FIT — Expected From Investors:** 12) Connections; 13) Geography experience; 14) Industry experience; 15) Business-challenge experience; 16) Impact on future investments.

## Scoring model (verified from formulas)
- Rating dropdown (5): **Very Aligned / Somewhat Aligned / Not Aligned / Don't Know / N/A**.
- **Knockout result** `=IF(count of the 6 knockouts rated Very OR Somewhat = 6, "Yes", "No")`. Any "Not Aligned" or "Don't Know" on a knockout → **No**.
- **High-Priority Fit** = (# high-priority fit criteria rated very/somewhat) / (# fit criteria the venture flagged "High"). Each fit criterion is pre-tagged High/Medium/Low.
- **All Fit** = (# of all 10 fit criteria aligned) / 10.
- No weighted composite — three readouts (Knockout Yes/No, HiPri X/Y, All X/10) drive a human ranking.

Investor types (14): Angel; Friends & Family; Family Office; Community/Family Foundation; CDFI/DFI; Private Foundation; Government; International NGO; Corporate; Investment Fund; Institutional Investor/Bank; Fellowship/Incubator/Accelerator; Crowdfunding; Other.

## "Fundable/aligned" = clears all 6 knockouts AND scores high on self-flagged high-priority fit criteria. (Example: a foundation passed knockouts but failed Mission/Vision/Restrictions → wrong target.)

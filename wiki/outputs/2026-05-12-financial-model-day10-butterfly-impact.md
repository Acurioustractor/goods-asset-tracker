## What this is

A side-by-side financial comparison of two entity structures:

- **Status quo (or near future):** Goods on Country Pty Ltd as a single trading entity, all revenue + costs in one place. Per-Day-6 base-case.
- **Post-Butterfly transition:** Goods on Country Pty Ltd (trading) + The Butterfly Movement Ltd (DGR charity, mission-aligned). Charity unlocks DGR donations, PAF/PuAF capital, and subsidised-access programs. Cost re-allocations follow.

The goal: answer "what does the Butterfly transition unlock financially?" with a number, not a story.

## What Butterfly DGR unlocks

### Revenue side (new lines that don't exist without Butterfly)

1. **DGR donations** — donor receives a tax deduction. Private donors and corporates routinely require DGR status before they'll give material amounts. ATO Item 1 DGR is the gold standard.
2. **PAF / PuAF eligibility** — Private Ancillary Funds and Public Ancillary Funds (the major Australian high-net-worth philanthropic vehicles) can only distribute to DGR Item 1 organisations. Without Butterfly, these funds can't fund Goods.
3. **Subsidised-access program** — Butterfly receives donations earmarked for community deployment. Butterfly purchases beds from Goods Pty Ltd. Beds delivered to communities at $0 to recipient. The donation flows: donor → Butterfly (deductible) → Goods Pty Ltd (revenue) → community (mission outcome).
4. **Capability funding** — donors fund training programs, MEL framework activation, evidence gathering. These are mission-aligned but not commercial revenue.
5. **Repeat donor cadence** — DGR enables annual giving and recurring committed-giving products which a Pty Ltd cannot offer.

### Cost re-allocations

| Function | Current state (Pty Ltd) | Post-Butterfly |
|---|---|---|
| Governance / board / audit | Pty Ltd absorbs | Butterfly has its own (charity board); Pty Ltd has commercial board |
| MEL framework + impact measurement | Pty Ltd absorbs | Butterfly funds this (charitable purpose) |
| Community capability training | Pty Ltd absorbs | Butterfly funds this (community development is charity object) |
| Storytelling + Empathy Ledger | Pty Ltd absorbs | Butterfly funds this (storytelling = charitable beneficiary outreach) |
| Compliance + reporting | Combined | Split: each entity has its own + shared services |
| Brand / marketing | Pty Ltd | Mostly Pty Ltd; Butterfly does donor comms |
| Plant / production / R&D | Pty Ltd | Stays in Pty Ltd (trading activity) |
| Sales operations | Pty Ltd | Stays in Pty Ltd |
| Founder time | Pty Ltd | Mostly Pty Ltd; some allocated to Butterfly for charity work |

### Tax / structural implications

- **Butterfly**: ACNC-registered charity; Item 1 DGR; income-tax-exempt; can receive PAF/PuAF money; constitution protects charitable purpose.
- **Goods on Country Pty Ltd**: trading company; pays company tax; R&D Tax Incentive eligible; charges Butterfly cost+small-margin for beds (avoiding profit accumulation in a related-party trading flow).
- **The Butterfly transaction**: donors give to Butterfly (deductible). Butterfly purchases beds from Goods Pty Ltd at cost (transparent related-party pricing). Beds delivered to communities. Mission outcome.

This structure is closely analogous to **TABOO**, which is why Eloise Hall is the right person to advise the board.

## Tax & charity-structure caveats

Several items need Mint Ellison + accountant confirmation:

1. **Related-party transfer pricing**: Butterfly→Pty Ltd bed purchases at "cost-plus small margin" must satisfy ACNC related-party rules. Standard practice but needs documentation.
2. **PAF / PuAF eligibility**: Butterfly needs Item 1 DGR status confirmed (not Item 2 or other variants). The existing Butterfly constitution likely supports this but ACNC review is required.
3. **GST treatment**: charity GST concessions vs Pty Ltd standard GST need clean separation.
4. **Income tax**: Butterfly should be income-tax-exempt; Pty Ltd pays standard company tax.
5. **R&D Tax Incentive**: stays with Pty Ltd. Butterfly cannot claim it.
6. **Auto-tagger / vendor rules** in Supabase: existing rules need cleanup so Butterfly transactions don't get tagged ACT-GD by mistake (see Day 1 Ingkerreke pattern).

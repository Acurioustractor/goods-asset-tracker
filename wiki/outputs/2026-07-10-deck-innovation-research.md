## [radical-honesty] The Register — a deck that audits itself — avg 8

**Tagline (the sentence a funder repeats):** Every number in their deck is a clickable claim with its evidence attached, the deck opens with what they DON'T claim, and it publicly commits to which claims will flip from modelled to measured by which date — you're invited back on 30 September to diff it.

**Core innovation:** Claims become first-class, versioned data objects. A new `claims-ledger.ts` (default-deny, like `cleared-voices.ts`) registers every externally-usable claim as {id, statement, status: verified|modelled|interest|future|locked, evidence refs, promised-flip date}. The deck renders each number as a ClaimChip; clicking opens an EvidenceDrawer showing the actual chain — the live Supabase asset count from `getCanonicalAssetRollup()` with today's drift-check result, the OCR'd invoice line behind a cost figure from `cost-model-scenarios.json`, the consent record behind a quote from `cleared-voices.ts` (cleared 2026-06-17, by name). The ledger is dated and diffable: the deck names, in advance, the two events that will move claims — the 50-bed run (flips $421 from modelled to measured) and the LOI ladder (flips $0 signed toward $400K) — and shows a changelog of every claim that has ever changed status. No other deck can do this because no other startup has a QR-traceable asset register, an invoice-OCR cost model with per-number confidence, and a formal consent architecture to wire it to.

**Experience flow:** The investor opens their gated instance (`/register/[slug]`, funder-pages pattern) and the first screen is black-on-cream text: "What we do not claim." Three lines — we do not claim prevented disease; demand is interest, not revenue; our cost-down is a hypothesis until beds 1–50 come off our own press. Then the ask, one line. From there it reads like the 14-slide deck they know, except every number wears a small labelled chip. They click "496 beds" and a drawer slides open: live count from the asset register, reconciliation date, drift-check ran today, agrees. They click Alfred Johnson's quote and see its consent record. They click "$421" and see the invoice OCR behind the verified lines and honest grey on the modelled ones, with the CostPlayground sliders right there to stress it. Mid-deck they hit The Experiment: the 50-bed run pre-registered like a trial — hypothesis, method, pass/fail thresholds, and what Goods will publish if it misses. Then the raise assembling in real time: the LOI ladder from the live pipeline, QBE match meter honestly at $0 signed, their own name on the next rung. The close: Norman Frank's quote, then "Come back 30 September and diff this page." A changelog link shows every claim that has ever moved, and when.

**Architecture:**
- 1. Anti-claims cold open — three things Goods does NOT claim (scabies→RHD is the why, not an outcome; interest ≠ revenue; cost-down unproven) before any logo or photo
- 2. The claim + ask — one line, AU$400K recoverable grant converting to community ownership, with the ask itself rendered as a ClaimChip (status: future, flip date 31 Aug)
- 3. The floor tonight — Alfred Johnson (Palm Island) quote whose chip opens his consent record from cleared-voices.ts; media-pack golden-hour photo
- 4. The bed and the making — assembly.mp4 inline, X-trestle explainer, 'the making belongs On Country' with process/ factory photography
- 5. Traction — 496/9/16/2,660 as live ClaimChips: drawer shows getCanonicalAssetRollup() vs asset-canonical.ts with today's drift-check verdict
- 6. Trust the register, not the pitch — embedded live /bed/GB0-156-40 record: scan log, event timeline, map; 'pick any bed, we'll show you its life'
- 7. What a bed really costs — $685→$421 waterfall where each line item carries its cost-model-scenarios.json confidence flag; verified invoice lines open their OCR source; Idiot Index (8.6× shred)
- 8. Stress it yourself — embedded CostPlayground (cost-story shared engine): price/volume sliders, BUY/MAKE toggle, link out to /investors?skin=investment
- 9. The Experiment — 50-bed run pre-registered: hypothesis ($421 ± threshold), method, AU$60–80K budget, pass/fail criteria, and the published-either-way commitment; the claim that flips modelled→measured
- 10. Risk register as a feature, not an appendix — scored risks with owners and gate dates (entity/51% end-July, SEFA independent-director covenant, founder concentration), each a claim object
- 11. The round assembling in real time — LOI ladder (target→signed→contract→cash) from loi-pipeline.ts, QBE match meter at honest $0 signed, this funder's rung highlighted
- 12. Why now — micro-manufacturing, IPP 51% threshold tightening 1 July 2026, HDPE + freight economics
- 13. Team and governance — 2 founders, 0 hires labelled honest; trigger-gated GM (Oct 2026) and BD (Feb 2027); board path that clears SEFA's covenant; one claim shown LOCKED: revenue withheld pending Standard Ledger sign-off, unlock condition stated
- 14. The changelog — dated ledger of every claim that has changed status since first publication; proof the machine is real, not decoration
- 15. Close — Norman Frank (Tennant Creek), then the dated invitation: 'Come back 30 September and diff this deck'; CTA to book the Stage 2 walkthrough

**Uses existing:** /deck's LabelKind system (verified/modelled/interest/future) and its 14-slide narrative spine — upgraded from static badge to interactive claim object; asset-canonical.ts + getCanonicalAssetRollup() in impact-fetcher.ts + the existing drift-check script (live-vs-canon agreement shown in-drawer); cost-model-scenarios.ts/.json v6 with invoice-OCR verification and per-number confidence flags; cost-story's shared cost-model engine (cost-model-context.tsx), CostPlayground (play.tsx), and Recharts charts; /investors?skin=investment cockpit as deep-link; /bed/[id] live Supabase-backed bed records (embed GB0-156-40 or similar); loi-pipeline.ts LOI ladder (LoiRung target/signed/contract/cash) + MATCH_TARGET wired to live GHL pipelines; cleared-voices.ts default-deny allowlist + curated-quotes.ts (Alfred Johnson, Ivy, Dianne Stokes, Norman Frank); funder-pages.ts per-slug password gating pattern in proxy.ts for personalised instances; media-pack and process/ photography, stretch-bed/assembly.mp4, brand tokens (--goods-terracotta etc., Georgia display); funder-shared-content.ts atoms (TRACTION_STATS, CAPITAL_STACK, QBE_PROGRAM, INVESTMENT_THESIS)

**Needs building:** claims-ledger.ts — default-deny registry of claim objects {id, statement, status, evidenceRefs, flipCondition, flipDate, history[]}; the single new source of truth; ClaimChip + EvidenceDrawer components (client) rendering evidence chains: canon-vs-live counts, invoice OCR excerpts, consent records, Xero-reconciliation summary cards (redacted, no raw ledger); /register/[slug] route: the deck itself, reusing /deck's keyboard nav and chrome-free overlay, gated per-funder via proxy.ts pattern; Changelog/diff view: dated JSON snapshots of the ledger committed on each change, rendered as a human-readable 'what moved and why' page; The Experiment page: 50-bed run pre-registration (hypothesis, method, thresholds, publish-either-way pledge) that the measured results later overwrite; LOI ladder deck widget reading loi-pipeline.ts stage data with the viewing funder's rung highlighted; LOCKED claim state rendering (revenue figure held pending accountant sign-off, unlock condition displayed)

**Effort:** ~3 weeks (2 founders + AI coding agent)

**Consent/claim-ceiling risks + mitigation:** Evidence drawers are the risk surface: they could leak un-cleared voices, the withheld AU$741,111+ revenue figure, raw Xero data, supplier pricing, or bed-owner PII from /bed embeds. Mitigations: claims-ledger.ts is default-deny — a claim renders ONLY if registered, and quote claims must reference a cleared-voices.ts entry or the chip refuses to render (same guard pattern as products.guards.test.ts); revenue ships as a LOCKED claim whose drawer shows only the unlock condition, never the number; Xero evidence is a summary card (date + reconciler + scope), never a ledger export; the embedded bed record uses a bed already public at /bed/[id] with no owner identity; the 50-bed Experiment page states cost/throughput hypotheses only — no health, disease, or savings outcomes anywhere in the ledger schema (no claim category exists for them, structurally enforcing the ceiling); scabies→RHD appears once, explicitly tagged 'the why — never a claimed outcome'. The changelog itself is the final mitigation: any over-claim would be permanently visible as a retraction, which keeps the founders honest by construction.

**Why remembered:** A week later the investor doesn't remember a deck — they remember clicking a number and watching it prove itself, and they remember a date. 'Come back 30 September and diff this deck' converts due diligence from something they do TO Goods into something Goods scheduled FOR them; the 50-bed run becomes an appointment, not an assertion. In a pipeline full of decks that say 'trust us', the only one that said 'audit us — here's the button' is the one they forward to their investment committee, because it does the committee's job for them. And it's unfakeable: it only works because Goods actually has a QR bed register, OCR'd invoices, and consent records — the deck is the moat, demonstrated.

## [living-instrument] The Working Deck — "Trust the register, not the pitch" — avg 7.67

**Tagline (the sentence a funder repeats):** Their deck is a live query against the same register that runs the company — I watched a number they'd honestly labelled "modelled" flip to "measured" the week after our meeting.

**Core innovation:** Claims with expiry dates and a per-investor diff. Every key number in the deck is a live render from the operational data layer (asset register rollup, cost-model engine, LOI ladder) governed by a claim state machine (verified / modelled / interest / future -> measured / re-based). The centrepiece is a pre-registered experiment slide: the AU$421 in-house cost is displayed beside a visibly EMPTY "measured" slot with a commitment date, and when the 50-bed run completes the deck flips the claim live — in the copy the investor already has open. On every return visit, a "since you last opened this" strip diffs the deck against a snapshot taken at their first view: beds +N, ladder rung moved, claim state changed. No other deck changes truthfully between meeting one and meeting two; this one is designed to.

**Experience flow:** The investor gets a personal link (`/deck/qbe`, per-slug password, same pattern as `/funders/[slug]`) and, in the meeting, a printed card with a real bed's QR code. The deck opens on the familiar one-liner, but under it beats a heartbeat strip: "496 beds on register · 9 communities · last reconciled 2026-05-30 14:12 AEST" — rendered live from `getCanonicalAssetRollup()`, not typed into a slide. Slide 3 they scan the card: a real `/bed/[id]` record opens inside the deck — manufacture date, delivery event, scan history, map pin. The product just audited itself. On the model slide they drag the price and volume sliders of the embedded cost playground (`useCostModel`) and watch breakeven move; the BUY/MAKE toggle shows why the 50-bed run matters. Then the experiment slide: "$421 is modelled. On completion of the 50-bed run this slot fills with the measured number — or we re-base and tell you." The ask slide shows the QBE match thermometer assembling from the live LOI ladder. Two weeks later they reopen the link: a quiet banner reads "3 things changed since 28 July — 12 beds deployed, one commitment moved Target to Committed, cost claim still modelled (run starts 15 Sep)." They forward the link to their investment committee, because the link is the diligence.

**Architecture:**

- 2. Why — Alfred Johnson freight quote (cleared), scabies->RHD framed explicitly as the why, never an outcome; static by design, honesty is the interactive element here

- 7. The model, playable — embedded CostPlayground (cost-model-context.tsx, invoice-OCR-verified v6 scenarios); investor drags price/beds, flips BUY/MAKE, sees breakeven 1,679 -> ~338

- 10. Risks — scored 4-row register incl. revenue held at AU$741,111 pending sign-off; the withheld number is itself displayed as a claim-state ('awaiting accountant')

## [physical-hybrid] Bed №497 — The Deck You Can Hold — avg 7.67

**Tagline (the sentence a funder repeats):** Goods mailed me a chunk of community plastic with a QR code; when I scanned it, my pitch deck turned out to be a live entry in their factory register — and signing the grant writes the next event on that bed's timeline.

**Core innovation:** The deck is a reserved serial number in Goods' real asset register. The physical kit (a pressed-HDPE factory offcut token + silk-screened canvas swatch carrying the QR) is slide one; the funder's signed commitment becomes a logged event on the bed's timeline; the 50-bed run that their money funds is the run that makes Bed №497 real. ## Completeness critique (what all six missed)

**(1) What all six missed**

Two gaps, both structural:

- **The community is a subject, never a presenter.** Every concept stages the investor's experience of verified data; none puts the future owners in the room. The Empathy Ledger has 240 cleared storytellers and the entire raise is an ownership-conversion story — yet no concept has an Elder or community member delivering a segment, no visible community sign-off/consent layer on the deck itself, no "the people you're transferring this to have reviewed this pitch" artifact. A deck about handing a factory to community, pitched entirely without them, is a credibility hole every diligent funder will notice.
- **The second audience: the investment committee.** All six are first-person, experiential, synchronous formats (ride the road, hold the plastic, assemble the term sheet). Real decisions happen when the champion re-pitches to an IC or trust board via a forwarded PDF and a 3-minute skim. None of the concepts degrades gracefully into a forwardable, skimmable, defensible artifact. The deck has no second life after the meeting.

**(2) Strongest combination**

**radical-honesty (The Register, 8.0) + ownership-conversion (The Deal Room, 7.7).** They are complementary halves of one funnel: the Register manufactures trust (clickable evidence, declared unknowns, dated modelled→measured commitments), the Deal Room converts that trust into commitment (assemble your own term sheet against the 31 Aug match deadline). The 30 Sept "come back and diff us" date and the 31 Aug close chain into a single timeline. Note the redundancy elsewhere: living-instrument and wildcard are essentially variants of the Register and add little as standalone concepts — fold their best mechanics (live query, round-assembling-in-real-time final page) into this pairing.

**(3) Cheapest <1-week conviction-raiser**

**A public Claims Register page.** One static Next.js route: every headline number in the raise ($685 freight, $421 modelled, 20kg HDPE/bed, 200kg capacity, unit economics) as a row tagged **measured / modelled / aspiration**, with its source linked (invoice, canon data, cost lab) and a dated commitment for when modelled flips to measured. It requires no new instrumentation — the canon data, cost lab, and gated briefs already exist; this is structured content, not engineering. It is the load-bearing substrate of all six concepts (five of them literally presuppose it), it survives forwarding to a committee (fixing gap #2), and it raises conviction even if the final deck ends up being a plain PDF.

# Marketplace for Nature Pitch Pack

> Goods should pitch into Marketplace for Nature as an Indigenous partnership and circular-economy readiness initiative, not as an already-certified biodiversity credit project. The strongest positioning is: Goods helps remote Indigenous communities turn waste plastic and failed household-goods systems into durable essential infrastructure, with a pathway toward community-owned On-Country production and future nature-market readiness.

## Best Pitch Angle

**Working title:** Goods on Country: Community-owned circular economy infrastructure for emerging nature markets

**One-line pitch:** Goods on Country works with remote Indigenous communities to turn recovered HDPE plastic into durable essential goods, starting with the Stretch Bed, while building the local governance, measurement and manufacturing capability needed for community-led circular economy and future nature-market participation.

**Why it fits Marketplace for Nature:**

- Marketplace for Nature is designed to connect voluntary biodiversity credit buyers and sellers and support more inclusive, equitable nature markets for Indigenous Peoples, local communities and allies.
- The platform maps active and emerging nature-credit market initiatives, including projects, standards and registries.
- Its resource library covers readiness, due diligence, safeguards, FPIC, Indigenous knowledge, negotiation, practice and capacity.
- Pollination Foundation's ICIP and Indigenous Data values statement references UNDRIP, FPIC and CARE Principles, which aligns with Goods' consent-led storytelling and data-sovereignty posture.

**What not to claim:** Goods is not yet a biodiversity-credit generator. Do not say we are producing biodiversity credits, nature credits or offsets. Say we are building the practical circular-economy and readiness layer that could support community-led nature-market projects.

## 30-Second Version

Goods on Country builds durable essential goods for remote Indigenous communities from recycled HDPE plastic, galvanised steel and Australian canvas. Our flagship product, the Stretch Bed, diverts 20kg of HDPE per bed and is designed to be washable, repairable, flat-packable and made On-Country. We want to partner with Indigenous communities, ranger groups, councils and nature-market practitioners to build community-owned circular-economy infrastructure: collect plastic, process it locally, make useful products, track impact through QR-linked asset records, and protect community stories and data through consent-led systems. We see this as a practical readiness pathway for Indigenous-led nature markets, grounded in real needs rather than abstract credits.

## Initiative Submission Draft

**Initiative name:** Goods on Country

**Country:** Australia

**Initiative type:** Emerging Indigenous-led circular economy and nature-market readiness initiative

**Status:** Active, with future nature-market pathway under exploration

**Short description:** Goods on Country works with remote Indigenous communities to design, deliver and eventually localise production of durable essential goods made from recovered HDPE plastic. The flagship Stretch Bed is a flat-packable, washable bed made from recycled plastic legs, galvanised steel poles and heavy-duty Australian canvas. Each bed diverts 20kg of HDPE and can be tracked through a QR-linked asset register. The wider model is to move production On-Country, support community ownership, reduce landfill pressure, create local roles and build the practical governance and measurement capability needed for future community-led circular economy and nature-market projects.

**Indigenous leadership and partnership:** Goods works through place-based relationships with Aboriginal community partners, councils, health services and local organisations. The intended operating model is not extractive project development. Communities decide whether the product, production model, data, story and ownership pathway are right for them. FPIC, consent-led storytelling, Indigenous data governance and community benefit-sharing are treated as core infrastructure rather than communications add-ons.

**Nature-market relevance:** Goods is not currently issuing biodiversity credits or offsets. The initiative is relevant to nature markets because it addresses a practical readiness gap: communities need tangible infrastructure, trusted data systems, local capability and fair partnership models before they can participate confidently in emerging markets. Goods can support plastic recovery, landfill reduction, circular production, durable goods, repairability, local jobs and asset-level impact tracking. These foundations could connect with future nature repair, circular economy, ranger-led monitoring or biodiversity finance projects where communities choose that path.

**Measured or trackable outcomes:**

- Stretch Beds produced and deployed.
- HDPE diverted into durable products, using the canonical factor of 20kg HDPE per Stretch Bed.
- Asset-level tracking via QR code, including product ID, location, delivery record and support history where consent allows.
- Local collection, sorting, shredding, pressing and assembly capability.
- Community partner participation and governance decisions.
- Jobs, training hours and local enterprise pathways.
- Stories and feedback managed through consent-led systems with withdrawal rights.

**Safeguards:** No biodiversity-credit, offset, health, income or community-benefit claim should be made unless verified and community-approved. Indigenous knowledge, images, names, stories and data remain subject to consent, purpose limitation and withdrawal. Any future nature-market participation must be community-led and negotiated on terms the relevant community controls.

## Five-Minute Pitch Structure

1. **Start with the real problem.** Remote communities carry high freight costs, poor-quality goods, failed mattresses, failed white goods and growing landfill pressure.
2. **Show the product.** The Stretch Bed is simple: recycled HDPE legs, two galvanised steel poles and washable canvas. It flat-packs, assembles without tools and is designed for harsh remote conditions.
3. **Connect to nature and waste.** Each Stretch Bed uses 20kg of HDPE. The long-term model is local collection, shredding, pressing and manufacturing.
4. **Make it Indigenous-led in practice.** Goods' role is to support communities into ownership, not hold the value forever. Decisions, data, stories and benefit-sharing need to sit with community.
5. **Name the honest gap.** We are not yet a nature-credit project. We are building readiness, measurement and circular-economy infrastructure that could support future community-led nature finance.
6. **Make the ask.** We want Marketplace for Nature to advise whether Goods belongs on the initiatives map, in the resource library, or in a partner conversation with funders and Indigenous-led nature-market practitioners.

## Proof Points To Use

- The Stretch Bed is the current flagship product.
- Materials: recycled HDPE plastic legs, galvanised steel poles and heavy-duty Australian canvas.
- Specs: 26kg, 200kg load capacity, 188 x 92 x 25cm, assembles in about 5 minutes, no tools, 10+ year design life, 5-year warranty.
- Circular metric: 20kg HDPE per Stretch Bed. Treat this as the design/spec value unless a verified weighbridge figure is available.
- Existing delivery proof exists through the Goods asset register and QR-linked tracking.
- The production pathway is a containerised mobile plastic re-production facility: shredder, heat press, cooling rack, CNC router and related production tools.
- Future ownership pathway: On-Country production and community ownership, with Goods aiming to become unnecessary over time.

## Claims To Avoid

- Do not say Goods is selling biodiversity credits.
- Do not say Goods is an offset.
- Do not say plastic diversion equals biodiversity improvement.
- Do not claim audited plastic diversion unless the underlying weights are verified.
- Do not claim health outcomes such as reduced rheumatic heart disease without an independent study.
- Do not treat demand signals as signed orders.
- Do not use named stories, portraits or videos without checking consent and audience approval.

## Ask Options

**Soft ask:** Could Marketplace for Nature advise whether Goods is suitable for the initiatives map, the resource library, or a partner conversation?

**Partnership ask:** Could Pollination Foundation introduce Goods to Indigenous-led nature-market projects that need practical circular-economy infrastructure, asset tracking or local manufacturing pathways?

**Resource ask:** Could Goods contribute a short practical resource on circular economy readiness for remote Indigenous communities: from plastic waste to locally useful products?

**Pilot ask:** Could Marketplace for Nature help identify one Indigenous community, ranger group, council or Aboriginal corporation interested in a pilot that combines plastic recovery, Stretch Bed production, asset tracking and consent-led impact measurement?

## Strongest Framing Sentence

Goods is not trying to turn Country into a commodity. We are trying to help communities turn waste, broken supply chains and failed goods into durable local infrastructure, with the governance and evidence needed to engage nature markets only where communities choose to.

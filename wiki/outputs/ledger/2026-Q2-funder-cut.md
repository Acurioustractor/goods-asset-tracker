## Five voices, five things a report cannot show on its own

**Rest and health.** Ray Nelson, Plenty Highway (bed GB0-156-96).
> "Since receiving their new beds, they are no longer experiencing back pains."

A lived-experience account, presented as such, not a clinical result.

**Dignity and safety.** Alfred Johnson, Palm Island.
> "You have to bring them on the barge. You can't just take them on the boat. You have to pay for freight. It all adds up."

The freight tax in one sentence: why a basic good is out of reach. Photo: `v2/public/images/people/alfred-johnson.jpg`.

**Community-led design.** Linda Turner, Tennant Creek.
> "We've never been asked what sort of house we'd like to live in."

The reason the design happens in community. Photo: `v2/public/images/people/linda-turner.jpg`.

**Youth and the path to ownership.** Kristy Bloomfield, Oonchiumpa, Alice Springs.
> "We want to create a safe space for our young people. There's a lack of housing, which leads to a lack of sleep, which leads to low school attendance."

The chain the work is built to break, named by the lead Indigenous partner.

**The clinical frame (practitioner, not a recipient).** Dr Boe Remenyi, cardiologist.
> "It's great to say you should wash your sheets every week. But if you don't have a washing machine, that's not going to work."

Why a washing machine is health hardware.

## What the ledger shows (canon only)

- 496 beds deployed across 9 communities (`bedsDeployed`, `communitiesServed`). Of those, 133 are the current Stretch Bed and 363 are the earlier Basket Bed (`stretchBedsDeployed`, `basketBedsDeployed`).
- 16 washing machines in community (`washersInCommunity`).
- 2,660kg of HDPE plastic diverted, at 20kg per bed, Stretch beds only (`plasticKg`, modelled).

These are deployment and diversion numbers, verified against the asset register. They are not health outcomes. A reduction in rheumatic heart disease is not claimed. That measure waits on a partner method with Miwatj and equivalent ACCHOs.

Date: 2026-05-29
Status: Built for founder and legal review

Primary question: Does the enterprise's legal structure enable delivery of its strategic objectives and vision?

Important: this is a legal-readiness artifact, not legal advice. It should be used to brief founders, SIH/QBE advisors, Standard Ledger and lawyers. Anything funder-facing needs founder/legal review before use.

## Meeting-ready read

Area 09 should be presented as partially mature and high-priority.

Goods trades today through Nicholas Marchesi as sole trader (ABN 21 591 780 066), with the go-forward trading home being A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd (ACN 697 347 676, t/a Goods on Country) which the whole operation migrates to this financial year (FY2026-27). The gap is not lack of thinking. The gap is that the structure is still mid-migration: entity roles, contracting party during migration, IP ownership, related-party flows, community ownership triggers and mission-protection mechanisms need to be written down and lawyer-reviewed before capital, DGR donations or ownership-transfer claims are made externally.

The clean line for QBE is: "We know the structure has to carry three jobs: trading and product risk, charitable/public-benefit funding, and future community-controlled production. We have the entities confirmed and the migration underway; the next phase is to paper the entity map, IP/licence logic, benefit-share and trigger points so community ownership is real, not rhetorical."

## Diagnostic summary

The diagnostic credits Goods with:

- A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd (ACN 697 347 676, t/a Goods on Country) as the confirmed go-forward trading vehicle for GOC, with all operations migrating to it this financial year.
- A founder vision for a hub-and-spoke model with community-controlled or co-operatively owned operating entities running production, recycling, sales and maintenance locally.
- A parent/shared-services function that can hold IP, attract capital and provide support.

The diagnostic gaps are:

- The trading company is still being migrated to from the current sole-trader operating base, which means contracting/applicant entity per grant during the transition is still being settled.
- Specialist legal advice is needed to map realistic community-ownership options and the trigger points for moving from one structure to another.
- Mission-protection mechanisms should be considered as GOC matures, including B Corp or equivalent, social-purpose clauses and cultural-protection provisions.

## IP / licence / benefit-share memo v0.1

The IP issue is not administrative. It decides whether community ownership is real.

IP and rights that need a register:

- Goods on Country brand and trademarks.
- Stretch Bed design, drawings, files, BOM, tooling and QA process.
- Basket Bed open-source materials and licence terms.
- Washing machine design, telemetry and the Pakkimjalki Kari name.
- Plant design, shredder/press/CNC workflow, production SOPs and training material.
- Community stories, photographs, videos, quotes and consent records.
- Data: asset register, QR scans, support records, household/location data and Empathy Ledger material.
- Supplier, buyer, funder and production contracts.

Legal questions:

- Who currently owns each asset: founder/sole trader, A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd, Butterfly, contractor, community partner or mixed ownership? (A Kind Tractor Ltd is dormant and not used, so it is not an owner.)
- Were contractor designs assigned to the right entity?
- What design knowledge came from the Bloomfield family, Dianne Stokes or other community partners?
- What should be open-sourced, licensed, retained centrally or transferred to community entities?
- What benefit-share or use conditions protect community input and prevent extractive IP use?
- Who signs warranty and product safety obligations if a community entity manufactures locally?

Practical first artifact:

- Create a one-page IP register with columns: asset, current owner, evidence, community contribution, intended future owner/user, licence/benefit-share model, consent/cultural sign-off needed, external-use status.

## Mission protection note v0.1

Current mission protection is mostly relational and cultural: founder intent, community relationships, advisory support, story/consent practice and repeated public commitment to become unnecessary.

That is strong early-stage practice. It is not enough for larger capital.

Mission-protection options to review with legal/advisors:

- Purpose clauses in company constitution.
- Shareholder agreement or special rights that protect community purpose.
- Reserved matters/veto rights for community or Aboriginal-led governance body.
- Aboriginal-majority or community-controlled membership model for Butterfly if it becomes the charitable home.
- Related-party policy and conflicts register.
- IP licence conditions that protect community design input.
- Equipment/plant transfer triggers.
- B Corp or purpose certification only if it adds real governance value.
- Benefit-share / community-reinvestment policy.
- Data sovereignty and consent clauses in contracts.

We brought the beds. The rest was theirs.

A few days in the Utopia Homelands, building beds with young people and sitting with Elders. The start of a longer relationship, not a delivery.

Arriving

We brought the beds to Utopia. From there it was not ours to run.

The community workers said where they went and who they were for. Our job on a trip like this is small and specific. Turn up, build, listen, and do what we are asked.

Lids. Canvas. Steel.

Everything this bed is made of was already here, or already thrown away.

Mykel · Utopia Homelands

He kept turning it over, like he couldn't quite trust it.

Never would've thought it would've come out like that.Mykel · Utopia Homelands

It came out of lids. Bottle caps and scraps of plastic headed for the tip, shredded and pressed until they were strong enough to stand on.

Two steel poles slide through the canvas. The legs click into place. That is the whole bed.

In Utopia, on Country, a young fella threaded the poles, clicked the legs, and built the bed he would sleep on that night.

Then he built another. Then another. Seven in total.

We asked him the one question that matters when you are the one who has to lie down on it. Is it comfortable?

Comfortable as. Smooth, tight, hard, fancy. It's not trampoline.Mykel

An Elder was watching him work. He called him grandson. He called him brother.

Well done, grandson. Well done, brother.An Elder, watching

And then, after a pause:

That could be a good employment for yourself too, grandson. Later on.An Elder, watching

So we asked Mykel the question that is really a door. If we built a place here to make these all the time, would you come?

Yeah, I'll be rocking up every day to make them.Mykel

Waste into rest.

A pile of lids into a bed.

A morning into a trade.

The close community

Then they told us where to go next. A community close by wanted beds too, more of them.

We did not plan that leg. They did. We drove where we were pointed, and we unloaded.

Ampilatwatja

We went to Ampilatwatja to sit with the old fellas. Two of them carry the Order of Australia, given to them this year.

We brought four beds. They had something to say about them, so we let the camera run, and then we got out of the way.

Before, and after

Before, some people slept on a door laid flat on the ground. A door, off its hinges, for a bed.

Now the door is a door again, and the bed is a bed. Off the ground. Washable. Built to last ten years.

Off the ground.

A bed is rest. Rest is healing. The simple things are not small.

What comes next

Utopia is one of the strongest candidates for the next place where these beds get made, not just delivered.

Mykel said he'd turn up every day. An Elder called it employment. The next question is whether the making moves here, to the people who already want it.

This is the first thing he built.

It is not the last thing we will build together.

# Repayable / Concessional Finance Shortlist for the QBE Match

Date: 2026-06-20

## Why this list is the one that matters

The QBE Foundation "Catalysing Impact" Stage 2 grant (up to AU$400,000, AU$150K floor, ~September 2026 application, ~November 2026 outcome) is NOT secured. To win it, Goods must assemble at least AU$400K of SIGNED, legally-binding EXTERNAL capital FIRST, and QBE PRIORITISES repayable finance (loans, concessional debt, blended) ABOVE grants when it scores the match. So repayable commitments are worth more per dollar than grants. This list ranks ONLY repayable / concessional / blended / guarantee sources by how realistically each could produce a SIGNED, match-eligible loan commitment before the September application.

THE CROSS-CUTTING GATE: many of the largest, cleanest repayable lines (IBA, First Australians Capital, the NAB/IBA Guarantee, CAGES corpus) require the BORROWER to be 50%+ First Nations owned and actively First-Nations-managed. Goods trades through A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd, which is NOT currently majority Indigenous-owned, and the 51% ownership structure is the explicitly-undecided keystone blocker (the MinterEllison entity work begins only after the Butterfly charity handover lands, ~end July 2026). Until that is resolved, those lines are post-September, not Sept anchors. Resolving the entity question (or identifying a 50%+ Indigenous-owned local production vehicle as the borrower) is therefore the single highest-leverage move and gates roughly half the repayable universe.

A second gate (for White Box SELF specifically): PBI + DGR + social-enterprise certification, which routes through the Butterfly Movement and/or Supply Nation registration.

# Goods on Country: Master Funder and Financier Register for the QBE $400K Match

Date compiled: 2026-06-20

## What this register is for

Goods is assembling the capital to win the QBE Foundation "Catalysing Impact" Stage 2 grant: up to AU$400,000 from a roughly AU$1M shared pool, with an AU$150K floor. THE HARD RULE: the QBE money must be at least matched 1:1 by SIGNED, legally-binding EXTERNAL capital that Goods raises FIRST, and QBE PRIORITISES repayable finance (loans, concessional debt) above grants when it assesses the match. The application is around September 2026; the outcome is around November 2026.

Honest labels used throughout: the QBE grant is CONTINGENT and NOT secured. It is the prize being matched, never a banked figure. Match-eligibility means a source can produce a SIGNED commitment before the September application, and repayable beats grant in QBE's eyes. Goods administers tax-deductible giving via The Butterfly Movement Ltd (DGR); the go-forward trading entity work (including the open question of First Nations ownership) is still in flight, and it gates several of the strongest repayable lenders.

A cross-cutting blocker recurs below: the Indigenous-led / 50%+ First Nations ownership test. Goods trades through A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd (directors Benjamin Knight and Nicholas Marchesi OAM), which is NOT currently majority Indigenous-owned. That single unresolved fact disqualifies IBA, First Australians Capital, NAB/IBA Guarantee and CAGES as direct lenders to the current entity. Resolving it (the MinterEllison entity work, after the Butterfly charity handover lands around end July 2026) is the precondition for the largest, cleanest repayable lines.

# Win the $400K: QBE Catalysing Impact Stage 2 match playbook

Compiled 2026-06-20. Working document for Ben. Honest-label discipline applies throughout.

## The prize and the rule (read this first)

QBE Foundation Catalysing Impact Stage 2 is a grant of up to AU$400,000 from a roughly AU$1M shared pool, with an AU$150K floor. It is **CONTINGENT and NOT secured**. Application is around September 2026, outcome around November 2026. Nothing here treats QBE money as banked.

The hard rule: the QBE grant must be **at least matched 1:1 by SIGNED, legally-binding EXTERNAL capital that Goods raises FIRST**. **REPAYABLE finance (loans, concessional debt) is PRIORITISED above grants** when QBE/SIH assess the match. Social Impact Hub (SIH) administers the program and holds a 3-year right to co-invest on the same terms in future raises.

So winning the full $400K means assembling **at least AU$400,000 of signed, match-eligible external commitments, loans first, before the September application**. As of the latest canon the signed/eligible commitment register holds **0 signed entries** (`wiki/canon/qbe-readiness.md`, build queue item 6). This is a conversion problem, not a discovery problem.

### The one fact that reshapes everything below

Goods' trading entity is **A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd** (ACN 697 347 676, directors Benjamin Knight + Nicholas Marchesi), which is **NOT majority First Nations owned**. The 51% First Nations ownership structure is the **keystone legal blocker and is explicitly UNDECIDED**; the entity work only begins after the Butterfly Movement charity/DGR handover lands (expected around end July 2026, already slipped once). See `wiki/canon/qbe-readiness.md` build queue item 1 and catalytic blocker 1.

This matters because the strongest, most thesis-perfect repayable sources, **First Australians Capital (FAC) and Indigenous Business Australia (IBA)**, both **gate on majority First Nations ownership of the borrowing entity**. On current structure Goods almost certainly **fails their eligibility test**, so they cannot be banked as the match anchor for September. The playbook is built around lenders that do NOT require First Nations ownership of the borrower, with FAC/IBA pursued only on the partner-entity path or as a post-September line.

## 5. Risks and honesty: what could sink the match

- **Signed-vs-in-principle.** QBE needs SIGNED, legally-binding commitments. A warm "yes in principle", a verbal nod, or an unsigned grant decision letter does NOT count. **Mitigation:** every prospect must reach an executed facility or an executed binding LOI/term sheet; track each in the commitment register; confirm with Jay exactly what document QBE accepts (section 6).

- **Timing slippage on credit assessment.** First-time-borrower credit underwriting runs 2 to 4+ months; from a 20 June start, anything not opened this week may not sign by 31 August. **Mitigation:** open SEFA + White Box this week; have the financials pack ready day one; lead every call with the deadline so the lender timeboxes.

- **The ownership-gate trap (FAC/IBA).** Banking the match on FAC or IBA is the single biggest strategic error available, because both fail Goods' current non-majority-First-Nations structure. **Mitigation:** do not count them for September; get the one-email eligibility read; build the match on ownership-gate-free lenders.

- **Equity traps under K5/K7.** QBE excludes equity and anything needing an exit/liquidity event. Several catalytic players do BOTH debt and equity (Tripple, Conscious Investment Management, Giant Leap/ESVCLP). **Mitigation:** explicitly request the debt/recoverable/fixed-interest sleeve only; never let an equity instrument into the match column.

- **Double-counting revenue as match.** Centrecorp is both a grantor and a bed-buyer; a bed order is REVENUE, not match capital. Counting it twice would be a credibility failure with SIH. **Mitigation:** split grant from order in writing; only the grant counts toward the match.

- **The SIH co-invest clause (cl5.3).** SIH holds a 3-year right to co-invest on the same terms in future raises. **Mitigation:** confirm with Jay how cl5.3 interacts with the match round, whether SIH co-investment counts as match, and whether it constrains future term-setting with other investors; surface it to the board before signing.

- **Self-originated debt may not count.** An IBA loan (Goods borrowing against itself) is leverage, not third-party matching capital, and QBE may not accept it. **Mitigation:** confirm with Jay in writing before relying on any self-originated facility.

- **Charity-handover dependency.** The White Box DGR route and any charity-side capital depend on the Butterfly Movement DGR landing (expected end July, already slipped once). **Mitigation:** pursue Goods-Pty-Ltd-direct routes (SEFA, Bank Australia) in parallel so the match does not hinge on the charity timeline.

- **QBE itself is contingent.** Even a fully assembled, signed $400K match does NOT guarantee the award (shared pool, roughly $1M, competitive, outcome around November). **Mitigation:** label QBE as contingent in all internal and external comms; the signed match capital is genuinely useful to Goods regardless of the QBE outcome.

## 6. What to confirm with QBE/SIH (the open Jay questions)

These rule questions change the plan; resolve them in writing before over-committing the 10-week runway.

1. **Exact Stage 2 application date** (and the hard close for match evidence).
2. **What counts as "signed match"?** Executed facility only, or does a binding term sheet / executed LOI qualify? What document does QBE want to see?
3. **Repayable-vs-grant weighting.** How is the 1:1 measured? Does a higher repayable proportion improve the assessment, or is any signed eligible dollar equal at 1:1? Is there a minimum repayable share?
4. **Does self-originated debt count?** A loan Goods takes against itself (IBA) vs a third-party co-investment (SEFA alongside QBE). Are both "external" for the match?
5. **Evidence required** for each committed dollar (executed agreement, lender letter, board resolution, etc.).
6. **Aggregation rules.** Can multiple sub-floor grants (VFFF + FRRR + TFN) aggregate toward the floor, or must a single commitment clear $150K?
7. **The cl5.3 SIH 3-year co-invest clause.** How does it interact with the match round and with terms Goods sets for other investors?
8. **Conflict-of-interest note.** SIH administers QBE Catalysing Impact AND administers SEDI capability grants and runs an Impact Investing Hub. Confirm there is no conflict in SIH-adjacent capital counting toward a match SIH assesses.

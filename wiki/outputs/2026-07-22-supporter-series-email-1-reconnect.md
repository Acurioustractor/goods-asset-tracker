## Body

Some of you we haven't written to in far too long. That's on us, not on you. Here is where things got to while it was quiet.

A Stretch Bed is a washable, flat-packable bed. 20kg of recycled plastic, heavy canvas and galvanised steel. It goes together in about five minutes with no tools, and it is built to last more than ten years in places where a cheap bed lasts months.

There are now 540 beds on the ground, across 11 communities. 22 washing machines as well, the ones a Warumungu Elder in Tennant Creek, Dianne Stokes, named Pakkimjalki Kari. And 3,540kg of plastic kept out of landfill and turned into something a family sleeps on.

Here is the part we are proudest of. We have started pressing the plastic ourselves, On Country. The next step is the one the whole thing was built for: the plant that makes the beds moving into community hands. Four communities are already asking for their own.

Over the coming weeks we are going to introduce you to some of them. The communities. The people. The young people choosing a day's work building something their family needs. One at a time, told properly.

If Goods still means something to you, stay with us. Hit reply and tell us where you are at. And if someone you know should be hearing this, send it their way.

From Nic and the Goods team

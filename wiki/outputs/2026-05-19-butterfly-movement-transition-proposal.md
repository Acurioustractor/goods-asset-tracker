# Butterfly Movement Ltd transition proposal

Purpose: invite the current directors and members to consider a respectful transition of stewardship so the charity's DGR status, constitution and public-benefit purpose can help Goods on Country scale community-led essential goods production.

[Goods on Country: Beds That Change Lives](https://www.goodsoncountry.com/)

## 1. The proposal

Goods is seeking to work with **The Butterfly Movement Ltd** on a positive transition of the charity into a new chapter: becoming the charitable home for **Goods on Country**.

Subject to legal, ACNC, DGR and governance due diligence, we propose:

- registering **Goods on Country Pty Ltd** as the mission-led trading and operating company
- transitioning **The Butterfly Movement Ltd** into the aligned charity / DGR entity that supports access, relief, governance, evidence and community capability
- building an Aboriginal-led board and transition plan that protects the charity's purpose while increasing its impact
- using the charity to receive and steward philanthropic support already being explored for Goods
- supporting Aboriginal-owned and operated production entities On Country, with Goods on Country providing product systems, training, logistics, evidence and back-office capability

This is similar in principle to the social enterprise / charitable support model used by organisations like **TABOO**: a trading enterprise builds the product engine, while a charitable vehicle enables deeper public benefit, access, education, relief and philanthropic participation.

## 2. Goods on Country at a glance

Goods on Country builds **repairable, washable, fit-for-place essential goods with remote First Nations communities**.

The first flagship product is the **Stretch Bed**: a durable, flat-packed bed made from recycled plastic, galvanised steel and heavy-duty canvas. It is designed for remote conditions, assembles without tools, and can be repaired and supported over time.

Current proof points:

- 369+ beds delivered
- 8 communities served
- 20kg of plastic diverted per bed
- containerised On-Country manufacturing pathway that turns community plastic waste into bed components
- community validation through storytellers and lived-experience feedback across remote communities

The broader Goods on Country vision is to build the missing layer of **household infrastructure** that many remote communities are expected to live without: beds, washing machines, fridges, storage, repair systems and other practical goods that make daily life healthier, safer and more dignified.

Goods on Country starts with the idea that essential products should be:

- designed with community, not imported as generic solutions
- durable enough for remote conditions, where freight, repair and replacement are expensive
- repairable and washable, so products stay useful for years rather than weeks
- locally makeable, so production can create jobs, skills and enterprise On Country
- circular, using materials like recycled plastic and reducing waste
- tracked and supported, so every product has a service pathway, not just a delivery date

The work is deliberately practical. A bed is not just furniture: it supports sleep, dignity, skin health, school readiness and family wellbeing. A washing machine is not just an appliance: it supports hygiene, infection prevention, care work, independence and household stability. A fridge is not just a whitegood: it supports food security, medicine storage and daily health.

Goods on Country is therefore both a product platform and a self-determination platform. The ambition is to move from outside delivery to local production: Aboriginal-owned and operated enterprises making, repairing and improving the goods their own communities need, with Goods on Country providing the design system, tools, training, finance pathways, evidence and back-office support behind them.

## 3. Alignment with The Butterfly Movement constitution

The Butterfly Movement's constitution is strongly aligned with Goods on Country's purpose. The objects include:

- providing assistance to disadvantaged persons
- creating opportunities to improve life situations
- developing self-help programs within communities
- providing practical solutions for real problems
- building self-esteem
- providing goods and funds to disadvantaged persons where there is need
- raising funds to achieve the objects
- applying the property and capacity of the company toward those objects

Goods on Country is a practical expression of these objects.

We are not proposing charity as handout. We are proposing charity as capability: good products, local making, repair, training, ownership and dignity.

## 4. Why this matters now

Goods on Country has generated field proof, community demand, product development and philanthropic interest. The missing piece is the right charitable structure to receive and steward support at the speed the opportunity now requires.

Through the QBE Catalysing Impact work, Goods on Country is actively developing its investment readiness, legal structure, capital stack, governance and operating model. The current pipeline shows strong demand for essential goods across remote communities, with a growing need for a clean DGR pathway to receive philanthropic capital and direct it toward public benefit.

A DGR-enabled charity would allow proposed philanthropic support to rapidly increase the project's ability to:

- deliver more essential goods to households and communities in need
- support Aboriginal-led production and employment pathways
- build container-based local manufacturing capability
- subsidise goods where commercial procurement is not yet available
- collect evidence on health, dignity, waste and economic outcomes
- support local Aboriginal-owned operators rather than centralising production away from community

## 5. Proposed operating model

The long-term goal is a network of locally controlled production and service entities.

**Goods on Country Pty Ltd would provide the product engine:**

- product design and technical development
- manufacturing systems and quality control
- training, tooling and support
- procurement, logistics and warranty systems
- storytelling, sales and buyer relationships
- back-office capability and evidence systems

**The Butterfly Movement Ltd would provide the charitable and public-benefit engine:**

- receive philanthropic and DGR support
- fund access for people and communities experiencing disadvantage
- support community-led production readiness
- fund training, evidence, governance and capability building
- protect the charitable purpose and public benefit
- help keep the work Aboriginal-led, community-accountable and mission-locked

**Community-led production entities would provide the local ownership engine:**

- Aboriginal-owned and operated production On Country
- local jobs, repair capability and skills transfer
- place-based decision-making
- community control over production, data, stories and future enterprise value

## 8. A positive transition plan

We would like to work with the current directors and members of The Butterfly Movement Ltd in a respectful, staged and supported way.

**Stage 1: Shared intent and fit**

- meet with current directors and members
- confirm alignment with the constitution and charitable purpose
- understand the charity's history, obligations, assets, liabilities, members and compliance status
- agree on principles for a respectful transition

**Stage 2: Legal and governance due diligence**

- confirm ACNC, ASIC, DGR and tax requirements
- review membership, director appointment and constitutional requirements
- confirm whether any amendments, member resolutions or ACNC notifications are needed
- design conflict-of-interest, related-party and charity/trading-arm safeguards

**Stage 3: Transition of stewardship**

- appoint agreed new directors in a staged way
- retain appropriate outgoing director support during handover
- transfer records, registers, governance documents and compliance knowledge
- establish new board cadence, policies and reporting

**Stage 4: Launch the next chapter**

- confirm the Goods on Country charity strategy
- activate philanthropic support and donor communications
- publish a clear public story about continuity, purpose and future impact
- begin funding community-led production, access and capability-building

**Stage 5: Ongoing support and impact reporting**

- provide regular reporting to the board, donors and communities
- track outcomes across goods delivered, households supported, local jobs, repairs, waste diverted and community ownership
- review governance and community accountability every six months during the first year

## 9. Invitation

We are seeking to respectfully steward an existing charitable platform into a new chapter that is deeply aligned with its constitution.

The opportunity is to turn The Butterfly Movement Ltd into the charitable home for Goods on Country: a vehicle that supports Aboriginal-led production, practical relief, self-determination, appropriate technology and social enterprise.

We would welcome a conversation with The Butterfly Movement Ltd directors and members about a staged transition that protects the charity's purpose, honours its history and rapidly increases its capacity to help people.

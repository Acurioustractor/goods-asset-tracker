## 1. Thesis
Goods makes quality, washable, flat-packable furniture for remote Indigenous communities from recycled plastic — and is moving production On-Country, toward community ownership. **496 beds are already in 9 communities. The unit economics work. We're raising catalytic capital to build the production capability that turns a proven pilot into a community-owned enterprise.**

## 2. The ask
- **~AU$425K** of signed, match-eligible capital, structured to unlock the **QBE catalytic match (up to AU$400K — contingent, not confirmed; do not quote the cap externally without sign-off)**.
- **Timeline:** first signed match-eligible commitment before **31 Aug 2026.** SEFA credit assessment runs 2-4 months — open now.
- **Status today: AU$0 signed.** This is the gate the raise turns on.

## 3. Why now / the problem
Remote communities receive furniture (and white goods) that fails fast in remote conditions and ends up in local waste streams. Goods replaces that with a bed engineered for a **10+ year lifespan**, built from the plastic those communities are trying to divert — and the production can move to the community itself.

## 4. What it is
- **The Stretch Bed** — flat-packable, washable, recycled-HDPE X-trestle legs + galvanised steel poles + heavy-duty canvas; 200 kg capacity; ~5-min no-tools assembly. **The only direct-sale product, AU$750.**
- Each bed diverts ~20 kg of HDPE *(design spec, not yet weighbridge-verified)*.

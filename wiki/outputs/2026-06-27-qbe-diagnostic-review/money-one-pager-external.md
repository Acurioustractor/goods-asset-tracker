## What Goods is
A social enterprise delivering quality, washable, flat-packable furniture to remote Indigenous communities across Australia. The flagship **Stretch Bed** is made from recycled HDPE plastic, galvanised steel and heavy-duty Australian canvas — built for remote conditions, with a pathway toward community-owned, On-Country manufacturing.

## The traction *(to date)*
- **496 beds deployed** across **9 communities served.**
- **~2,660 kg of HDPE diverted** *(modelled: 133 Stretch Beds × the 20 kg/bed design spec, which is not yet weighbridge-verified).*
- A proven product in country, a live public storefront, and 32 community voices consent-cleared to tell the story.

## The model
- **The Stretch Bed is the only direct-sale product — AU$750.** A clear unit, a real price, with a buyer pathway through direct and partner channels.
- **Revenue (management figure): AU$713,827, Goods-only basis** *(not audited; whole-of-entity figures differ and are not Goods-only).*
- **Unit economics (cost-model v6, computed):** marginal **~AU$426/bed** for in-source (factory) production — versus ~AU$685/bed buying kits today — against an annual fixed block of **~AU$109,500** (today's run-rate), rising to **~AU$133,500** for On-Country production. Bed-level bill-of-materials is largely verified; facility/site capex is **modelled pending vendor quotes.**

## The opportunity
Stand up On-Country Stretch Bed production at scale — moving from project delivery toward a transferable, community-owned production capability. Each bed diverts plastic from remote-community waste streams and is designed for a 10+ year lifespan in remote conditions.

## The raise
We are assembling a **blended catalytic capital stack** to fund the production scale-up, structured to unlock matched philanthropic capital. *(Match terms are contingent and being confirmed; this one-pager does not represent committed capital or a finalised facility.)*
- **Use of funds:** On-Country production facility (equipment + build), a 50-bed in-source production run, and the working capital to convert demand into delivery.

## Why now
The product is proven in country — 496 beds deployed across 9 communities — and the production model is ready to move from pilot to scale. Catalytic capital at this point compounds: it builds an asset that communities can ultimately own and operate.

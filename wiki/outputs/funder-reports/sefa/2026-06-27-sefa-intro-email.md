## Subject
Goods on Country: impact finance enquiry (recycled-plastic beds, made On Country)

## Cover email

Hello SEFA team,

I'm Ben Knight, co-founder of Goods on Country. We make quality, washable, flat-packable furniture for remote Indigenous communities, built from recycled plastic. Our flagship Stretch Bed is now in 9 communities, with 496 beds deployed and a live public storefront behind it.

We're getting in touch because Goods sits squarely inside both of SEFA's impact streams, Aboriginal Community Enterprises and Community Environment Enterprises, and we'd like to open a conversation about a repayable facility of around AU$300,000 to bring our bed production On Country.

The case is a structural cost-down. Today we buy our bed kits in at about AU$685 each. Pressing the recycled-plastic components ourselves brings that down to about AU$426, a saving of roughly AU$260 on every bed, while building a production asset a community can ultimately own and operate. (Figures are management data, not audited; the attached brief sets out the full picture.)

We're moving now because we're assembling a blended catalytic stack, and a philanthropic match we're working toward turns on a first signed commitment landing before the end of August. SEFA is the anchor we would most like to build that around, which is why we'd value getting the credit conversation started early, knowing a first facility can take a few months to assess.

I've attached a short brief covering the ask, the unit economics, how the loan is repaid, and the security. We'd welcome a short call to confirm a few things: whether AU$300,000 to AU$400,000 sits inside your current band, your appetite for remote NT and WA manufacturing, a realistic timeline for a first-time borrower, and indicative rate and security.

Thank you for the work you do. I'd love to find a time to talk.

Warm regards,
Ben Knight
Co-founder, Goods on Country
[phone] · [email] · goodsoncountry.com

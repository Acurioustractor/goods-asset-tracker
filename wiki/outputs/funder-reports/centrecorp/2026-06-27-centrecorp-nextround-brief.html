What your backing built, and the next round.

Ahead of your June board, one request: that Centrecorp's next round of beds for Central Australia be a decision the board can make this month, and that it count as committed support we can build further capital behind.

First, what your support already did

Three weeks ago we delivered 87 Stretch Beds across Utopia Homelands over two days, working with the Oonchiumpa Consultancy and the Utopia Council. Thirty-six went straight into households; the rest went to community institutions to place on their own terms. That run kept 1.74 tonnes of plastic out of the community dump. Every bed carries a QR tag, so you can follow each one in the register rather than take our word for it.

The honest position, the ask, the timing
Confidential draft · 27 June 2026
Where the business honestly sits

We will be straight, because Centrecorp has earned that. Goods is about 89% grant-funded today, by design. We have proven communities want the bed and that funders will back deployments. What we have not yet proven is that we can make the beds cheaply enough to stand on their own. The next step is a 50-bed run made end to end, costed at about AU$60,000 to $80,000, that turns our central cost claim from modelled to measured.

The ask

Commit to the next round of Stretch Beds for Aboriginal families and young people in Central Australia (the consolidated board package proposes ~130 beds; confirm the next-round amount). As before, this is restricted to Central Australian benefit: beds delivered with local partners, allocated transparently, with photos, counts and feedback reported back to you. No part of it funds product development or work outside Central Australia.

Why the timing matters
  • Your board meets this month. A clear decision then lets us plan delivery properly.
  • A separate unrestricted contribution, kept distinct from any bed order, could count as match-eligible external capital under QBE's Catalysing Impact program, provided a commitment is in place before 31 August 2026 and the match rules are confirmed in writing. A bed purchase is revenue, not match. Today we hold no signed match.
The real ask: a board decision in the next few weeks

Everything that backs this is in the consolidated 26 June board package and the live register. We can walk the board through any of it, and bring the per-bed records and the cost work to whatever depth helps. Thank you for backing this from the start.

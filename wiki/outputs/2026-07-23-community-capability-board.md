# Goods on Country: community capability map

**Working board source, 23 July 2026**

## The simplest story

Goods on Country does not arrive with one fixed program or sell every community the same factory.

We listen to what a community wants to make possible, then assemble the smallest useful combination of equipment, product, training, logistics, finance and story support. A community can begin with one module, prove it in practice, add the next module when it is useful, and progressively hold more of the capability, margin, knowledge and decisions.

**The funder proposition:** fund a community-defined step, see the practical result, learn from it, and back the next step when the community asks for it.

**The Goods role:** the vehicle that helps communities price, procure, deploy, operate, maintain, measure and tell the story of what they chose.

## Board reading path

Build the live board left to right:

1. **Start with the community**
2. **Choose only what is useful now**
3. **Price the complete capability**
4. **Deliver and learn together**
5. **Return evidence and story**
6. **Grow, pause or transfer**

The main loop sits across the centre of the board. Community examples sit beneath it. The media and evidence library sits below the examples. Funder-ready slides sit across the top.

## Frame 1: The proposition

### Headline

**Start where the community is. Build only what they need.**

### Supporting line

Goods on Country is a modular pathway from a practical need to community-held capability.

### Three proof points

- 540 beds across 11 communities (current code canon).
- 40 Maningrida Stretch Beds were pressed at the existing facility. In-house pressing is demonstrated, although production-rate cost is not yet measured.
- Every Stretch Bed can be QR-tracked through delivery, use, feedback and repair.

### Guardrail

Do not claim that every community wants manufacturing, or that ownership transfer is complete. The offer is a pathway, chosen and paced by community.

## Frame 2: The live community loop

### 1. Listen

What does the community want to make possible now?

- A useful product
- A waste problem solved
- Equipment
- Local paid work
- Repair capability
- A full production enterprise
- A story that can unlock the next step

### 2. Scope

Define the smallest useful first step.

- Who asked?
- What problem are they solving?
- Who will use or hold the capability?
- What local assets and skills already exist?
- What is the first 90-day proof?
- What should not be included yet?

### 3. Assemble modules

Choose the relevant modules from the capability menu below.

### 4. Price the whole capability

The price is not just a machine.

**Complete price = equipment + freight + site readiness + safety + training + paid local participation + first production run + maintenance + measurement + story return + contingency**

### 5. Deliver and learn

- Procure and freight
- Install safely
- Train local operators
- Run a real first job
- Record time, cost, quality and issues
- Adjust the next step with community

### 6. Prove and return

Create a short evidence pack:

- What the community asked for
- What was funded
- What happened
- Who participated
- What changed
- What was learned
- What the community wants next

### 7. Grow, pause or transfer

There is no forced graduation.

- Keep the module as-is
- Add another module
- Share equipment across places
- Build a local enterprise
- Move contracts, margin, knowledge and decisions into community hands

## Frame 3: The capability menu

### Module A: Listen, design and scope

**Community receives:** facilitated scoping, local asset map, practical brief, first-step design.

**Goods provides:** listening process, technical translation, pathway design, partner coordination.

**Evidence:** signed-off community brief and a defined 90-day proof.

**Pricing status:** to model as a fixed scoping package plus travel.

### Module B: Product access and delivery

**Community receives:** Stretch Beds, assembly, delivery, allocation support and repair pathway.

**Goods provides:** product supply, freight planning, local assembly support, QR tracking and reporting.

**Evidence:** bed-level delivery records, partner sign-off, feedback and repairs.

**Known anchor:** Stretch Bed selling price is $750. Delivery and field costs must be priced separately by destination.

### Module C: Collection and shred

**Community receives:** a local plastic collection and shredding capability.

**Goods provides:** equipment specification, procurement, freight, setup, safe operating procedures, operator training, feedstock standards and maintenance plan.

**Evidence:** kilograms collected and processed, contamination rate, paid hours, uptime, output quality and next buyer/use.

**Current equipment range:** shredder $15K to $30K, modelled and pending vendor quotes.

**Critical design question:** where does the shredded material go? Never fund a shredder without a reliable offtake or next production step.

### Module D: Assembly and repair hub

**Community receives:** tools, spares, benches and the capability to assemble, maintain and repair products locally.

**Goods provides:** workflow, training, parts system, quality checks and repair reporting.

**Evidence:** beds assembled, repairs completed, response time, paid local hours and avoided freight.

**Pricing status:** benches are modelled at about $2K. Tools, spares, freight, training and wages still require a site-specific budget.

### Module E: Press and make

**Community receives:** the capability to press recycled plastic into bed components or sheets.

**Goods provides:** press specification, tooling, recipes, safety system, training, quality control and measured production run.

**Evidence:** cycle time, cost per sheet or bed, quality pass rate, paid hours and material yield.

**Current equipment range:** hot press $80K to $150K, modelled and pending vendor quotes.

### Module F: Cut and finish

**Community receives:** CNC cutting and finishing capability.

**Goods provides:** CNC specification, toolpaths, fixtures, dust and safety systems, training and quality control.

**Evidence:** cut time, yield, defects, maintenance and operator capability.

**Current equipment range:** CNC $15K to $40K, modelled and pending vendor quotes.

### Module G: Full production facility

**Community receives:** an integrated shred, press, cut, assemble and quality-control facility.

**Goods provides:** plant design, equipment, site integration, training, first production run, operating model, demand pathway and transfer plan.

**Evidence:** measured cost per bed, throughput, quality, paid local hours, plastic used, sales or committed demand, margin retained locally.

**Current gross equipment range:** $112K to $222K, modelled. $110,046 has already been invested in the existing facility. Do not use that prior investment to understate the cost of a separate community site.

**Volume gate:** major plant expenditure should follow credible demand. The current model uses roughly 300+ committed beds per year as the gate.

### Module H: Workforce and enterprise

**Community receives:** paid learning, operator roles, enterprise support and a pathway to hold contracts and margin.

**Goods provides:** role design, training, safe work systems, production coaching, unit economics, buyer pathway and governance support.

**Evidence:** people trained, paid hours, qualifications or demonstrated competencies, retention, local wages and local surplus.

**Modelled community path:** about $130 fair-wage labour per bed. This is not yet a measured result.

### Module I: Demand and finance

**Community receives:** a bankable first order or demand pathway, plus a fit-for-purpose capital stack.

**Goods provides:** buyer and funder packaging, quote-backed budgets, demand commitments, grant and finance structure, and reporting.

**Evidence:** signed demand, funded budget, delivery milestones and repayment or sustainability logic where relevant.

### Module J: Story, evidence and learning

**Community receives:** consent-led documentation and a reusable case study that belongs in the relationship, not an extractive marketing asset.

**Goods provides:** photos, video, interviews, asset data, outcomes reporting, a short deck and a live map update.

**Evidence:** a community-approved story, an honest funder report, reusable media with recorded consent and a clear next ask.

## Frame 4: Community pathway cards

### Working story A: Jane at Utopia wants a shredder

**Status:** working hypothesis from Ben's brief. Validate Jane's name, role, authority to request, exact place, intended plastic stream and permission to use the story.

**What she is asking for:** local ability to turn suitable waste plastic into clean, usable flake.

**Smallest useful pathway:**

1. Listen and scope
2. Waste and feedstock audit
3. Confirm offtake or next production step
4. Quote a suitable shredder
5. Freight, power, shelter and safety readiness
6. Operator training and paid first run
7. Maintenance and spare-parts plan
8. Measure kilograms, uptime, contamination and paid hours
9. Community-approved 90-day field note

**Starting budget logic:**

- Shredder: $15K to $30K, modelled pending quotes
- Plus freight to site
- Plus electrical and site works
- Plus guarding, PPE and safe work system
- Plus training and paid local operator time
- Plus first-run support and maintenance spares
- Plus measurement, consent and story return
- Plus contingency

**Do not call the machine price the project price.**

**Funder story:** "Jane did not ask for a factory. She asked for the useful next piece. The funder backed a defined capability, and the next decision belongs to Utopia."

### Working story B: "Nchumba" wants the whole production facility

**Status:** working hypothesis from Ben's brief. Confirm whether this refers to Oonchiumpa, and validate the requested scope with the organisation before external use.

**What they may be asking for:** an integrated production enterprise that can turn plastic into finished Stretch Beds and retain capability, work and margin locally.

**Readiness gates before pricing the plant:**

- Community and organisational mandate
- Named site and tenure
- Suitable power, water, access and storage
- Operators and local governance
- Credible demand, with roughly 300+ beds/year as the current model gate
- Equipment vendor quotes
- Freight and installation plan
- Safe work and maintenance system
- Working capital for material, wages and first runs
- Buyer, contracting and margin pathway
- Clear transfer and commercial-rights pathway

**Starting budget logic:**

- Shredder: $15K to $30K
- Hot press: $80K to $150K
- CNC: $15K to $40K
- Benches: about $2K
- Gross equipment subtotal: $112K to $222K, modelled
- Plus site works, freight, installation, safety, training, wages, working capital, measured first run, maintenance, demand development, governance, evidence and contingency

**Do not present $112K to $222K as the complete site price.**

**First proof:** a measured 50-bed run that records cost, cycle time, quality and paid hours. Current target budget for this proof is about $60K to $80K.

**Funder story:** "The return is not the container. The return is a functioning community-held production pathway: equipment, knowledge, demand, margin and decisions moving together."

### Proven story template: Utopia bed delivery

Use the existing Centrecorp and Utopia material as the first completed case study:

- Community-defined delivery and distribution
- 79 of 109 Utopia beds delivered in two days in the May 2026 report
- 28 direct household placements
- 51 beds placed with Utopia Homelands Council and Arts Centre for community distribution
- QR-linked asset tracking
- Consent and count reconciliation gates remain visible

This story proves the delivery and reporting modules. It does not yet prove a production-facility outcome.

## Frame 5: The pricing canvas

Duplicate this canvas for every community request.

### Community-defined outcome

- What do people want to make possible?
- What is the smallest useful first result?
- Who decides whether it worked?

### Readiness

- Local lead and mandate
- Place and site
- Existing skills and assets
- Inputs and feedstock
- Demand or offtake
- Power, water, transport and storage
- Safety and governance

### Cost stack

| Cost layer | What belongs here |
| --- | --- |
| Equipment | Machine, tooling, guards, spares |
| Place | Freight, cranage, site works, power, water, shelter, storage |
| People | Paid local participation, training, travel, accommodation, technical support |
| Production | Inputs, first run, quality testing, packaging, delivery |
| Continuity | Maintenance, consumables, remote support, replacement parts |
| Enterprise | Demand development, contracts, finance, governance, working capital |
| Evidence | Baseline, asset data, photos, video, interviews, consent, reporting |
| Risk | Contingency and clearly assigned responsibilities |

### Evidence label

Every number must be labelled:

- **Verified:** invoice, signed document or measured record
- **Workpaper:** checkable internal calculation
- **Modelled:** built from inputs, not yet demonstrated at this site
- **Target:** desired future state
- **Unknown:** requires a quote or community decision

## Frame 8: Shared backbone that funders can support

Some costs should not be forced into one community's machine budget:

- Listening and scoping
- Engineering and product standards
- Vendor procurement
- Remote freight coordination
- Training systems
- Safety and quality systems
- Maintenance and spare-parts network
- Demand aggregation and buyer relationships
- Asset and outcome tracking
- Consent-led media and case studies
- Finance, contracts and governance
- Community-to-community learning

This is the Goods platform. It makes each local module safer, faster, more honest and less expensive to repeat.

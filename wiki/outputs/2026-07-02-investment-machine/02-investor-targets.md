## Tier B: New verified candidates

Four survived an adversarial refute pass (open now, no ownership gate, timeline, match eligibility). Every claim carries its source URL. None is in GHL yet: adding them is a staged write in `03-machine-blueprint.md`, not done.

### 1. LendForGood: crowd-lent impact loan. Priority NOW. SIGNABLE.

- **Instrument:** repayable debt via an approved intermediary, funded by crowd lenders, repaid at an agreed amount and date. Exactly the shape QBE prefers.
- **Ticket:** AU$40K-$150K per campaign (verified AUD deals: $38K Nov 2025, $150K Oct 2025, $101K Jun 2026). $150K is the observed ceiling, so this substitutes or supplements the Snow or Centrecorp leg, not the SEFA anchor.
- **Ownership gate:** none. Borrowers span Australia and internationally; Indigenous business is a theme, not a requirement.
- **Speed to paper:** continuous campaign model, no committee rounds. A July start makes a signed loan agreement by 31 Aug realistic; plausibly the fastest repayable path on the list.
- **Evidence:** platform live and deploying through 2026 (50th and 51st loans announced 12 Mar 2026; ~$3.1M across ~60 deals), and Social Impact Hub is a confirmed named LendForGood intermediary, which matters because SIH already holds Goods' diligence through Catalysing Impact.
- **First move:** one line to Jay/SIH asking whether SIH will originate a Goods campaign. Caveats: recent deals were originated by other intermediaries, throughput is modest (~9 deals a year), crowd campaigns can under-fill, and rates are not published.
- **Source:** https://www.lendforgood.io/

### 2. Metro Finance, MetroEco green equipment finance. Priority NOW. SIGNABLE.

- **Instrument:** broker-originated asset finance (chattel mortgage), 2-5 year fixed. The ~1% MetroEco discount (CEFC 0.5% + Metro 0.5%) applies only to listed green assets (solar, batteries, EVs, electric machinery); the shredder, granulator and press would likely finance at Metro's standard commercial rate, which is still match-eligible, just not discounted.
- **Ticket:** AU$10K-$1M per loan, $2M total exposure cap.
- **Ownership gate:** none, purely commercial credit criteria.
- **Speed to paper:** broker asset finance approves in 2-6 weeks. One of the few genuinely realistic signed-paper paths inside the window.
- **Evidence:** product page live and soliciting via brokers; CEFC has $100M committed to a $500M MetroEco warehouse; Metro launched its 2026-1 Trust ABS in April 2026, proving active origination this quarter.
- **First move:** engage a broker now and decide which ABN borrows. The real risk is borrower credit, not the program: a newly incorporated Pty Ltd with trading history on the sole-trader ABN should expect full-doc treatment and director guarantees.
- **Source:** https://metrofin.com.au/metro-eco-equipment/

### 3. CommBank Green Vehicle and Equipment Finance. Priority SOON. STRETCH.

- **Instrument:** equipment loan, chattel mortgage, hire purchase or finance lease against specific asset invoices; up to 0.50% p.a. green discount on qualifying assets (solar and batteries clearly qualify; confirm the plant machinery on 1800 ASSETS).
- **Ticket:** AU$20K minimum; green discounts on transactions under $250K, larger by negotiation.
- **Ownership gate:** none ("approved business customers for business purposes only").
- **Speed to paper:** 1-business-day approval-contact aim, paperless docs, no committee dates. Plausible inside the window, but eligibility asks for 12+ months trading and a good credit rating, so the accountant-prepared credit package (business plan, 12-month cash flow, financials) is the gating work, and the new-entity question must be answered first.
- **Evidence:** product page live (fetched 2026-07-02) with the stated discounts and the $20K minimum; evergreen bank product since April 2023.
- **First move:** early eligibility call to 1800 ASSETS, because credit lead time is the binding constraint. Secondary to the SEFA stack; do not let it distract from the anchor.
- **Source:** https://www.commbank.com.au/business/loans-and-finance/car-and-equipment-finance/green-finance.html

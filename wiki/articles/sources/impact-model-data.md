## Reviewable Substance

The impact model is useful as a design map. It should not be presented as a completed evaluation system.

| Dimension | Useful indicators | Current caution |
|---|---|---|
| Health and housing | Beds delivered, bedding washability, washing-machine availability, partner health feedback, safer sleep context. | No independent clinical outcome study yet. |
| Environmental | Plastic reused, products repaired, bulky waste avoided, old item disposition and product lifespan. | Plastic diversion estimates need source and audit status labels. |
| Economic | Local paid hours, production roles, procurement value, resident savings and reduced replacement cycles. | Need clean unit economics and place-level evidence. |
| Community ownership | Local production roles, partner governance, revenue retention and community-controlled pathways. | Legal and governance model not settled. |
| Story and agency | Consent, story return, retraction rights, named community interpretation and feedback loops. | Requires consent review before external use. |

## Live Versus Candidate Metrics

Use this distinction in every impact discussion.

| Metric type | Examples |
|---|---|
| Live or currently recorded | Products delivered, communities served, direct feedback, photos/videos/interviews, asset IDs or QR records where created. |
| Partly live | Washing-machine telemetry where connected, repair/support requests where submitted, partner check-ins. |
| Candidate/future | Mattress wash frequency, 12/24/36-month retention, avoided dump events, local jobs over time, longitudinal health outcomes. |

## Measurement Rule

Impact claims should be written in three layers:

- what we know from product records,
- what we know from community voice and partner feedback,
- what we are still testing or building.

That structure keeps the model human and prevents dashboards from sounding more mature than the evidence.

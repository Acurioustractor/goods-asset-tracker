## What It Contains

- Investor-fit criteria and readiness questions.
- Capital-type alignment logic.
- Trade-offs between grant, debt, concessional finance and other instruments.
- A discipline for choosing patient capital rather than chasing any capital.

## Reviewable Substance

The tool is valuable because it asks "fit" before "money".

| Capital type | When it fits Goods | When it is risky |
|---|---|---|
| Grant | R&D, plant readiness, community engagement, impact evidence and early capacity. | If it keeps Goods in charity-delivery mode forever. |
| Philanthropy | Patient support for products, production, local roles and evidence building. | If funder priorities distort place-based judgement. |
| Concessional debt | Inventory or working capital matched to credible orders and payment timing. | If used to cover general uncertainty or unfunded operating gaps. |
| Repayable grant | Useful transition between grant and debt if repayment terms match revenue. | If repayment conditions arrive before product operations are repeatable. |
| Equity | Not a near-term priority unless community ownership is the equity pathway. | If it reduces flexibility or pulls control away from purpose/community. |
| Procurement revenue | Strongest proof when a buyer pays for real product need. | If buyer procurement ignores the resident/user experience. |

## Goods-Specific Screening Questions

- Does the capital help people get better goods, or mainly help us look bigger?
- Does it fund inventory, plant readiness, local roles, repair, evidence or working capital?
- Does it require a legal/governance structure we do not yet have?
- Does repayment depend on signed demand or hope?
- Does it strengthen community ownership or centralise value in ACT?
- Does it require claims we cannot yet evidence?

## How To Use It

Use the alignment tool before adding an investor to HighLevel/GHL. A capital target should have:

- capital type,
- use of funds,
- fit reason,
- risk reason,
- evidence needed,
- governance/legal readiness,
- next action,
- decision owner.

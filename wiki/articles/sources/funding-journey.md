## What It Contains

- Confirmed received funding.
- Active applications and decisions pending.
- Relationship-building without formal applications.
- Misses worth being honest about.
- Corporate, supply chain and community partnerships.
- Key people for mentors to understand.

## Reviewable Substance

This source should be used to show learning and traction, not to imply the capital stack is finished.

| Funding category | What to capture | Review caution |
|---|---|---|
| Confirmed received funding | Funder, amount, date, purpose, restrictions and acquittal status. | Reconcile with finance records before repeating totals. |
| Active applications | Submitted amount, decision date, match relevance and required follow-up. | Do not count as secured revenue. |
| Relationship building | Warm funder, mentor or capital relationship with no formal application yet. | Useful for pipeline, not financial proof. |
| Misses | Unsuccessful applications and what was learned. | Keep these because they show discipline and pattern recognition. |
| Partner commitments | Letters, buyer signals, in-kind support and warm procurement pathways. | Separate non-binding support from fundable commitments. |

## Numbers To Treat Carefully

- The broader pack references about `$537,595` cumulative revenue/support and about `$460K` confirmed received funding in different contexts.
- Those figures may be measuring different things: revenue through ACT, confirmed grants, payments received, pledged support or historic project income.
- The next finance pass should produce one reconciled table with amount, status, cash received, receivable, restricted purpose and source document.

## Best Use In The QBE Pack

- Show that Goods has already attracted real philanthropic and program support.
- Show that the team has applied, missed, learned and kept going.
- Show where capital relationships are warm enough for Stage 2.
- Show the gap: investor-grade finance reporting and matched-funding evidence are not yet clean enough.

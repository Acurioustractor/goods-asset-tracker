## What It Contains

- Market sizing scenarios for 5,000+ beds and 500+ washing machines.
- Eight channels: NT housing, Aboriginal Hostels, Outback Stores/ALPA, NACCHO/ACCHOs, NIAA laundries, state Aboriginal housing, mining-region foundations and NDIS/SDA.
- Pricing and financing model.
- Structural requirement for 51 percent First Nations-owned operating entity.
- Production capacity scaling and 90-day plan.
- Kill risks and platform pathway.

## Reviewable Substance

This source is useful as a stress test. It asks what would need to be true if Goods moved from hundreds of products to thousands.

| Scenario area | Useful question |
|---|---|
| Buyer channels | Which channels can actually buy at scale without distorting the mission? |
| Production capacity | What equipment, people, shifts and quality controls are needed for volume? |
| Working capital | How much cash is needed before buyer payments arrive? |
| Resident access | How does scale make products more affordable rather than just more visible? |
| First Nations ownership | What structure gives real control rather than symbolic ownership? |
| Kill risks | What would make the scale plan irresponsible? |

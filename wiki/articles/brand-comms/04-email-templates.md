# 04. Email and Comms Templates

Every Goods email follows the voice rules in [01-voice-and-tone.md](01-voice-and-tone.md). These templates are starting points, not scripts. Personalise the opening and the ask. Keep the spine.

Default sender: Nic (founder context), Ben (operations and tech), or Sally (strategic / capital). Sign-off below is plain.

## Default signature block

```
[First name]
Goods on Country
[Title where useful]
goodsoncountry.com  |  hi@act.place
[mobile when appropriate]
```

No ornament. No logo image (it makes emails look transactional). No "warm regards" or "best wishes" gloss. Sign with first name only.

## Subject line patterns

- `Goods on Country / [topic in 3-5 words]`
- `[Number] beds [outcome] / Goods on Country`
- `Following up: [meeting context]`
- `[Funder name] + Goods: [shared interest]`

Keep under 60 characters. Lowercase the topic half. Numbers in subjects pull more opens.

Examples that work:
- `Goods on Country / 100 beds, on-country production`
- `400 beds delivered / 8 communities / one ask`
- `Following up on QBE Catalysing Impact intro`
- `Snow Foundation + Goods: production plant funding`

## Template 1: Funder intro (cold or warm)

**Subject:** Goods on Country / [shared interest in 3 words]

> Hi [first name],
>
> [Optional: warm intro line if referred. Otherwise skip the small talk.]
>
> Goods on Country builds essential goods with remote First Nations communities in Australia. We've delivered 540 beds across 11 communities (177 Stretch Beds, 363 Basket Beds). The Stretch Bed flat-packs, weighs 26kg, and is built from recycled HDPE plastic, galvanised steel, and Australian canvas. Every component is designed in community.
>
> The reason I'm writing: [specific link to their work]. We're [specific stage / ask], and we'd value [specific thing: 30 minutes, an intro, a written response].
>
> A short read if useful: [one specific URL from goodsoncountry.com, see 05-pipelines-x-brand.md]
>
> Happy to send the 12-slide overview deck or jump on a call.
>
> [First name]

**Notes:**
- Lead with what we make, not who we are.
- Use one number, one place, one ask.
- Pick the URL based on funder type. See [05-pipelines-x-brand.md](05-pipelines-x-brand.md).
- Never attach the deck unprompted. Offer it.

## Template 2: Funder follow-up (post meeting)

**Subject:** Following up: [funder] + Goods on Country

> Hi [first name],
>
> Thanks for the time on [day of week]. The three things you asked for:
>
> 1. **[Item one]** [one-sentence answer or link]
> 2. **[Item two]** [one-sentence answer or link]
> 3. **[Item three]** [one-sentence answer or link]
>
> [Optional: a single new thing they didn't ask for but should know.]
>
> Next step from your side: [what they said they would do, restated].
> Next step from our side: [what we'll do, with a date].
>
> [First name]

**Notes:**
- Numbered list of exactly what they asked for, in their words.
- Restate next steps for both sides explicitly.
- No hedging. If we don't have the answer, say "I'll have this to you by [date]".

**Subject:** Goods on Country / [period] report / [funder name]

> Hi [first name],

> Quick report for [period]. Plain numbers below, three stories underneath, and what we'd ask of you next.
>
> **Where the funding went**
>
> | Item | Funded | Delivered |
> |------|--------|-----------|
> | Beds | $[X] | [Y] beds across [Z] communities |
> | Plant works | $[X] | [outcome] |
> | [Other line] | $[X] | [outcome] |
>
> **Three moments**
>
> 1. **[Community]:** [Two sentences. What happened. Quote if available.]
> 2. **[Community]:** [Same.]
> 3. **[Community]:** [Same.]
>
> **What's hard right now**
>
> [Honest paragraph. One challenge with our current response.]
>
> **What we'd ask of you next**
>
> [Specific. Optional. Either an introduction, a renewal conversation, or "no ask, just an update".]
>
> Photos and the full story logs are at [URL]. Happy to walk through any of this.
>
> [First name]

**Notes:**
- Numbers first.
- Show one thing that's hard. Funders trust honesty more than triumph.
- Specific named communities only. No "remote Australia" generic.

## Template 4: Procurement / B2B (health service, council, housing org)

**Subject:** Stretch Bed / [their context: clinic, housing program, etc.]

> Hi [first name],
>
> I understand [their org] is [specific work, e.g. fitting out 30 homelands homes]. We make a bed designed for that brief.
>
> **Stretch Bed specs:**
>
> - 26kg, 200kg load, 188×92×25cm, assembles in 5 minutes, no tools.
> - Recycled HDPE legs, galvanised steel frame (DNA Steel, Alice Springs), heavy-duty Australian canvas (Centre Canvas, Alice Springs).
> - 5-year warranty, 10+ year design life.
> - Washable canvas, repairable in community.
>
> **Pricing:** $560 institutional, $600 retail. Volume pricing available above 50 units.
>
> **Lead time:** [Current capacity, e.g. 4-6 weeks for orders under 100 units].
>
> **Freight:** [Honest answer. Quote on confirmation of destination.]
>
> Spec sheet attached. Happy to send a sample or run a video walk-through.
>
> [First name]

**Notes:**
- Specs forward. Less story, more signal.
- Real supplier names build credibility.
- Always state freight as a known unknown. We don't pretend.

## Template 5: Community partner (deployment conversation)

**Subject:** Yarn about beds / [community name]

> [First name], [optional honorific],
>
> [If introduced: warm acknowledgement of the introducer.]
>
> We're working with communities across the NT and QLD on beds and washing machines. [Local example: e.g. "Diane Stokes named the washing machine in Warumungu, and we delivered 139 beds to Tennant Creek."]
>
> We're not pitching anything. Just opening a yarn about whether anything we make would be useful in [community], and on what terms. Decisions sit with you.
>
> When suits, I'm in [travel context] [date range], or happy to speak by phone.
>
> [First name]

**Notes:**
- Slowest tone. No urgency.
- Acknowledge introducer fully if there is one.
- Make clear decisions belong to community.
- Never ask for a specific outcome in the first message.
- Don't attach anything unless requested.

## Email anti-patterns

- "Hope this finds you well." (Skip.)
- "Just circling back." (Use "Following up.")
- "I wanted to reach out." (You did. Get to the point.)
- Multi-paragraph context before the ask. (Lead with the ask.)
- Attaching the deck without offering it. (Offer first.)
- Em dashes anywhere in the body. (Banned.)
- Long sign-offs. (One line.)

## Translating tone for context

| Recipient | Tone shift |
|-----------|-----------|
| Foundation program officer | Funder intro template, warm, specific to their portfolio |
| Foundation CEO | Slightly more strategic, fewer specifics, name a peer they respect |
| Health service procurement | B2B template, specs first, lead time |
| Community organisation | Community template, slow, no ask |
| Government department | Procurement template, formal but specific. CC your community partner. |
| Corporate ESG | Funder intro, but lead with the model (community ownership transfer), not just the bed |
| Investor (impact / catalytic) | Different again. Use the QBE-context capital deck, not these templates. See [v2/docs/COMPENDIUM_MARCH_2026.md](../../v2/docs/COMPENDIUM_MARCH_2026.md). |

## Response time targets

| Type | Target |
|------|--------|
| Funder follow-up | 24h |
| Procurement | 48h |
| Community | When community responds, never push |
| Media | Same day if feasible |
| Internal | Same day |

# Photography Commission Brief

> Hand this to a photographer (or to Nic with a phone) before the next On-Country trip. Lists the gaps we know we have, the shot direction, technical specs, consent protocol, and deliverables.

## Why this brief exists

The Goods on Country brand library is photography-led. We have strong product detail and Tennant Creek storyteller portraits, but we have specific gaps that block stronger comms in 2026 (funder decks, the /brand page, press kit). Filling these gaps once, properly, unlocks the rest of the year's brand work.

Reference for what we have already: [03-product-image-library.md](03-product-image-library.md).

## Photo direction (non-negotiable)

1. **Real moments On-Country.** Photographed where the product lives. Tennant Creek verandah, Palm Island front yard, Maningrida community centre. Never a studio.
2. **Golden hour by default.** Australian light at sunrise and sunset. The existing media-pack hero shots set the standard.
3. **Centre Elders and community members where present.** Never crop them out. Never use them as background figures.
4. **Material honesty.** Show the recycled HDPE plastic legs, the galvanised steel, the canvas weave. Close-ups that show how it's made.
5. **Hands and people, not just object shots.** Beds being assembled. Beds being slept on. Beds in family rooms, not white voids.
6. **Honest backgrounds.** Show the verandah, the dirt, the corrugated roof, the kids in the doorway. Don't tidy the frame.
7. **No saviour framing.** No photos of staff handing over a bed to a community member. No "before and after". Show partnership, not delivery.
8. **Consent is photography law.** Every identifiable person needs documented consent. Always credit. Always allow withdrawal.

## What no Goods photo should be

- Stock photography of any kind
- Generic "Indigenous Australia" imagery (red dirt, dot painting, didgeridoo) used as decoration
- Beds floating on white seamless backgrounds
- Heavy filter, oversaturated red, or "outback aesthetic" colour grading
- Hero portraits without consent state on file

## Composition cues

- Wide environmental shot first, detail shots second.
- 3:2 horizontal for hero / banner. Square for grid. 4:5 vertical for mobile-first marketing.
- Negative space top-right of hero shots so we have room for headlines and brand mark.
- Brand mark visible in at least one product hero shot per shoot (see [media-pack/goods-branding-golden-hour.jpg](../../v2/public/images/media-pack/goods-branding-golden-hour.jpg)).

## Technical specs

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| Minimum resolution | 4096px on the long side, RAW + JPEG |
| Compression for delivery | JPEG quality 85, sRGB colour space |
| Web hero target | 1920px on the long side, under 500kb |
| Format diversity | Each subject shot in 3:2 horizontal AND 4:5 vertical |
| File naming | `{category}/{subject}-{moment}.jpg` (lowercase, hyphenated) |
| Categories | `community/`, `people/`, `process/`, `product/`, `media-pack/` |
| Metadata | Storyteller name, community, date, photographer credit, consent status, any cultural restrictions |

## Consent protocol

1. **Before any shoot:** the community partner organisation (PICC, Oonchiumpa, Anyinginyi, etc.) is briefed on the planned photography.
2. **For each identifiable person:**
   - Verbal consent before first shot
   - Written consent form signed before publication
   - Storyteller chooses their preferred name/credit
   - Storyteller can withdraw consent at any time, with 24h removal commitment
3. **For children:** parent / guardian consent in writing, no exceptions.
4. **For cultural content:** Elder approval required. Some content may have restrictions on context of use (e.g. funder decks fine, social media not).
5. **Storage:** all metadata including consent state goes into Empathy Ledger immediately on return from the field.

## Deliverables (per shoot)

1. RAW files (all)
2. JPEG selects (the photographer's top 30 picks per priority gap)
3. Web-optimised JPEGs (1920px long side, under 500kb)
4. CSV of metadata (filename, storyteller, community, date, photographer credit, consent status)
5. Signed consent forms (one per identifiable person)
6. A briefing-back conversation with the team within 7 days of return

## Budget guide

- Day rate: $1,200-$2,000/day for a documentary photographer with social-impact experience
- Travel: separate, settled per trip
- Cultural consultation rate (if photographer is not Indigenous, an Indigenous photographer or cultural lead should be engaged at $3,800/day comparable to university research rates)
- Editing: include in day rate up to a fixed file count, additional editing billable

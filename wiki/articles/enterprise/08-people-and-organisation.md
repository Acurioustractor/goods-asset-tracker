# 8. People and Organisation

> Goods is still founder-led, but it is not just two people with an idea. It is us, ACT shared services, community partners, production partners, design partners, funders, advisors and emerging local operators. The next organisational question is how to remove founder bottlenecks without building a heavy organisation too early.

## The human version

We are still close to the work because the work is not abstract. We are in houses, in dumps, in production sheds, in funding conversations, and in the awkward middle between product, community, capital and operations.

That closeness is a strength. It is also the ceiling. Goods cannot become a real product system across remote communities if every relationship, decision, production issue, grant, story, sale and repair depends on the founders being there.

## Current organisation shape

| Part of the system | Who or what it is | What it does | Main risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Founder leadership | Ben Knight and Nicholas Marchesi. | Holds vision, product direction, relationships, fundraising, partner trust and operating decisions. | Key-person dependency and context trapped in founder heads. |
| ACT shared services | A Curious Tractor capability across strategy, communications, finance/admin, tooling and project support. | Lets Goods move without hiring a standalone team too early. | Shared resources can hide the true cost of Goods operations. |
| Community partners | Place-based partners such as Ntumbu pathways, Palm Island Community Company conversations and other local organisations. | Local trust, delivery insight, feedback, future production/repair pathways and community ownership. | Relationship load grows faster than the team can support. |
| Production partners/operators | Current and emerging people who can collect, shred, press, assemble and deliver. | Moves production away from founders physically doing every step. | Quality, safety and training systems must mature before scale. |
| Design and technical partners | Defy Design, manufacturing advisors, Empathy Ledger/tech support and product specialists. | Product iteration, technical build, story/data tooling and production problem-solving. | Too much knowledge remains in informal conversations unless documented. |
| Advisory network | Goods advisory group, philanthropy supporters, industry advisors and social enterprise peers. | Challenge, advice, openings and credibility. | Advisory does not replace operational capacity or governance authority. |

## Capacity risk

The capacity issue is already visible.

- We are the only two full-time-equivalent people consistently holding Goods.
- They are not currently fixed salaried Goods employees in a mature structure.
- ACT shared services and founder flexibility are subsidising the work.
- Production, sales, operations, procurement, finance, governance, community engagement and capital raising are all competing for the same small founder bandwidth.
- The team knows this: Nicholas named the need for people better than the founders at scaling product and operations.

The good sign is that production knowledge is starting to leave founder hands. Nicholas described the move from us teaching ourselves shredding, pressing, CNC and assembly to lower-skilled operators being able to run parts of the process. That is the organisational direction Goods needs everywhere: turn founder intuition into teachable systems.

## Capability gaps

| Gap | Why it matters | What "good enough for now" looks like |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sales and buyer management | Goods needs institutional buyers, community buyers and resident pathways without losing the ownership vision. | A relationship owner, target list, pipeline stages and follow-up rhythm in HighLevel/GHL. |
| Procurement and partnerships | Government, councils, health buyers, Aboriginal trusts and philanthropy all buy differently. | A procurement pathway map connected to Grantscope and GHL target categories. |
| Operations | Orders, inventory, freight, production, repair and delivery need repeatable routines. | One operations lead or operator with clear SOPs and a weekly operating rhythm. |
| Production leadership | On-Country production cannot depend on founders troubleshooting every process step. | Production guide, quality checks, training record and named local process owner. |
| Finance and modelling | Repayable capital needs cashflow discipline, unit economics and working-capital visibility. | Goods-specific reporting pack and external CFO/accounting support. |
| Governance and board depth | Advisory input is useful, but investors will need clear decision rights and risk review. | Advisory terms, decision matrix and commercial/social enterprise governance expertise. |
| Community relationship capacity | More communities means more consent, feedback, follow-up and expectation management. | Place-based coordinator roles or partner-held roles where possible. |

## What not to build

Goods should avoid building a large central organisation just to feel serious.

The stated direction is shared services, local production, community ownership and practical roles close to the work. That means the organisation should grow through:

- local enterprise capability
- production and repair roles
- procurement and relationship discipline
- community partner capacity
- shared ACT infrastructure
- lean governance and finance systems

The point is not "make Goods as big as possible". The point is to make the product system work and shift more power, production and value On-Country.

## Human review questions

- What work must stay with us for the next 6 months?
- What work can be handed to ACT shared services, a contractor or a partner now?
- Which role removes the largest bottleneck first: sales, operations, production or finance?
- What local roles should be funded as part of each production facility?
- Which advisory gaps need a person, and which need a process?
- How do we show true staff cost when founder time and ACT shared services are subsidising Goods?

## Next outputs to create

- Current organisation map.
- Founder role map and delegation plan.
- Hiring sequence tied to capital milestones.
- Partner capacity map by place.
- Production training and local-role model.
- Goods-specific team budget with founder time and ACT shared services made visible.

# 5. Strategic Planning and Risk Management

> Goods plans through live relationships, funding windows, product proof and community readiness. That is the right way to work in this context, but it needs a clearer operating rhythm for QBE: what decisions are live, what evidence moves a decision forward, what risks could cause harm, and what gets reviewed weekly, monthly and before capital is accepted.

## The human version

Goods is not missing ambition. The ambition is big: change the product ecosystem in remote communities so essential goods are desirable, repairable, washable, locally meaningful and eventually produced On-Country.

The hard part is sequencing. A plant cannot be chosen just because it looks good in a strategy document. It depends on trust, partner readiness, funding, machinery, plastic supply, freight, people, local governance and whether the community actually wants the products. The strategy should show that judgement rather than pretending there is a neat Gantt chart underneath.

## Current strategic direction

| Strategic layer | Current position | What it means for the pack |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Long-term vision | Products in remote communities across Australia, with multiple decentralised production plants. | Keep the ambition visible, but do not write it as a forecast. |
| Medium-term shape | ACT/shared-services capability supporting local or Aboriginal-controlled production entities. | Show the operating model and the questions still open around ownership, IP, machinery and shared services. |
| Near-term priority | Deploy the first On-Country production facility, with August 2026 as the working target from the QBE period. | Treat August as a target tied to readiness gates, not a guaranteed date. |
| Product focus | Stretch Bed first, washing machine prototype second, future fridge third. | Keep resources anchored to the bed proof before overextending. |
| Capital focus | Grants, patient/sub-market loans and sales revenue before any equity. | Tie capital to inventory, plant readiness, local roles and working capital, not generic growth. |
| Operating focus | Move from founder-led batches toward repeatable production, delivery, feedback and repair. | Show what has become teachable and what still depends on founder. |

## Production facility decision

The next facility should be chosen through readiness, not enthusiasm.

Potential locations named in the working material include Palm Island, Alice Springs, Mount Isa, Tennant Creek and Central Australia pathways. The location is not just a site choice. It is a governance, labour, funding, freight, plastic supply and relationship decision.

| Readiness question | Evidence to check | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Is there real local demand? | Bed requests, partner demand, product feedback, delivery history and resident/institutional interest. | Avoid building a plant before the product has pull. |
| Is there a trusted local partner? | Partner conversations, governance maturity, local leadership and follow-through history. | The plant needs local authority, not just permission. |
| Can plastic be collected and used? | Waste stream, collection permissions, sorting/shredding feasibility and council incentives. | Circular economy claims depend on usable local material. |
| Can production be run safely? | Equipment, SOPs, training, supervision, WHS, maintenance and QA checks. | Product safety and worker safety are non-negotiable. |
| Can beds reach households? | Freight, delivery partner, assembly capability and repair pathway. | A production plant is not useful if delivery fails. |
| Can it create local value? | Paid roles, local ownership pathway, enterprise readiness and youth/employment alignment. | The facility should build power, not only output. |
| Is funding matched to timing? | Grants, orders, payment terms, working capital and capital commitments. | A plant can fail if cash arrives after costs. |

## Planning rhythm

Goods needs a planning system light enough to use every week and clear enough for funders.

| Rhythm | What gets reviewed | Output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Weekly | QBE actions, live partner follow-up, funding deadlines, production blockers, CRM/procurement actions. | Updated action list and owner decisions. |
| Fortnightly | Source register, evidence gaps, story consent items, product feedback and Notion review queue. | Updated wiki/Notion decision notes. |
| Monthly | Cashflow, orders, grants receivable, plant readiness, risk register, advisory questions and capital pipeline. | Governance/advisory pack. |
| Before accepting capital | Debt service, use of funds, legal vehicle, reporting capacity, risk owners and community impact. | Capital-readiness decision record. |
| Before choosing a plant | Partner readiness, community consent, equipment, labour, plastic, funding, production safety and delivery route. | Place-readiness decision record. |

## Risk frame

Nicholas named the main test clearly: do no harm.

For Goods, "do no harm" is practical:

- Do not put unsafe products into houses.
- Do not put unsafe equipment into community production.
- Do not use stories in ways people cannot control.
- Do not claim circular economy while creating a new waste problem.
- Do not take debt that forces bad product or bad community decisions.
- Do not let funder timelines override community readiness.
- Do not centralise value in ACT while talking about community ownership.

## Working risk register

| Risk area | Current risk | Current read | What makes it worse | Next control |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Product safety | A bed, washing machine or plant process harms someone. | Low to medium likelihood, high consequence. | Faster production without QA, unclear warranty/service loops or untrained operators. | Product QA checklist, warranty pathway, incident process and production sign-off. |
| Environmental | Goods accidentally adds to the waste problem it is trying to solve. | Medium likelihood, high consequence. | Broken products not repaired, plastic not usable, offcuts unmanaged or end-of-life pathway missing. | Environmental risk line with repair, reuse, offcut and end-of-life controls. |
| Community trust | Poor follow-through damages a relationship. | Always live. | Overpromising delivery, slow communication, unclear partner role or story misuse. | Partner check-in cadence and decision record for each place. |
| Story and data | A story, image or product data point is used without enough control. | Medium likelihood, very high consequence. | Raw transcripts/images shared too widely or CRM/product data mixed without consent. | Consent-led source review and redacted examples only. |
| Key people | We remain the bottleneck. | Already happening. | Every buyer, funder, production and community issue comes back to founders. | Delegation map, role priorities and operating handover. |
| Production | Machinery, material, labour or quality delays delivery. | Medium likelihood, medium to high consequence. | Plant location chosen before training, power, plastic and equipment are ready. | Facility readiness checklist and SOPs. |
| Cashflow | Costs land before grant, customer or partner payments. | High likelihood, medium to high consequence. | Inventory build or plant deployment without payment timing discipline. | Working-capital model and payment milestone rules. |
| Debt | Repayable capital is taken before the model can service it. | Future risk, high consequence. | Debt used to cover general operating pressure rather than matched demand. | Debt gate: credible revenue, stress test, repayment reserve and governance approval. |
| Governance | Legal or board structure lags investor needs. | Medium likelihood, high consequence. | Capital lands before entity, board/advisory authority and reporting are clear. | Minimum viable governance pack. |
| Community ownership | The ownership model is too Western, complex or under-resourced. | Medium likelihood, high consequence. | Local entity carries labour/risk while ACT holds value, data, brand and decisions. | Community ownership options paper and place-by-place governance review. |

## Scenario decisions

| Scenario | Strategic response |
| --- | --- |
| QBE match or key grant is missed | Slow the plant rollout, keep smaller funded batches moving, protect trust, and avoid debt just to maintain momentum. |
| Capital lands faster than expected | Resist central over-hiring. Put money into inventory, production readiness, local roles, finance controls and repair/service loops. |
| Resident purchasing is slower than hoped | Keep institutional and philanthropic channels open while improving affordability, desirability and local proof. |
| Plant partner is ready but capital is late | Use a staged readiness pathway: training, source material, orders, facility design and partner governance before machinery commitment. |
| Demand spikes before supply is ready | Prioritise transparent waitlists, buyer communication and quality over rushed production. |
| Story or data consent is unclear | Do not publish or send externally. Move it to review queue and use de-identified pattern evidence only. |

## What was missing in older material

- A clear distinction between ambition, plan, target and forecast.
- Place-by-place readiness criteria for production facilities.
- A risk register that names environmental risk properly.
- A cashflow and debt risk gate before impact investment.
- A way to connect QBE weekly actions, Notion review, HighLevel/GHL follow-up and Grantscope opportunities.
- A process for deciding what is safe to share externally.
- Specific owners and review cadence for each major risk.

## Human review questions

- Which facility location is actually first today, and what evidence supports that?
- What must be true before August 2026 can remain a credible production target?
- Which risks need a founder owner, which need an advisor, and which need a local partner owner?
- What would make us pause a plant even if funding landed?
- Which risks would make repayable capital inappropriate right now?
- What needs to be in the monthly governance pack so the risk register gets used?

## Next outputs to create

- Place-readiness checklist for each candidate production facility.
- Strategic milestones page for the next 6, 12 and 18 months.
- Board/advisory risk register with owners and review dates.
- Debt and working-capital risk gate.
- QBE weekly actions to Notion/GHL workflow.
- Scenario plan for "capital late", "capital early" and "demand faster than supply".

# 1. Clarity of Vision and Ambition

> Does the organisation have a clear overall vision, including the path for how to get there?

## Vision (one sentence)

Make Goods beds as ubiquitous in remote Australian communities as a troop carrier or an Akubra hat, and transfer ownership of production and profits to the communities who use them.

## Mission

Revolutionise how essential goods are created by transferring ownership of production and profits to First Nations communities through local resource utilisation, starting with beds and appliances that survive remote conditions.

## The Big Hairy Audacious Goal

1. **Product ubiquity.** Visit any remote community in Australia and a Goods bed is either in use or available to buy. It becomes the product of first choice.
2. **Environmental reversal.** Slow the flood of disposable furniture into remote communities. One Alice Springs provider sells $3M of washing machines annually, most ending up in dumps within months. We displace that with products that last ten years and can be repaired on Country.
3. **Community ownership of production.** 40% profit share to communities as a non-negotiable. Production plants owned locally. Jobs created On-Country.

## Path (three horizons)

**Horizon 1 (now to Q4 2026): prove the commercial loop.**
Close QBE match-funding target of $400K. Stand up the first containerised production circuit (Alice Springs, Tennant Creek, Katherine, Darwin). Move from $50K actual revenue to a first-year run rate that services debt. Demonstrate 40% community profit share on a live deployment.

**Horizon 2 (2027-2028): scale the circuit.**
Multiple containerised facilities operating, each anchored by a community partner (Oonchiumpa, PICC, Centrecorp). Institutional sales channel live (NT Housing, health services, schools). Washing machine product moves from prototype to commercial. First plant transitioned to community ownership.

**Horizon 3 (2029+): the whole structure becomes unnecessary.**
ACT hands plants to community owners (PICC is the first signalled buyer). Goods becomes a license / design-and-support layer, not a vertically integrated manufacturer. Beautiful Obsolescence fulfilled.

## Why this ambition is credible

- **140+ beds deployed, 389 products total, 9,225 kg plastic diverted.** Real product in real communities, across Palm Island, Tennant Creek, Maningrida, Alice Homelands, Kalgoorlie (Ninga Mia), Mount Isa.
- **Pull, not push.** Dianne Stokes received one bed, asked for 20 more within two weeks and offered to fund them. Norm Frank called asking for three more in maroon. Homeland Schools Company has requested 65 beds. NPY Women's Council keeps asking. Demand is not a hypothesis.
- **Confirmed manufacturing partner.** Oonchiumpa Consultancy (100% Aboriginal-staffed) is the confirmed Central Australian manufacturing partner. Two years of co-design.
- **NT Housing $4B program** is the institutional demand curve. We do not need to create a market; we need to qualify into an existing one.

## Where the vision is still under-specified

- **Unit economics at community-ownership handover** (what the P&L looks like for PICC after they buy the plant) is not yet modelled.
- **International replication.** We have been asked about Pacific Islands and Canada First Nations; no decision on whether that is in scope.

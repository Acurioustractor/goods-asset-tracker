# Data Sovereignty

> Indigenous data sovereignty is a governance principle, not a policy checkbox. Goods' implementation runs through Empathy Ledger (the consent-first storytelling platform ACT built). OCAP principles are the default, not a bolt-on.

## Principles

**OCAP:** Ownership, Control, Access, Possession. The First Nations data-sovereignty framework. Each storyteller owns their story, controls its use, has access, and retains possession.

See ACT Tractorpedia:
- `act-global-infrastructure/wiki/concepts/ocap-principles.md`
- `act-global-infrastructure/wiki/concepts/indigenous-data-sovereignty.md`
- `act-global-infrastructure/wiki/concepts/consent-as-infrastructure.md`

## Implementation via Empathy Ledger

- Every story carries explicit, renewable consent.
- One-click withdrawal cascades through the system (story removed everywhere).
- Elder review workflow for sensitive content.
- Trigger warnings for content types.
- 33 Goods-connected storytellers currently, all with active consent.
- Auto-sync cron at `/api/cron/el-sync` runs daily.
- Storytellers own their records; Goods-side operational data is separated.

## Why this matters to funders

Most social enterprises extract community stories for fundraising and never give them back. Our architecture reverses that. This is:
- A cultural-safety commitment (no savior framing, no extraction).
- A reputational risk control (prevents the "nice story without consent" incidents that destroy trust).
- A funder differentiator (OCAP-compliant impact evidence is rare).

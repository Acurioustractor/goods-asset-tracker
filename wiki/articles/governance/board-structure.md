## What this gives us now

- Named people around the work, not only documents.
- First Nations, health, manufacturing, philanthropy, product and social enterprise perspectives close to the project.
- A monthly advisory rhythm and ad hoc support when specific questions come up.
- Evidence that the work is tested with community partners, funders and technical partners.
- A base for forming a tighter advisory committee, formal board or community governance structure later.

## What this does not yet give us

- It is not a legal board.
- It does not remove founder accountability.
- It does not create formal delegations.
- It does not give investors independent finance oversight yet.
- It does not define who approves conflicts of interest.
- It does not define who can stop a product, story, data use or community partnership if the risk is too high.
- It does not answer whether Goods needs independent directors, a board committee, a separate Goods entity, or a community-controlled operating vehicle.

## Known governance gap

We need more commercial and social-enterprise scale experience around the table. The missing capability is people who understand:

- product scale
- distribution into hard geographies
- procurement contracts
- working capital and inventory risk
- concessional debt and impact investment
- product liability and warranty exposure
- community-controlled enterprise structures
- the trade-off between growing fast and protecting the mission

## Next decision

The next step is not to add a board because it looks mature. The next step is to decide the minimum real governance structure needed for:

- QBE Stage 2
- SEFA or other debt
- IBA or government finance
- partner confidence
- community-ownership pathways
- story consent and data sovereignty
- product safety, warranty and repair accountability

## Practical next outputs

- Advisory board terms of reference.
- One-page governance map: who advises, who decides, who is accountable.
- Skills matrix for any formal board or advisory committee conversion.
- Conflict-of-interest register.
- Delegation matrix for product, finance, story/data, community partnerships and capital.
- Monthly governance pack: decisions, risks, cash, product quality, delivery, partner feedback and story/data issues.
- Legal-structure options note with Keith Rovers / Mint Ellison input.

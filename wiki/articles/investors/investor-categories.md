# Investor Categories (11 Types)

> CASE Smart Impact Capital taxonomy, presented at QBE induction. Grouped by scale: individuals, small/medium institutions, and full-spectrum institutions.

## Individuals

1. **High Net Worth Individuals and Angels** — personal capital, small-to-medium tickets, often relationship-driven.
2. **Friends & Family** — typically earliest stage, high flexibility, low sophistication.

## Small and medium institutions

3. **Small and Large Family Offices** — discretionary capital, often relationship + values aligned.
4. **Community and Family Foundations** — grant-making, some PRI capacity. Australian equivalents: Snow, TFN, VFFF, Tim Fairfax, Dusseldorp.
5. **CDFIs and DFIs** — Community / Development Financial Institutions. Australian equivalents: SEFA, First Australians Capital, some state funds.

## Individuals + small, medium and large institutions

6. **Private Foundations** — catalytic capital, PRI debt/equity, recoverable grants. Mindaroo, PFI, Ian Potter, Westpac Foundation.
7. **Government Investment Funds** — IBA, QLD Social Enterprise Jobs Fund, NT Housing-linked instruments.
8. **International NGOs** — not a 2026 priority for Goods.
9. **Corporates** — QBE Ventures (CVC), corporate partnerships.
10. **Investment Funds** — impact VC, commercial debt funds.
11. **Institutional Investors and Banks** — mainstream debt at scale (IBA sits in here as well as in #7).

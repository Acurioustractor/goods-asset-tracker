# Capital Types (13 Instruments)

> There are 13 recognised instruments of impact capital, grouped into grant-like, debt-like, and equity-like. Most raises use a blend. Framework from CASE Smart Impact Capital via the QBE Catalysing Impact program.

## Grant & grant-like

| Instrument | Description |
|---|---|
| **Grant** | Gift of cash, restricted (milestones/deliverables) or unrestricted. No repayment. |
| **Crowdfunding Donations** | Small contributions from many people, usually via a platform. | <!-- brand-lint-ignore-line: banned-donate -->
| **Pay For Success** | Contract between government, service provider, and investor tied to a social outcome. |
| **Loan Guarantee** | Third-party capital that protects an investor if an investment doesn't come back in full. |
| **Recoverable Grant** | High-risk loan that will either be repaid or written off by a certain date. |

## Debt & debt-like

| Instrument | Description |
|---|---|
| **PRI Debt** | Program-Related Investment debt from a private foundation (charitable loan). |
| **Variable Repayment Debt** | Loan with customised repayment terms tied to company operations (BOLD-style). |
| **Debt** | Standard loan, repaid with interest. |
| **Crowdfunding Debt** | Borrowing from many people via a platform, repayable in cash or goods/services. |
| **Convertible Debt** | Loan intended to convert into equity under defined conditions (usually a future round). |

## Equity & equity-like

| Instrument | Description |
|---|---|
| **PRI Equity** | Charitable investment by a private foundation in exchange for shares. | <!-- brand-lint-ignore-line: banned-donate -->
| **Equity** | Capital for ownership shares. |
| **Crowdfunding Equity / DPO** | Advertising an equity opportunity to the public. |

## What Goods is using

The current Goods stack touches grant, recoverable grant, loan guarantee, PRI debt, variable repayment debt, standard debt, and possibly crowdfunding equity (DPO). Equity at the ACT Ventures level is legally possible but not prioritised for 2026.

See [[capital-stack]] for the assembled picture.

## Choosing between them

The five [[the-big-questions]] are the forcing function:
1. Can you make regular cash repayments?
2. Planning an exit?
3. Willing to be valued?
4. Targeting 3-10x growth in 5 years?
5. Willing to customise terms per investor?

Goods can service debt (margin supports it), is not planning a conventional exit (community ownership pathway is the "exit"), can be valued but does not need to be for the 2026 round, can target 3-5x growth against NT Housing demand, and is willing to customise terms.

# PAFs, PuAFs and DGRs

> How Australian ancillary funds can participate in catalytic capital. Below-market-rate loans to DGRs count toward the required distribution. Losses on those loans also count.

## The mechanic

Australian Private Ancillary Funds (PAFs) and Public Ancillary Funds (PuAFs) are required to distribute a minimum percentage of their corpus each year. Catalytic loans to DGR-eligible charities count toward that distribution.

**Rule:** where rates charged are below market rates, the PAF can be credited with a distribution for the difference.

**Example:**
- PAF lends to a DGR at 5% p.a.
- Market rate would be 12% p.a.
- PAF is deemed to have distributed 7% p.a. (the difference).
- Required distribution reduced by that amount.

**Losses** on loans to DGRs are also credited as distributions.

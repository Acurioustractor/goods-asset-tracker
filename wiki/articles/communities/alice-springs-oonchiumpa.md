The Ntumbu group in Alice Springs is a core relationship where trust, communication and follow-through matter.

## Story and design evidence

Fred's feedback makes the product practical, not abstract:

- "That's something Central Australia need" because a simple recycled product can become something useful for people in the bush and on communities.
- Beds being off the ground matters for safety when families are currently sleeping on the ground.
- A portable bed matters during sorry business and family movement because people can move with it rather than sleeping on the ground.
- Fred also named the youth pathway: Xavier building beds with pride and energy is evidence that production work can become more than assembly.

Jacqueline's feedback connects the circular economy to the buyer reality:

- "From the waste, plastic. Perfect."
- Her comparison was simple: shop beds are expensive and can break easily, while the recycled plastic bed looked like it would stand and not break.

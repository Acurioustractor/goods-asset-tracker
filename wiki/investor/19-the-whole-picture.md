## 1 · Three names, one work

People keep meeting three names and wondering which one is real. They all are. They are three hands of the same body:

**Goods.** is the maker. The brand on the wall at Maningrida, sprayed by a young person's hand. It designs, presses, builds, sells and delivers. A bed costs $750 from Goods. and the money that comes back makes the next bed. Legally it lives inside A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd, trading as Goods.

**Goods on Country** is the charity. The Butterfly Movement transitioning into Aboriginal-led governance, where Aboriginal-owned social enterprises become members, not clients. Gifts land here (receiptable now under the interim arrangement, full endorsement FY2026-27). The charity pays the yearly bills while the maker grows, and it is the steward of the handover: it exists so the plant can be given away properly.

**A Curious Tractor** is the loan arm and the shed the tools came from. Repayable money lives here: the SEFA-shaped loans that bridge big orders and go home again, and the patient capital that moves through, not in. ACT is also where the sibling tools live, JusticeHub and Empathy Ledger, built in the same shed for the same communities.

One sentence for a funder: *give to Goods on Country, buy from Goods., lend through A Curious Tractor, and all three roads end at the same place: a plant in community hands.*

## 2 · The on-Country model, aligned

The model was never "make beds cheaper". It is: move each part of the making onto Country, in order, and let each move pay for the next.

1. **Collect** — waste plastic, gathered in community (the end-state; today plastic is still bought at ~$2.75/kg and we say so).
2. **Press** — 180 degrees at the farm today, proven by the Maningrida 40; on Country tomorrow ($100-150K a site, when a community is ready).
3. **Build** — already on Country. Kids built Maningrida's beds under the mango trees. Assembly is the first handover.
4. **Sell and deliver** — orders from community organisations at $750; each one shrinks what philanthropy must carry.
5. **Own** — the site, the press, the enterprise, the story. Four communities are already in the queue: Palm Island (council, community, PICC), Alice Springs (Oonchiumpa with the $2M REAL application in), Utopia (wants the shredder), Tennant Creek (The Community Shed).

Each stage has its money: gifts buy the early stages time, loans bridge the orders, sales carry the middle, and site capital (IBA-shaped, post-ownership-gate) finishes it. A community site keeps roughly $329 of every bed; the beds a year it needs to cover its own running costs is being rebuilt from the Oonchiumpa facility budget (the earlier 75-100 figure rested on a rent-only site bill of $24K/yr and understated the real cost, see `wiki/outputs/2026-07-22-community-facility-operating-model.md`). The drawn frames 08-11 in the deck file hold this as pictures; the taper (frame 11) is the whole section in one image.

## 3 · The overlapping systems (where the bed is a door)

We keep being filed under "furniture". The bed is a door into every system that touches a young person's life, and we have receipts in each:

**Youth justice.** Oonchiumpa works with the most at-risk young people in Alice. They build beds; some chose to be paid with a bed. The REAL application turns that into 16-20 near-full-time jobs. A young person on this pathway costs about $100 a day, next to more than $3,600 a day for detention (Productivity Commission, 2026). JusticeHub carries the language and the field stories (Jeremy Donovan's fire is the register this whole strategy now writes in).

**Health.** Scabies leads toward rheumatic heart disease; that is WHY beds off the floor and washing machines matter, and it is the claim ceiling: we say why, we never claim health outcomes. Pakkimjalki Kari carries Dianne's naming. The Deadly Heart Trek connection is already in the registry (Katherine's misfiled quote found its way home today).

**Education.** Homeland School Company registered a school, and adequate sleep was named a barrier to attendance. Forty beds and two washers went in (INV-0303). Beds as school infrastructure: nobody else in the country is saying that with invoices.

**Story and consent.** Empathy Ledger is how the voices travel with their consent attached: Dianne, Mykel, Alfred, Linda, Margaret, forty-plus tellers, thirty-four cleared for external use. The deck's voice slides pull only from there. The ledger is why our storytelling can be trusted at diligence depth.

The strategic sentence: **one product, four systems, one pathway out.** Funders who cannot fund furniture can fund justice, health, education or self-determination, and they are all buying the same bed.

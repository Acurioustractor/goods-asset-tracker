# Case study 4 — Oonchiumpa: the ownership claim in the owner's voice

> Pattern: an ownership pathway is only credible when the community leader says it unprompted,
> about their own agenda, with the enterprise as one instrument. Quotes below are internal
> analysis extracts pending Ben's clearing pass; use only after clearance.

## What happened

Kristy Bloomfield and Tanya Turner lead Oonchiumpa Consultancy in Alice Springs, working with
young people other services could not hold, on cultural authority. Oonchiumpa leads the Goods
work in Alice Springs and has asked to own a production plant: a request on the record, not a
settled deal, and always labelled that way.

Their Empathy Ledger corpus is the deepest in the Goods archive: nine transcripts, more than
43,000 words. It shows the ownership agenda is theirs, with Goods as one instrument inside it.
Kristy (pending clearance): "We wanna be able to create generational wealth, economic
development on our own land. We see too many times that a lot of other businesses are thriving.
Why can't we?" And: "We know what we wanna do on our land, and we know how to get there."

She also names the bed's why, in causal chain (pending clearance): a lack of housing leads to a
lack of sleep for young people, which leads to low school attendance. Tanya names the posture
Goods must hold (pending clearance): letting Aboriginal people lead, with the partner as an ally
on the side.

## Why it matters as a model

Enterprises claim community ownership pathways constantly; funders discount the claims because
the enterprise authored them. Here the authorship is reversed: the leaders' own recorded agenda
precedes and exceeds the enterprise. Goods' role is legible inside their words, not the other
way around. The truck test applies to this archive too: interpretive authority over these
transcripts is part of what transfers.

## The replicable pattern

1. Record the community organisation's own agenda deeply, beyond the product.
2. Position the enterprise inside their story; never position them inside yours.
3. Label requests as requests until they are deals.
4. Plan the transfer of the story system alongside the transfer of the plant.

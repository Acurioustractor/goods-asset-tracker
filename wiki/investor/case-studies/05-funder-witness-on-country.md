# Case study 5 — The funder witness: bring the funder on Country

> Pattern: let funders test the claim in community and record their own verdict; nothing the

## What happened

Georgina Byron AM, CEO of the Snow Foundation, travelled to Tennant Creek and later joined the
Deadly Heart Trek in Katherine, where the Goods team built a bed with a local family mid-trek.
Both reflections are recorded in the Empathy Ledger. Her verdict on the beds (pending
clearance): "The reaction has been so overwhelmingly positive and for me to get that insight
is just, validated what they're doing." And the line that lands the model (pending clearance):
"It was an overwhelming, yes, we want and need these beds... And you're doing it with
community. It's not a for, it's a with, and that's really important too."

Snow has backed rheumatic heart disease work for over a decade and has funded Goods through
multiple rounds. The witness trip is part of why.

## Why it matters as a model

Funder due diligence usually reads reports the enterprise wrote. A funder standing in community,
watching a family build a bed and recording their own conclusion, produces evidence with a
different standing: it is the funder's asset, in the funder's voice, testable against the same
consent rules as every other voice in the archive. For the QBE Stage 2 lens this is Area 10
material: an investor relationship deepened by direct observation, on the record.

## The replicable pattern

1. Invite funders on Country as witnesses, not inspectors; Elders' time is a gift, credited.
2. Record their reflection into the same consented archive as community voices.
3. Label it a funder voice, always; it never substitutes for community testimony.
4. Let their with-not-for verdict travel back through their own board in their own words.

# Case study 2 — Margaret Lloyd: proof captured at the moment of delivery

> Pattern: record the before and after in the person's own words, at delivery. 292 words
> outweigh a dashboard. Quotes registry-verbatim and cleared (Ben pass 2026-07-20).

## What happened

Margaret Lloyd has lived on her homeland in the Utopia region for about thirty years. When the
Goods truck arrived she was sleeping in a tent beside a house that no longer kept the weather
out:

"I sleep in the tent but look, there's my house or the rubbish house. No mattress. Just for
chucking us blanket, that's all I was sleeping."

"Because I was sleeping on floor, turn around, turn around. No comfortable nothing."

The bed went together in front of her, no tools, about five minutes. Her verdict:

"Our bed is right. That's good. Good."

## Why it matters as a model

Impact measurement usually reconstructs change after the fact, through surveys designed far
away. This recording is 292 words long, made at the moment of delivery, held in the Empathy
Ledger under Margaret's ongoing consent. It contains a complete evidence arc: baseline (tent,
floor, no mattress), intervention (the bed, assembled with her), and outcome in the
storyteller's own verdict. Nothing was translated into a proxy metric; nothing needed to be.

## The replicable pattern

1. Capture at delivery, not months later: the before is still present and speakable.
2. Short is fine. Authenticity beats length; a verdict in the person's own words is the datum.
3. File it as the storyteller's asset with consent that can be withdrawn.
4. Pair it with the register row (asset ID, date, place) so narrative and number cross-check.

## What this is

Nic met the Brian M. Davis Charitable Foundation and wrote up the case. Their world is children and young people; their 2025-30 strategy runs on three ideas that could have been written at our shed: Good Design, Authentic Giving, Seeing the Bigger Picture. Their priority list starts with youth employment. That is the strongest single-funder fit we have seen on paper: we are not bending our story to reach them, the Maningrida trip IS their strategy with sand on it.

The doc asks the Foundation to help stand up the Aboriginal-owned enterprise sites, expand production, build the learning, and double through QBE. No amount named yet.

## What's new and useful (bring into the pitch everywhere)

1. **The youth employment engine, named properly.** The Alice facility with Oonchiumpa: 16 to 20 young people near full-time over three years, across recycling, CNC, assembly, logistics. A staged pathway: taster days, on-Country camps, supervised training, supervised paid production, ongoing paid roles carried increasingly by bed revenue.
2. **The bed-as-wages story.** Young people in Alice who assembled beds chose to be paid with a bed. That is the whole value proposition in one sentence and it belongs in the deck's voice material.
3. **The detention benchmark: CLEARED with wording.** Detention side verified: Productivity Commission RoGS (early 2026) = ~$1.3M per child per year, more than $3,600 a day, NT detains 95% First Nations kids. Our side re-derives at the locked $2M REAL figure to ~$100/day. USE: "about $100 a day, next to more than $3,600 a day for detention (Productivity Commission, 2026)". Do not print $91 or $3,852 without their exact source lines.
4. **NIAA brokerage ~$15K per young person** for gear, licences, transport, ID, bank, TFN. Removes barriers without handing cash to kids.
5. **The charity model, said better than we say it:** Butterfly transitions to Goods on Country with Aboriginal-led governance, and Aboriginal-owned social enterprises become MEMBERS of the charity. That membership idea should reach the entity-doors story.

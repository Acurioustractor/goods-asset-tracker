# 17 — The Measured Run (draft v1)

## Why we're doing this

There's one number the whole plan leans on, and we haven't earned it yet.

We say a bed costs about $426 when we press our own legs. We believe it, because the parts prices are real invoices and because the Maningrida beds, all 40 of them, came off our press at the farm. But believing isn't the same as measuring. Nobody has stood at that press for fifty beds straight with a stopwatch and kept the receipts. That's what this run is. Not a pilot, not a trial, not a proof of concept. Fifty beds, made properly, counted honestly.

When a funder asks what their $60-80K buys, this is the answer: it buys the number.

## The run

Fifty Stretch Beds, pressed and built one after another at the farm, at working pace, not craft pace. Whatever pace that turns out to be, we write it down. Start date waits on the equipment being finished; Nic sets the window.

## What we count

For every bed, and for the run as a whole:

1. **The plastic.** Kilos of HDPE per set of legs, where it came from, what it cost landed (about $2.75 a kilo today).
2. **The press.** Time per sheet at 180 degrees, sheets per set of legs, and the power bill metered per cycle.
3. **The cutting.** CNC hours per bed. Right now we think about 3.5 hours, and we think it's the biggest cost hiding in the process. The stopwatch decides.
4. **The hands.** Operator hours at the day rate, kept separate from founder time so nothing gets buried.
5. **The mistakes.** Failed sheets, cracked legs, offcuts. The model currently pretends there's no scrap. The run will tell us the truth.
6. **The rest.** Assembly and freight, already known lines, checked again.
7. **What breaks.** Any maintenance during the run, so the 3-5% reserve stops being a guess.

## What happens to the number

If it comes in at $426 or under: the label changes from modelled to measured, the model regenerates, and every page, deck and database updates from the one chain. If it comes in over: **we publish it anyway** and redo the break-even maths in the open. We told funders "$0 signed" and "zero pressed at production rate" before anyone made us. Same rule here. The honesty is the asset; the number is just the number.

## Worth deciding

Should the fifty beds be a real order for a real community, not a batch for the shed? Homeland already said any beds past the first forty are "a separate, later request". One run could be the measurement and the delivery at once: counted on the way through the press, slept on within the month.

# Area 2: The Financial Model

> Sources: `v2/src/lib/data/canon.ts`, `v2/src/lib/cost-model/engine.ts`, `v2/src/lib/data/cost-model-scenarios.ts` (+ `cost-model-scenarios.json`), `v2/src/lib/data/impact-model.ts`, `v2/src/lib/data/claims-ledger.ts`. Every figure carries the claim label recorded in code. Canon's label vocabulary is `verified / modelled / target / future / internal-only` (there is no "measured" label in the system; "verified" is the strongest tier).

## 1. The model in one paragraph

Goods sells the Stretch Bed at $750 (verified, Supabase products table). Today we buy finished recycled-plastic leg kits from Defy, so the marginal cash cost of one more bed is $685 (verified, engine-locked BOM; exactly $684.79 in the engine). The capital case is to press our own legs on Country: the same engine puts the in-house Factory path at $426 marginal (verified, engine-locked; exactly $425.74), because the finished Defy kit costs $344.05 against a ~$40 raw shred floor, an 8.6x markup, worth $194 saved per bed (verified). Beyond that sits the Community path at $421 marginal (modelled, on a $130 fair-wage assumption in a $100-160 band plus $0 free community-collected feedstock; never grouped with the engine-locked figures). Overhead is treated as an annual fixed block of ~$109,500 to fund, not a per-bed tax; at $750 price the Factory path breaks even at ~338 beds/yr (locked engine outputs, modelled on the fixed-block assumptions). Gross capital to reach the Factory state is $112,000-$222,000, or roughly $2,000-$112,000 net of the $110,046 already invested (modelled capex range). Revenue to date: the accountant-signed Goods-only carve-out is $713,827 (verified), the only figure used externally; the internal all-sources figure is $741,111 (verified, amber/internal class) plus $143,000 accounts receivable (verified).

## 3. How the engine works

- **Engine**: `cost-model/engine.ts` exports `computeModel(inputs)`, a pure function. Inputs (`CostModelInputs`) cover materials (HDPE kg and $/kg, Defy kit $344.05, steel $27, canvas $93.50, hardware $5.24), labour ($400/day operator, beds/day per path, community fair wage $130/bed), the fixed block (Kirmos $2,250/mo at 50%, founder 30 production days at $560, admin $14,700, field travel $51,000, location rent), volume (default 120 beds/yr, the honest run-rate, not 500), freight, price ($750), and capital range.
- **Defaults are locked, not typed twice**: `CostModelDefaults` in `cost-model-scenarios.ts` maps `cost-model-scenarios.json` (v5+/v6 locked 2026-05-29) plus `supplier-quotes.ts` into the input shape, reconciling to the locked numbers to the cent. `reconcileAgainstCanonicalBOM()` fires a drift warning if the JSON and supplier-quotes disagree.
- **Outputs**: four build paths (Kits / Panels / Factory / Community), each with direct, marginal (direct + long-haul freight), contribution, its own breakeven (fixed block divided by its own contribution), demoted fully-loaded, both idiot indices, and capital payback computed on the $194.05/bed legs saving only.
- **Scenarios**: five presets in `PRESETS`: Today (Buy-Kit 120/yr), In-source (~250/yr), Factory at target (500/yr), On-Country container (500/yr, containerise cuts $70/bed freight), Community-owned (1,000/yr).
- **Verified vs modelled**: a figure is "verified" when it traces to a supplier invoice or confirmed source (`VERIFIED_HINTS` lists them: Defy INV-1602/1731/1732, DNA Steel, Centre Canvas 3 invoices, Coastal Fasteners); "modelled" when it rests on an assumption (community fair wage band, commercial counterfactual $1,500-$2,000 marked "inferred, no firm quotes"). Canon money facts are `check: 'manual'`, never auto-written by loops; cost facts marked `check: 'auto'` are engine-locked. The claims ledger (`claims-ledger.ts`) is the default-deny external gate: a figure renders externally only if its canon fact is dataClass green, so amber revenue figures can never ship to the open web.
- **Surface**: the engine was lifted verbatim from the `/admin/cost-model` explorer; multiple UI skins (Mission Control / Tesla / Terminal) share it, with sliders defined in `SLIDERS`. A playable workbook is served at `/Goods-Cost-Per-Bed-SIMPLE-v1.xlsx`.

## 4. How to explain it (funder talk track)

1. **Revenue to date.** "Goods has received $713,827 (verified, accountant-signed Goods-only carve-out). That is the only external revenue figure we cite. There is a further $143,000 in live authorised receivables (verified)." Do not cite $741,111 or any surplus externally; the connected entity runs an FY26 net loss.
2. **Cost today.** "The marginal cash cost of one more bed today is $685 (verified, engine-locked, buying finished leg kits from our fabricator). Against the $750 price that is a thin but real contribution per bed."
3. **Cost in-house.** "Pressing our own legs takes marginal cost to $426 (verified, engine-locked BOM). Note: this is a locked bill-of-materials computation from verified supplier invoices, not yet a figure measured in an operating factory. If the brief's word 'measured' is used, correct it to canon's label: verified/engine-locked, factory not yet running at this state."
4. **What moves cost down.** "The finished leg kit costs $344.05 per bed; the raw shred underneath is about $40. That 8.6x markup is the whole capital case: $194 saved per bed (verified). Beyond that, the community path models $421 (modelled: fair wage $130/bed, free community-collected plastic), roughly parity with the factory but with the margin staying in community."
5. **What capital unlocks.** "Gross capital to reach the factory state is $112,000-$222,000; net of the $110,046 already invested, roughly $2,000-$112,000 (modelled). At $750 the factory path breaks even at ~338 beds/yr covering the ~$109,500 annual fixed block. FLAG: the brief names 'the QBE $400K'; the QBE figure is 'up to $400K' program capacity per `qbe-stage2-program-terms` and appears nowhere in these five files. The engine's own capital ask is the $112-222K gross range. Do not present $400K as the model's capital requirement."

## 5. Gaps / open items

- **"Measured $426" is an overstatement of the code.** Canon labels marginal-factory `verified` on an engine-locked BOM; no factory is operating at that state, so it is a verified computation, not a measured production cost. Say "verified, engine-locked" externally.
- **QBE $400K not in scope files.** No fact in canon, the engine, or scenarios carries $400K. The engine's capital range is $112-222K gross. The link between the QBE ask and this model is asserted in decks, not in code.
- **CONFLICT (framing, not value): "current cost per bed."** impact-model.ts `FINANCIAL_SUMMARY.currentCostPerUnit` = $534.79 (Buy-Kit DIRECT, labelled MODELLED) while canon's headline today-cost is $685 (marginal, labelled verified). Both derive from the same BOM ($534.79 + $150 freight = $684.79) but two surfaces headline different cuts with different claim labels; a reader comparing them sees a $150 discrepancy.
- **CONFLICT (label): the same Buy-Kit/Factory numbers are `verified` in canon.ts and "MODELLED" in impact-model.ts** (CANONICAL_FACTORY_DIRECT_COST comment, cost-per-unit metric). The files disagree about the claim tier of identical figures.
- **Revenue triangle unresolved.** $741,111 (revenue-received) vs $650,910.79 (Xero ACT-GD cut, includes PICC $436,700 which revenue-received excludes) vs $713,827 (carve-out). They `reconcilesWith` each other but no file records the reconciliation bridging them; memory notes a ~$907,569 unresolved reconciliation. All three are amber: none may carry a figure on the open web under the claims-ledger green-only rule.
- **Fixed-block coverage is assumption-heavy.** The ~$109,500 block depends on founder $560/day (locked but self-set), Kirmos 50% allocation, admin $14,700 and field travel $51,000; breakeven 338 inherits all of it. Community-path breakeven assumes the fixed block shape is unchanged on Country.
- **Modelled inputs flagged in code**: commercial counterfactual $1,500-$2,000 "inferred, no firm quotes"; community fair wage band inferred; labour hours per bed (impact-model PRODUCTION_LABOUR_STAGES) explicitly "not yet time-studied". Old fabricated per-stage dollar costs were deleted 2026-05-29; do not resurrect.
- **Fully-loaded ~$1,780 is a trap number.** Demoted in code with an explicit "must never headline an investor view again" rule; keep it that way.

## 2. CAPITAL TERMS (exact)

### The headline number and pool
- Induction "Structure" slide, **Stage 2**: "Selected enterprises can apply for funding **up to $400,000** which must be **at least matched to the external investment they have secured**. The more external investment the better! **Repayable finance will be prioritised over grant funding**."
- Induction "Funding" slide: "A unique opportunity to secure grants **up to $400,000**, drawn from a **$1 million pool**. The goal of this funding is to be catalytic..."
- Letter to Funders: "apply for grant funding of **up to $400,000, with a total pool of $1 million available.** Applications are due **September 2026**."

### Agreement clause 6 (Funding) — binding wording
- 6.1: "Social Impact Hub reserves the right to allocate funding from the **AU$1,000,000 Catalysing Impact funding pool, at its discretion.**"
- 6.2: "Individual funding grants **between $150,000 AUD and $400,000 AUD** may be awarded to Participants, on the following basis, amongst other criteria: (a) The amount of external capital secured. (b) Submission of necessary documentation verifying secured funds. (c) The Participant being selected for additional funding grants." *(Note: this sets an explicit **floor of $150,000** per grant — figure appears only in the Agreement, not in the slides.)*
- 6.3: "Participants are required to submit applications for funding as part of a **competitive selection process**."
- 6.4: "The **Steering Committee** will assess applications based on various factors including, but not limited to, the degree to which the funding will leverage further funding and generate impact."
- 6.5 / 6.6: No warranty all Participants secure funding; "Funding allocation decisions are subject to the outcome of the competitive assessment process."
- 6.9: Awarded Participants must enter a further agreement with accountability measures incl. "(a) Clear documentation demonstrating that **matched funding has been secured prior to the allocation of awarded funds.**"

### Conditional vs unconditional grants
- Induction "Funding" slide, Grant allocation: "Grants awarded at **Steering Committee's discretion**" · "**Unconditional grants require upfront evidence of co-funding**" · "**Conditional grants require additional evidence before release**."

### "Catalytic" framing
- Letter to Funders: "The funding is **not automatic or guaranteed**" and "will be awarded through a competitive process based on multiple factors, including the **catalytic impact** of the potential QBE grant funds to **unlock funding from other funders** (including yourselves)."
- Illustrative capital stack example shown in deck (WV Tech): "$1m – Debt Facility, First Australians Capital / $750K – Equity Investments / **$400K – Catalysing Impact Grant** = $2.25m". (Illustrative example only, not ACT's.)

> Net: cap **$400,000**, floor **$150,000** (Agreement only), from a shared **$1,000,000** pool across the 10-enterprise cohort. Award is **discretionary, competitive, at least matched by external commitments, repayable finance preferred over grants, NOT secured until awarded.**

## 4. ENTERPRISE AGREEMENT — obligations (template, between SIH Foundation Ltd and Participant; NSW law)

**What the Program gives (cl 2.1):** (a) a **$10,000 participation grant**; (b) induction session; (c) a diagnostic ('Impact Investment Readiness' or 'Growth and Impact')*; (d) **tailored advisory work delivered by members of SIH's Professional Impact Network (PIN)**\*; (e) one-on-one mentoring; (f) online tools/education/resources; (g) a skilled volunteering project with QBE; (h) possible hackathon\*; (i) possible live crowdfunding event\*; (j) the opportunity to apply for funding from the AU$1,000,000 pool\*. (\* = "likely only available to select Participants.")

**Fees (cl 3):** "**There is no fee charged to the Participant** for participating in the Program."

**Impact reporting (cl 4):**
- 4.1: complete a survey at the **beginning and end** of the Program.
- 4.2: provide a report to SIH's Program Manager **within six (6) months of completion**.

**Capital raising / SIH co-invest right (cl 5):**
- 5.1/5.2: SIH makes no warranty on the Participant's investment readiness and does not commit to invest.
- **5.3 (right of first participation):** "SIH or its nominee retains the right to invest on the same terms as any other lenders or investors in the Participant ... for **three (3) years its completion of the Program**." If a debt/equity round is likely, Participant must offer SIH the chance to participate on the same terms in writing; **SIH has 45 days** to indicate intent. *(This is an ongoing 3-year SIH co-investment option — flag for ACT.)*

**Program Material / IP (cl 7):**
- 7.1: "The Participant **owns the Material created by the Participant** during the Program." But grants SIH/nominee "a **perpetual, worldwide, irrevocable, royalty-free licence** to use, copy, adapt, modify and communicate non-business sensitive Material for the purpose of promoting, growing and reporting on" SIH programs.
- 7.2: Material presented stays owned by its presenter; if SIH owns it, Participant gets a royalty-free licence to use during the Program but **must not share the Materials with anyone else**.
- 7.3: "**The Social Impact Hub owns the intellectual property in Material created as part of the tailored advisory work.**" SIH grants Participant a perpetual royalty-free licence to use it for scaling their business. *(Flag: advisory-work IP defaults to SIH, not ACT.)*

**Acknowledgments (cl 8):**
- 8.1: Participant must acknowledge in **all investor materials** the Program by referencing "**'Catalysing Impact, powered by Social Impact Hub and in partnership with QBE Foundation.**'"
- 8.2: Participant agrees to the **Enterprise Participant Waiver** (Appendix 1) for hackathon/skilled volunteering — establishes that QBE/SIH/PIN involvement is **not** an advisor-client relationship, not professional advice, one-off/day-only, IP stays with each party, and the Participant **will not bring any claim against SIH or QBE**.

**Termination (cl 9):** SIH may terminate **for convenience** on **30 days' written notice** (no liability, Participant waives claims); plus termination for breach (30-day cure period). Fraud/misrepresentation (cl 6.10) lets SIH **demand repayment of funds within 30 days**, suspend, withhold, or exclude from future programs.

**Indemnity / liability (cl 10–11):** Participant indemnifies SIH (incl. full-indemnity legal costs). SIH's total liability capped at: funded Participants = total funds disbursed; **non-funded Participants = $10,000 AUD**. Participant "**will have no right to bring a claim against any QBE Insurance Group company, Affiliates or employees**" (cl 11.1.3).

**Use of information / publicity (cl 12):** SIH and QBE may publicise the Participant (description, photos, video, etc.) across QBE/SIH channels; Participant grants logo use; Participant may only use QBE logos/names with **QBE's prior written consent**.

**Other:** Dispute resolution by negotiation first (cl 13); Force Majeure (cl 13.4); clauses 5,6,7,9,10,11 survive termination (cl 14.1); NSW law; entire agreement; no variation unless executed same way.

## 5. MENTORING CHARTER (where the Matt/Mal costing-advisory work sits)

**Model:** Mentees (founders/leaders) are connected with skilled professionals from the **Professional Impact Network (PIN)** for one-on-one mentoring to "amplify their impact and achieve their goals."

- **Note on deliverables:** "While there are **no specific deliverables and no explicit outcomes to be reported**, the key objectives include: Professional Development, Knowledge Sharing, Networking, Personal Growth." *(i.e. mentoring is developmental/informal, distinct from the paid/scoped Tailored Advisory in cl 2.1(d) and 7.3.)*
- **Cadence/hours:** "a **6 session commitment expected to be completed over a 6 month period**." "Sessions are **60 minutes** in duration and are generally expected to be conducted **over video conference**" (in person welcome).
- **Roles:** Mentor (PIN Member) = Sharer / Support System / Resource / Guide / Role Model. Mentee = Investigator / Student / Proactive Learner / Goal Setter / Respectful Communicator.
- **Ground rules:** Mutual Respect, Engagement, Reciprocity, **Confidentiality** ("All discussions between mentor and mentee should remain confidential"), Commitment, Conflict Resolution (SIH can mediate).
- **Method:** encouraged to set **SMART goals** early; ongoing reflection/feedback; SIH may arrange periodic check-ins.
- **Contact:** **Jay Boolkin** (0401 389 442 / jay@socialimpacthub.org).

**PIN (from induction):** "An innovative and selective member network..." Established **2019**, **100+ vetted members**. The **Tailored Advisory** stream (the scoped costing/advisory work) draws on PIN experts focused on the highest-priority areas from the diagnostic; "What is the commitment? **Project dependent**" — and its IP defaults to SIH (Agreement cl 7.3). *(The Matt/Mal costing-advisory work most plausibly sits under Tailored Advisory and/or Mentoring — the documents do not name Matt or Mal anywhere; UNCLEAR exactly which stream and who, confirm with SIH/Jay.)*

## Quick-reference: Stage 2 application requirements (induction "Funding Application" slide)
Submission: application form; **evidence of funding commitments (legally binding — letters of commitment, loan agreements, investment agreements)**; supporting materials (pitch docs, business plans, financial models); impact demonstration (Impact Measurement Report, beneficiary demographics); corporate structure, financials (audited or accountant-endorsed), governance docs. **Pitch & Interview (1hr): 15 min pitch + 45 min interview** with Steering Committee members. **Due diligence:** SIH does standard checks and **confirms the funding commitments directly with the investors/lenders/funders.**

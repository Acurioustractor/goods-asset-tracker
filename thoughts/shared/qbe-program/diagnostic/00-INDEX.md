## About this pack

This pack is an honest operating snapshot. It is written to stand up when Ben and Nicholas are not in the room: direct about what is real today, what is being tested, and what needs deeper human review before the material is strong enough for investor, partner and community-facing use.

Goods builds desirable, functional and fit-for-purpose products for remote Indigenous communities: starting with the Stretch Bed, then washing machines, with future fridge work still at concept stage. The thread is repairable and washable technology for health, household dignity, local work and less waste.

## Review method

Work topic by topic. Each page should include the story, the proof, the media, the claim status and the human-review questions. The standard is not "key points with source links." The standard is "could someone understand the substance when Ben and Nicholas are not there to fill in the gaps?"

Topics 1, 2 and 3 now follow the rebuilt standard: visible proof in the page, real product and community evidence, media, accessible links, claim-status discipline and review questions.

A `.ts` file, Notion page, source document or transcript is not the answer. Use those sources to pull real facts, photos, stories, numbers, examples and uncertainty into the topic page. Source paths stay at the end for checking only.

Before sending materials, use `wiki/articles/program/workshop-evidence-map.md`. It maps the exact 10 workshop prompts to what we have, what is draft, what is missing, and which existing documents should go in the pre-read.

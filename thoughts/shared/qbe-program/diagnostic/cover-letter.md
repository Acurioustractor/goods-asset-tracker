# Cover Letter: Goods on Country Diagnostic Materials

**To:** SIH / PIN Diagnostic Team

**From:** Benjamin Knight, A Curious Tractor (Goods on Country)

**Re:** Goods on Country working pack for QBE Catalysing Impact

This pack is designed to support a practical working conversation and to stand up when Ben and Nicholas are not in the room. The work is strongest when it speaks plainly: what is real, what is still being built, and where sharper human review is still needed before the next external version.

This refreshed pack is more honest about stage. Goods has real products, real demand and real community relationships. It is also still early in several places: impact measurement, legal structure, governance, finance modelling, sales discipline and working capital.

A few things worth knowing up front:

- Goods builds desirable, functional and fit-for-purpose products made by, with and for remote Indigenous communities. We are starting with the Stretch Bed and washing machines because washable and repairable household technology matters for health, money and waste.

- We want resident ownership in the long run. Today, most products are still paid for by funders, councils, corporates, schools, health services or community organisations. That gap is part of the work.

- We are not trying to make Goods as big as possible centrally. The model we are exploring is lean shared services through ACT, with more production, repair, jobs and ownership moving to community partners over time.

- The impact system is not finished. Current evidence is deliveries, feedback, stories, partner check-ins and limited telemetry. QR tracking, wash frequency, repair data and longitudinal outcomes are next-stage work.

- This is still working material. The next pass should happen with Ben and Nicholas, then move into Notion as the basis for a deeper workshop.

Ben Knight

On behalf of Nicholas Marchesi OAM and the Goods team

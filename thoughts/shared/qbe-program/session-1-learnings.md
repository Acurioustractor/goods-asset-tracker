## Blended Finance Model
> "A strategy that combines the right mix of capital from multiple stakeholders who have different risk-return requirements on their capital but common impact objectives."
— The GIIN, Big Society Capital, World Bank

### Capital Stack (bottom = most senior)
1. Standard Debt (most senior, most protection)
2. Subordinated Debt
3. Common Equity
4. Junior Equity Position
5. Grants
6. Guarantees (least senior)

### 4 Types of Blended Finance
1. **Fund-level** — concessional + commercial in one fund
2. **Project-level** — partial funding/guarantees for infrastructure (MOST RELEVANT for manufacturing plant)
3. **Company-level** — credit enhancement, below-market loans
4. **Outcome-based** — corporate bonds/loans tied to SDG impact

## Investor Knockout Criteria
When searching for aligned investors, filter on:
1. **Investment Geography** — invests in places we operate (NT, remote Australia)
2. **Legal Structure** — invests in our structure (not-for-profit, hybrid)
3. **Investment Size** — generally invests the amount we're seeking
4. **Return Expectations** — aligns on financial/social return (i.e. timeframe, exit)
5. **Capital Type** — offers what we need (grant, debt, equity, recoverable grant)
6. **Size of Growth** — invests at our stage

## SEFA Loan Process
1. **Initial Phase (2-4 weeks):** Pipeline → Initial Assessment (enquiry/referral, initial conversation, mutual understanding)
2. **Analysis Phase (2-3 months):** Due Diligence & Structuring → Investment Committee (documentation review, information gathering, scenario analysis → investment proposal, investment decision)
3. **Execution Phase (2-4 weeks):** Legal Documentation → Disbursement → Ongoing Reporting (letter of offer, legal documents, facility deed, mortgage, general security deed → drawdown conditions, searches → social impact & financial, annual investment review & updates)

### SEFA Key Facts
- $9M undeployed capital ready to deploy
- 75-80 loans to date
- Only 3 out of 75 ever got into financial difficulty (2 were fraud, 1 was ignorance)
- No early repayment penalties — 40%+ of clients repaid early
- Requires independent board governance (majority independent directors)
- 1% establishment fee (only charged after approval)

## Panel: Impact Investor Tips

### Alex (QBE Ventures — CVC)
- **Check size:** $5-15M
- **Instruments:** SAFEs, convertible notes, equity, follow-ons
- **Lighthouse Program:** Early-stage discovery funding to explore value fit, ~1 year, small amount to compensate time/materials
- **3 Things to Convince an Investor:**
  1. **Why is what you're doing important?** (convince them to care)
  2. **Why are YOU the right person/org?** (secret sauce)
  3. **Is it achievable?** (do you believe you can do it?)
- **Key insight:** "Numbers going up" don't have to be money — impact going up works for CVC
- Insurance cares about RISK reduction — think about adjacencies (e.g. preventing mine explosions, disaster prevention)
- **For Goods:** Remote housing resilience = reduced insurance risk for remote assets. Think bigger about what QBE saves by resilient communities having proper beds/furniture.

### Rebecca (Beck) Parkinson
- **Top tip:** Know EXACTLY what capital you want. Be targeted. Drop mismatches fast.
- Don't be scattergun — "you only have one life"
- Research who funders have funded before. Ask who they've rejected and why.
- Key foundations doing catalytic/blended:
  - **Mindaroo Foundation** — has catalytic capital bucket (must align: oceans, modern slavery)
  - **Snow Foundation** — partners long-term, flexible capital over time, fellowship model
- A quick "no" from an investor is much more helpful than a long drawn-out process
- Understand what your funders care about — tailor pitch to each one
- Often one person represents whole org — make their job easy as your internal advocate

### Hannah (CEO, SEFA)
- **Myth buster:** Debt is not scary — "All debt does is bring cash flow from the future into the now"
- If you know an activity will generate revenue, and you know when → debt fits
- Strategic investments (new platforms, systems, strategy work) → better suited for grants/equity
- Sometimes the financial discipline of debt is actually beneficial
- Don't wait forever for grants — "grants are the most precious/scarce form of capital"
- SEFA has $9M ready to go vs grants where there's always more demand than supply
- Personalized approach — they want to understand your business, work WITH you
- **Governance requirement:** Independent board directors (majority), or at minimum advisory group

- Manufacturing plant = local jobs = community stability = lower social risk = lower insurance exposure
- Disaster resilience: flat-pack beds can be deployed rapidly after floods/cyclones
- HDPE recycling = environmental credit = ESG alignment for QBE's reporting

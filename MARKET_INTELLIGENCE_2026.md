## 4. Gaps and opportunities

1. **Federal panel access is wide open for "remote-grade beds"** — Australian Bedding Co is the only mid-sized incumbent. Goods has the differentiation story (recycled, washable, Indigenous-owned, on-country production).

2. **Aboriginal Hostels Limited bulk-replacement** — 40 hostels × ~50 beds × replacement cycle = thousands of bed slots over 5-7 years.

3. **ALPA + Outback Stores retail channel** — get Stretch Bed onto the shelves of stores already serving the same communities.

4. **Big-house construction → fit-out gap** — NT Infrastructure spends $290M building houses but who furnishes them? This is the "where do the beds go in" hand-off that Goods can plug.

5. **NACCHO single-channel deal** — one peak body endorsement reaches 144+ ACCHOs.

6. **Tim Fairfax Family Foundation** — the most precisely geographically-aligned foundation that hasn't yet been engaged.

7. **Mining royalty/community investment funds** — BHP, Rio Tinto, Glencore foundations all have remote Indigenous focus and 9-figure annual giving — currently not in the Goods pipeline.

Our beds are designed for durability in remote conditions. For the Basket Bed, wipe down the recycled plastic weave with a damp cloth regularly. The modular design means individual sections can be replaced if damaged. For the Weave Bed, the rope fibre construction is weather-resistant but should be kept dry when possible. Both beds can be hosed down for deep cleaning. Avoid using harsh chemicals that may damage the weave.
Yes! Loose weaving is usually an easy fix. For minor loosening, the weave can often be tightened by hand. For more significant damage, our team can arrange for a local artisan or repair technician to re-weave the affected section. Since our beds are handwoven by Indigenous artisans, repairs support the same communities who made your bed. Submit a support request with a photo of the damage and we'll advise on the best solution.
Yes, replacement mattresses are available for both Basket Beds and Weave Beds. Our mattresses are specifically designed to fit our bed frames and withstand remote conditions. If your mattress is worn, stained, or damaged, submit a support request and we can arrange a replacement. Mattress replacements are typically provided at no cost if within the warranty period.
First, check these common issues: 1) Make sure the power is connected and the outlet is working (try plugging in another device). 2) Check that the door/lid is fully closed - the machine won't start if it's not sealed. 3) Check if the water supply is turned on. 4) Look for any error codes on the display. If none of these help, submit a support request with details about what happens when you try to start it, and any error codes shown.
Water leaks are usually covered under warranty and should be reported immediately to prevent further damage. Common causes include loose hose connections, worn door seals, or blocked drainage. Important: If you notice a leak, turn off the water supply to the machine and unplug it before submitting a support request. Include photos of where the water is coming from if possible - this helps our technicians prepare the right parts for the repair visit.
Our Pakkimjalki Kari machines are built tough for remote communities, but regular care helps them last longer: 1) Clean the lint filter after each wash. 2) Leave the door open between washes to prevent mould. 3) Run an empty hot wash monthly to clean the drum. 4) Check hoses for cracks or wear every few months. 5) If you have bore water or hard water, use the recommended amount of detergent to prevent buildup.
Every Goods on Country item has a QR code sticker with a unique Asset ID (like GB0-152-1). For beds: Check the bed frame near the head or foot, or on the underside of the frame. For washing machines: Look on the side panel, back of the machine, or inside the door frame. If the sticker is missing or damaged, don't worry - just describe your item and when/where it was delivered in your support request, and we can look it up.
We understand that getting repairs done in remote areas takes longer than in cities. Our typical timeframes: Simple fixes (loose parts, minor adjustments): 1-2 weeks. Part replacements (new mattress, hoses, seals): 2-4 weeks. Major repairs (frame damage, motor issues): 3-6 weeks depending on parts availability and technician visits to your community. We'll keep you updated throughout the process and prioritise urgent issues like complete breakdowns.
Most repairs and replacements are provided at no cost to community members. Our beds and washing machines are supplied through community programs and include ongoing support. If your item is very old or the damage is from misuse (not normal wear and tear), we'll discuss options with you before any work is done. We always aim to keep your household goods working - that's what Goods on Country is all about.

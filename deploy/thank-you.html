Available in Single, Double, and Queen sizes with a 5 year warranty.

Pakkimjalki Kari means "Washing Machine" in Warlpiri. It's a community-tough washing machine designed for remote conditions. Specs: 8kg load capacity, uses only 50 litres per wash, runs on 240V or solar power, has 5 simple wash cycles, and is designed to be community serviceable. Comes with a 3 year warranty.
The Pakkimjalki Kari is designed to be simple. Check: 1) Power is connected (240V outlet or solar). 2) Water supply is turned on. 3) Door/lid is fully closed. 4) The machine isn't overloaded (max 8kg). If it still won't work, submit a support request - the machine is designed to be community serviceable so repairs can often be done locally.
Every Goods on Country item has a QR code sticker with a unique Asset ID (like GB0-152-1). For beds: Check the frame near the head or foot. For Pakkimjalki Kari: Check the side panel or back of the machine. If you can't find it, just tell us when and where your item was delivered and we can look it up.
Beds (5 year warranty): Frame defects, weave issues, structural problems. Pakkimjalki Kari (3 year warranty): Mechanical faults, electrical issues, pump problems. Most repairs are provided at no cost. The warranty doesn't cover damage from misuse, but we'll always discuss options with you.
The beds are handcrafted in Australia by Indigenous artisans using traditional weaving techniques. Communities like Tennant Creek, Palm Island, and Maningrida are involved in making and designing these products. The Pakkimjalki Kari washing machine is community designed specifically for remote conditions. Over 369 beds have been delivered to communities across Australia.

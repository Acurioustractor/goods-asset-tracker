The problem

A washable bed off the ground is health hardware.

In remote communities a bed you can wash, and a washing machine that survives the conditions, are the first practical links in a chain that public health already understands.

13 / 16

Why catalytic capital

Too proven for a grant. Not yet bankable for commercial money.

The gap
Real deployment proof, but commercial lenders will not yet underwrite a first-of-kind containerised plant for remote communities.
Additionality
The press is exactly what commercial money will not fund yet, so the catalytic dollar is additional by definition.
Exit of concession
Structured as a recoverable grant with a transfer trigger. The plant transfers to a community corporation at nominal value, the concession recovered from margin or written down into community ownership.
Leverage
About $4.10 of commercial capital is historically mobilised per $1 of concessional capital. External benchmark.

Transition

Endpoint
A community corporation, 51%+ owned and controlled, holds the plant, the jobs and the margin. An optional trust locks the asset to community.

This pathway is in active design with Palm Island Community Company and Oonchiumpa, not designed for them. 51% certification also unlocks the Indigenous Procurement Policy set-aside, making the community plant a preferred supplier.

Entity and ownership structure is an open decision in progress with legal counsel. Stated honestly, not as finished.

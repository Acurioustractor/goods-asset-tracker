Goods on Country

Quality beds as health hardware, made and owned On Country.
The work

The Stretch Bed is health hardware, not furniture. Recycled HDPE X-trestle legs, 2 galvanised steel poles and a heavy-duty canvas that is structural to the frame. It carries 200kg, weighs 26kg and assembles in about 5 minutes with no tools. Every bed diverts 20kg of HDPE from waste.

Beds are made on a movable On Country plant: collect plastic, shred, melt, press into components. The plant can pass to community ownership, so the value and the jobs stay local.

Why it matters: a clean, washable bed is part of the scabies to rheumatic heart disease pathway. Better sleeping conditions, alongside named clinical partners, are how that pathway could be interrupted over time. This is the reason for the work, not a claimed Goods outcome.

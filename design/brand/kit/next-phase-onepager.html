The next phase

Community ownership

The On Country plant is designed to move into community hands. The 51% First Nations ownership pathway is in legal design now.

Capacity and jobs

Communities building their own products. Real jobs, real skills, and the skills stay. First concrete step: a 50-bed production run made in-house, turning the modelled CANON:num:marginal-factory unit cost into a measured one.

Unit cost is modelled until the run is complete.

The health pathway

Beds lift people off the floor. Washable canvas cuts the scabies pathway, and scabies can lead to rheumatic heart disease. That pathway is why we do this work. We count beds, washers and communities. We do not claim health outcomes.

What it takes

AU$400,000 catalytic capital that converts into community ownership.

A recoverable grant funds the plant's move into community hands, matched by QBE Catalysing Impact up to AU$400,000 against signed external capital. The indicative stack: SEFA $300K, Snow Foundation $100K, Centrecorp $75K. This stack is pipeline, not committed capital. Every dollar is traced.
